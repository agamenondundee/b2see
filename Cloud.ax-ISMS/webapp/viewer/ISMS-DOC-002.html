4. Purpose and Scope

4.1 Purpose

This Statement of Applicability identifies the controls from ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A that have been determined to be applicable to the Cloudax Ltd Information Security Management System (ISMS), records the justification for inclusion or exclusion of each control, and references the documents and configurations that evidence implementation. It is the principal control-level reference for the ISMS, satisfies the requirement of Clause 6.1.3.d of ISO/IEC 27001:2022, and is reviewed at least annually and following any material change to the ISMS scope, risk environment, or applicable controls.

4.2 ISMS Scope

The ISMS scope encompasses the design, development, hosting, operation and support of the Cloudax Connect conversational AI platform — including inbound triage, outbound campaign and virtual receptionist services — delivered to enterprise customers from cloud-hosted infrastructure (Amazon Web Services, Microsoft Azure and Google Cloud Platform) by Cloudax personnel operating remotely from the United Kingdom.

Excluded from scope: legacy marketing-agency services (pay-per-click advertising, search engine optimisation and bespoke web development) where these are delivered separately from and do not interact with the Cloudax Connect platform.

4.3 Organisational Context

Cloudax Ltd is a United Kingdom company (Companies House registered number 14717183), with registered office at 167-169 Great Portland Street, Fifth Floor, London, W1W 5PF. The organisation operates with a fully remote workforce, no operational offices, and an entirely cloud-hosted technology estate. Personnel access information assets exclusively through company-managed devices over the public internet, secured via centralised identity, conditional access and endpoint controls.

5. Methodology

5.1 Determining Applicability

Each Annex A control was assessed for applicability using the following criteria, applied in sequence. A control is applicable where one or more of the criteria is satisfied:

a) the control treats one or more risks identified in the Risk Register (REG-RISK-001), as a treatment selected during risk treatment planning;

b) the control is required to meet a legal, statutory, regulatory or contractual obligation identified in the Compliance Obligations Register (REG-COMP-001);

c) the control reflects a generally accepted information security best practice that addresses a credible threat to the assets within the ISMS scope, even where no specific risk has yet been formally registered;

d) the control is implicitly required to give effect to other applicable controls (for example, governance controls in clause A.5.1 are required to give effect to all other controls).

A control is excluded only where none of the above criteria is satisfied, where the control addresses an asset, activity or context that does not exist within the ISMS scope, and where exclusion does not affect the organisation's ability or responsibility to provide information security.

5.2 Inheritance of Controls

Where a control's intent is satisfied through reliance on a third-party service provider (typically the cloud service providers), the control is recorded as applicable and the justification documents the reliance and the evidence basis. Reliance is supported by current independent audit reports (ISO/IEC 27001 certificates, SOC 2 Type II reports, or equivalent) maintained on file and reviewed at least annually under control 5.22.

5.3 Implementation Status Definitions

Implementation status is recorded for each applicable control using the following definitions:

Implemented — the control is in operation, evidenced by configuration or records, and integrated into routine ISMS operation.

Partially Implemented — the control is in operation but with one or more gaps in coverage, formality or evidence, identified for remediation.

Planned — the control is not yet in operation but is included in the documented implementation plan, with a target completion date and assigned owner.

Not Implemented — the control is applicable but not in operation, and not yet included in a remediation plan. (No controls currently bear this status.)

5.4 Linkage to the Risk Register

The justification for each applicable control includes the rationale derived from the risk assessment. The Risk Register (REG-RISK-001) maintains the bidirectional linkage between risks and treatments, identifying for each risk the controls applied as treatment, and for each control the risks it treats.

5.5 Review and Maintenance

This Statement of Applicability is reviewed at least annually as part of the management review cycle, and additionally upon any of the following triggers: a material change to the ISMS scope; the addition or removal of risks from the Risk Register that affect control applicability; the publication of a new version of ISO/IEC 27001 or its Annex A; the receipt of a non-conformity finding from internal or external audit affecting the SoA; or any other change determined by the Information Security Manager to warrant review.

Changes are managed under the ISMS document-control process, with all versions retained and listed in the Version History at section 2 of this document.

Annex B. Risk Register Cross-Reference

The Risk Register (REG-RISK-001) maintains the bidirectional linkage between identified risks and the Annex A controls applied as treatment. The cross-reference is held in REG-RISK-001 rather than duplicated in this Statement of Applicability, to ensure a single authoritative source of risk-to-control linkage.

The linkage is reviewed in conjunction with this Statement of Applicability under the maintenance cycle described at section 5.5 and is verified during internal audits conducted under PROG-AUDIT-001.
