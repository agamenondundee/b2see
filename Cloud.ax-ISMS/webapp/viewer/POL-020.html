1. Purpose

This Policy defines how Cloudax Ltd. monitors its information systems, networks, and services to detect anomalous behaviour, potential incidents, and policy breaches.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.8.16 Monitoring activities

2. Scope

This Policy applies to all systems in the ISMS scope, including the Cloudax platform, supporting cloud services, identity providers, endpoints, networks, and approved SaaS applications.

3. Definitions

  • Monitoring: the systematic observation and analysis of systems, networks, and behaviour for security and operational signals.
  • Use case: a defined detection scenario, including data sources, logic, severity, and response.

4. Principles

  • Risk-driven: monitoring focuses on highest-risk assets and behaviours.
  • Layered: detection covers identity, endpoint, network, application, and cloud-control plane.
  • Actionable: alerts are tuned to be high-fidelity, with documented playbooks.
  • Continuously improving: detections evolve in response to incidents and threat intelligence.
  • Privacy-aware: monitoring is proportionate, transparently communicated, and complies with the UK GDPR.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Information Security Manager: owns this Policy and the monitoring programme.
  • Chief Technology Officer: ensures monitoring data is available.
  • On-call team: triage and respond to alerts.

6. Monitoring Objectives

  • Early detection of compromise.
  • Detection of misuse of access and policy breaches.
  • Detection of operational anomalies that may indicate security issues.
  • Support for forensic investigation.
  • Evidence for compliance and assurance.

7. What is Monitored

  • Identity events (authentication, MFA, privileged access).
  • Endpoint security telemetry.
  • Cloud control plane and configuration events.
  • Network traffic at egress and at material chokepoints.
  • Application security events.
  • Source code repository and CI/CD events.
  • DLP events (POL-017).
  • Customer data access events.

8. Detection Use Cases

Documented detection use cases cover, at minimum:

  • Suspicious authentication (impossible travel, repeated failures, MFA fatigue).
  • Privilege escalation and unusual administrative activity.
  • Malware detection and EDR alerts.
  • Anomalous customer data access.
  • Public exposure of cloud resources.
  • Secrets in source code and configuration.
  • Unusual data egress.
  • Unauthorised changes to security configuration.

9. Alerting and Triage

  • Alerts are routed to the appropriate channel and severity.
  • Documented playbooks support triage and initial response.
  • Confirmed incidents are escalated to PLAN-IRP-001.

10. Threat Intelligence

  • Cloudax consumes threat intelligence relevant to its sector and technology stack.
  • Indicators are operationalised in detection rules where appropriate.

11. Customer Data Access Monitoring

  • Access to customer data — including voice recordings, transcripts, and derived data — is monitored for anomalous behaviour, including bulk download, unusual volume, and out-of-pattern access.

12. Privacy and Personnel Monitoring

  • Cloudax’s monitoring of communications and content is operated under the UK GDPR and DPA 2018.
  • Personnel are informed of monitoring through this Policy and the Acceptable Use Policy.
  • Routine monitoring focuses on system behaviour rather than the content of personal communications.
  • Targeted monitoring of an individual occurs only where authorised and proportionate (e.g. as part of an authorised investigation).

13. Out-of-Hours Coverage

  • On-call arrangements provide out-of-hours coverage proportionate to risk.
  • Tooling sends critical alerts to on-call channels.

14. Reporting and Metrics

  • Alert volumes, false-positive rates, and time-to-triage are reported to management review.

15. Review and Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually and on material change.

16. Acknowledgement

All personnel are required to acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
