1. Purpose

This Risk Treatment Plan documents how Cloudax Ltd. is treating the information security risks identified in the Risk Register (REG-RISK-001). It records the selected treatment, the controls applied, the owner, the target completion date, and the current status for each risk requiring treatment.

This Plan operates as a living document, updated as treatments are implemented, completed, or revised.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Clause 6.1.3 Information security risk treatment; Clause 8.3 Information security risk treatment

2. Scope

This Plan covers all risks recorded in REG-RISK-001 that require treatment under the Risk Assessment Methodology (ISMS-RA-001), within the scope defined in ISMS-SCOPE-001.

3. Relationship to the Risk Register and the SoA

This Plan, the Risk Register (REG-RISK-001), and the Statement of Applicability (ISMS-SOA-001) are mutually linked:

  • The Risk Register identifies risks, owners, and inherent and residual scores.
  • The Statement of Applicability records, for each Annex A control, whether it is applicable and the documents that give it effect.
  • This Plan records, for each risk requiring treatment, which controls are being applied, the target date, and the status of implementation.

4. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Chief Executive Officer: approves the Plan and accepts residual risk above the executive threshold.
  • Information Security Manager: owns the Plan; coordinates risk workshops; tracks progress; reports to management review.
  • Risk owners: drive treatment to completion, including approval of design, implementation, and verification.
  • Control implementers: deliver the controls assigned to a treatment.

5. Treatment Selection Approach

Treatment is selected by the risk owner in accordance with the Risk Assessment Methodology (Section 11). The default is to apply controls drawn from the Statement of Applicability and supporting policies. Where the SoA controls are not sufficient, additional controls are identified and recorded against the affected SoA entries.

6. Treatment Categories

Treatments are categorised for tracking purposes:

  • Implemented: controls are in place and operating; residual risk has been re-scored and accepted by the risk owner.
  • In progress: treatment is being designed or implemented against a target date.
  • Planned: treatment is approved but not yet started.
  • Accepted: residual risk is accepted by an authorised role without further treatment.
  • Avoided: the activity giving rise to the risk has been ceased or changed.
  • Shared: treatment is implemented through transfer to a third party (e.g. insurance).

7. The Risk Treatment Plan

The detail of every active and accepted risk treatment is recorded in the Risk Register (REG-RISK-001), which is the operational tool for managing this Plan day-to-day. The summary tables in this section give the high-level position. Detailed risk records, including descriptions, scoring, and implementation evidence, are in REG-RISK-001.

8. Tracking and Reporting

  • Treatment status is tracked in the Risk Register and reviewed by the Information Security Manager at least monthly.
  • Critical and High risks with active treatment are reviewed by the risk owner at least quarterly.
  • This Plan is refreshed at each management review, with a written summary of progress, slippage, and changes to the population of risks.
  • Where a target date is missed, the risk owner records the reason and proposes a revised date or revised treatment, subject to the appropriate authorisation.

9. Closure of Treatments

A treatment is closed when:

  • The control(s) are operating effectively, evidence is recorded, and residual risk has been re-scored.
  • The risk owner has formally accepted the residual risk in accordance with the Methodology.
  • The Risk Register is updated to reflect the closure.

Closed treatments remain visible in the Risk Register history and may be reopened if circumstances change.

10. Review and Maintenance

This Plan is refreshed at each management review, and republished at least annually as a controlled document. Material changes are approved by the Chief Executive Officer and recorded in Annex B.
