1. Purpose

This Record of Processing Activities (ROPA) is the documented record required by Article 30 of the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and supports A.5.34 Privacy and protection of PII.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.5.34 Privacy and protection of PII; Article 30 UK GDPR (legal obligation)

2. Scope and Legal Basis

This ROPA covers all processing of personal data by Cloudax Ltd., as both controller (Cloudax’s own processing of personal data) and processor (processing carried out on behalf of customers).

The legal basis for maintaining the ROPA is Article 30 of the UK GDPR. Maintenance of the ROPA is a statutory obligation independent of ISO/IEC 27001.

3. Definitions

Terms used have the meanings given in the UK GDPR. Particular attention to:

  • Controller: the natural or legal person who determines the purposes and means of processing.
  • Processor: the natural or legal person who processes personal data on behalf of the controller.
  • Special category data: Article 9 categories — health, biometric for identification, racial/ethnic origin, religion, etc.
  • Data subject: the identified or identifiable natural person to whom the personal data relates.

4. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Privacy Lead: owns this ROPA; reviews entries; coordinates with controllers and processors.
  • Information Security Manager: ensures security measures recorded in the ROPA reflect the ISMS controls in place.
  • Process owners: provide the operational facts for ROPA entries within their domain.

5. How to Use This Record

  • Each processing activity has a unique identifier (ROPA-NNN).
  • Article 30(1) entries (Cloudax as controller) and Article 30(2) entries (Cloudax as processor) are kept in distinct sections.
  • This document holds a representative sample showing the structure; the live ROPA is maintained as an Excel workbook with the same column structure and is the authoritative source.
  • Entries are reviewed when the underlying processing changes and at least annually.

9. Maintenance

  • This ROPA is reviewed by the Privacy Lead at least annually and on material change to processing.
  • New customers, new sub-processors, new processing activities, and changes to international transfers trigger an entry update within 30 days.
  • Significant changes are reported to management review.
