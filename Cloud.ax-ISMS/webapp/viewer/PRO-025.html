1. Purpose

This Procedure defines how Cloudax Ltd. engages with special interest groups, threat intelligence sharing communities, and other external bodies to maintain awareness of evolving threats and good practice. It implements POL-006 (where applicable) and gives effect to A.5.6 Contact with special interest groups and A.5.7 Threat intelligence.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.5.6 Contact with special interest groups; A.5.7 Threat intelligence

2. Scope

This Procedure applies to all engagement by Cloudax personnel with external special interest groups, communities, intelligence-sharing arrangements, and forums in connection with their Cloudax role.

3. Definitions

  • Special interest group: an external community focused on a particular topic relevant to information security, privacy, or technology.
  • Threat intelligence: information about threats, threat actors, and techniques used in attacks, used to inform defence.

4. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Information Security Manager: owns this Procedure; approves new memberships and engagement; coordinates threat intelligence.
  • Privacy Lead: leads engagement with privacy-focused groups.
  • CTO / Engineering Lead: leads engagement with technical communities.
  • All personnel: comply with this Procedure when participating in external groups in their Cloudax capacity.

5. Approved Categories of Engagement

The following categories are pre-approved at the level documented in REG-AUTH-001 §6:

  • Industry trade bodies (e.g. techUK).
  • Practitioner communities (BCS, OWASP, regional cyber communities).
  • Privacy professional bodies (IAPP).
  • UK national arrangements (NCSC CiSP, ICO communications).
  • Sector ISACs / ISAOs (where relevant to Cloudax’s customer base).
  • Vendor-specific user groups (where Cloudax depends on the vendor’s technology).

New categories or memberships beyond these are approved by the Information Security Manager.

6. Joining and Maintaining Memberships

  • Membership applications are made through the Information Security Manager (or Privacy Lead / CTO for their domains).
  • Where membership is paid, it is approved through Cloudax’s normal procurement.
  • Where membership requires individual representation, the representative is named and recorded in REG-AUTH-001.
  • Memberships are reviewed annually for ongoing relevance.

7. Threat Intelligence Consumption

  • Threat intelligence consumed from special interest groups is filtered for relevance to Cloudax’s technology stack and customer base.
  • Actionable intelligence is operationalised — indicators of compromise added to monitoring (POL-020), patches prioritised (POL-014), or briefings issued to personnel (PROG-TRAIN-001 just-in-time training).
  • Threat intelligence handling respects any sharing constraints (e.g. Traffic Light Protocol).

8. Confidential Information Handling

  • Personnel must not disclose Cloudax confidential information when participating in external groups.
  • Where threat intelligence sharing requires reciprocity, what may be shared is approved in advance by the Information Security Manager and is anonymised where practicable.
  • Where information is shared under TLP:RED or equivalent constraint, distribution within Cloudax is on a need-to-know basis.
  • Discussions in external groups concerning incidents — even general patterns — must be limited to what has been approved for sharing.

9. Contributing to External Groups

  • Personnel may contribute (e.g. talks, blog posts, panel participation) where this is consistent with their role.
  • Contributions concerning Cloudax content (technology, customers, incidents) require approval per the Cloudax communications process.
  • Personal contributions (independent of Cloudax) follow the Acceptable Use Policy (POL-003 §13).

10. Records

  • Memberships and primary contacts are recorded in REG-AUTH-001.
  • Material engagement (e.g. attending threat-sharing meetings) is summarised periodically in management review.

11. Maintenance

This Procedure is reviewed at least annually and on material change to engagement obligations.

12. Acknowledgement

Personnel engaging with external groups in their Cloudax capacity are required to acknowledge this Procedure.
