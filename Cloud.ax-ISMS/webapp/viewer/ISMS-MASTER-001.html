1. Purpose

This Master Document List is the controlled, authoritative inventory of every document that forms the Cloudax Information Security Management System (ISMS). It identifies, classifies, and tracks the version of each document, and records the relationship of each document to the ISMS map (ISMS-MAP-001), the Statement of Applicability (ISMS-SOA-001), and the controls and clauses of ISO/IEC 27001:2022.

It supports Clause 7.5 of ISO/IEC 27001:2022 (Documented information — control of documented information) by providing a single point of reference for the existence, currency, and version of every ISMS document.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Cl. 7.5 Documented information; supports A.5.37 Documented operating procedures

2. Scope

This list covers all documented information that forms the Cloudax ISMS, including:

  • The apex Information Security Policy (POL-001).
  • Core ISMS documents required by ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clauses 4 to 10.
  • Plans, programmes, and policies giving effect to the controls in the Statement of Applicability.
  • Procedures, registers, and governance records that operationalise the policy framework.

It does not list operational records produced by the ISMS in life (e.g. individual training records, individual incident records, individual change records). Those records are governed by POL-009 Records Retention Policy and held in the relevant operational system.

3. Definitions

  • Document: a controlled item of documented information that forms part of the ISMS, e.g. a policy, procedure, register, plan, or programme.
  • Document ID: a unique controlled identifier in the form TYPE-NUMBER (e.g. POL-001, PRO-005, REG-IAR-001).
  • Tier: the level of the document in the ISMS hierarchy as defined in ISMS-MAP-001 v1.0.
  • Version: the controlled version number of the document. Material changes increment the version.

4. How to Use This List

  • This list is the authoritative reference for whether a document exists in the Cloudax ISMS, what version it is at, and which control or clause it gives effect to.
  • When citing a document, always use the Document ID, the title, and the version (e.g. POL-002 Access Control Policy v1.0).
  • When introducing a new document, add it to this list at issue and update the SoA where applicable.
  • When a document is superseded or withdrawn, the entry is updated in this list with the closure date — entries are not deleted.
  • Auditors and certification bodies may be provided this list as the index to the ISMS evidence pack.

7. Status Conventions

  • Approved: the document is in force and is the authoritative reference.
  • Draft: the document is under preparation and not yet authoritative.
  • Superseded: the document has been replaced by a later version. The superseded version remains in this list with its closure date.
  • Withdrawn: the document is no longer applicable; the closure date is recorded.

8. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Information Security Manager: owns this list; updates entries on issue, revision, supersession, or withdrawal of any ISMS document.
  • Document owners: notify the Information Security Manager when their document is updated.
  • Chief Executive Officer: approves Tier 1 to Tier 4 documents.
  • All personnel: refer to this list as the authoritative reference for ISMS document existence and version.

12. Maintenance

  • This list is reviewed on every issue, revision, supersession, or withdrawal of any ISMS document. Updates are recorded in the version history.
  • A scheduled quarterly review confirms the list against the actual document repository.
  • An annual full review aligns the list with the ISMS map (ISMS-MAP-001) and the Statement of Applicability.
  • Material structural changes (e.g. addition of a new tier, change in numbering convention) are approved by the Chief Executive Officer.

13. Acknowledgement

This list is communicated to all personnel as the authoritative reference for ISMS documents. Personnel are required to use the controlled documents listed here — not personal copies, drafts, or superseded versions.
