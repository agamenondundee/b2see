1. Purpose

This Procedure defines how Cloudax Ltd. screens candidates and personnel before engagement, in line with applicable law and the sensitivity of the role.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.6.1 Screening

2. Scope

This Procedure applies to all candidates being considered for employment, contract engagement, or third-party access to Cloudax information assets.

3. Definitions

  • Screening: the process of verifying identity, right to work, qualifications, and background information about a candidate.
  • DBS: Disclosure and Barring Service — the UK service providing criminal records checks.

4. Principles

  • Screening is proportionate to the role: the extent of screening reflects the sensitivity of the access and information involved.
  • Screening complies with applicable law, including the Equality Act 2010, the UK GDPR, and the Rehabilitation of Offenders Act 1974.
  • Personal data collected during screening is held only as long as necessary and processed under POL-010 Privacy Policy.
  • Adverse findings are considered in context, fairly, and consistently.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • HR / People function: leads the screening process; collects and verifies evidence; maintains records.
  • Hiring manager: identifies role-specific screening requirements; receives screening confirmation.
  • Information Security Manager: defines the screening levels by role profile; reviews exceptions.
  • Chief Executive Officer: approves engagements where screening produces material adverse findings.

6. Pre-Engagement Screening

6.1 Identity Verification

  • Identity verified through valid government-issued photo ID (passport or photocard driving licence).
  • Where statute permits or requires, additional documents are accepted.
  • Verification is carried out by a person who has met the candidate (in person or via secure video call) and seen the original or a verified digital copy of the document.

6.2 Right-to-Work Verification

  • Right-to-work checks are performed in accordance with Home Office guidance, before engagement begins.
  • Evidence is recorded and retained for the duration of engagement plus two years.

6.3 Reference Checks

  • Two professional references are obtained for each candidate — typically from the most recent employers.
  • References are sought in writing or via verified channel; verbal references are followed up in writing.
  • Where references cannot be obtained for legitimate reasons (e.g. recent graduate, business closure), alternative evidence is sought.

6.4 Qualifications and Professional Membership

  • Where qualifications are stated to be required, evidence of award is verified for the highest relevant qualification.
  • Professional memberships and certifications relevant to the role are verified.

6.5 Criminal Records Checks (where required)

  • Basic DBS checks are obtained where the role profile requires (e.g. handling of customer data classified as Restricted at scale).
  • Standard or Enhanced DBS checks are not generally required at Cloudax; if a customer contract or future role requires them, this Procedure is updated.
  • DBS results are reviewed by HR with the hiring manager; adverse findings are considered with the Information Security Manager and, where material, the Chief Executive Officer.

6.6 Financial Background (privileged or financial roles)

  • For roles with significant financial authority or access to financial systems, a financial background check (e.g. CCJ check) may be performed where law permits and consent is given.
  • This is the exception, not the rule, at Cloudax’s current size and is not currently in scope for engineering or product roles.

8. Contractors and Third Parties

  • Contractors and third-party personnel are screened by their employer where contractual arrangements are in place that meet Cloudax’s standards.
  • Cloudax may verify the screening status with the supplier and require evidence.
  • Where supplier-led screening is not equivalent, Cloudax may apply additional screening as a condition of access.

9. Re-Screening on Role Change

  • Material role change — e.g. promotion to a role requiring privileged access — triggers re-screening to the new level if the prior level was lower.
  • Re-screening uses the same evidence categories as initial screening, refreshed where applicable.

10. Records and Retention

  • Screening records are held in HR systems with appropriate access controls (POL-002, POL-010).
  • Retention is per POL-009 — typically duration of engagement plus 6 years for employees and statutory minima where applicable.
  • DBS certificates are not retained beyond the period reasonably required to confirm the result.

11. Failure to Pass Screening

  • Where screening produces an adverse finding, HR and the hiring manager assess whether engagement should proceed.
  • Material findings (e.g. relevant unspent convictions, false statements on application) are reviewed by the Information Security Manager and, where material, the Chief Executive Officer.
  • Decisions are taken consistently, fairly, and in compliance with the Equality Act 2010 and the Rehabilitation of Offenders Act 1974.
  • Outcomes are recorded; reasons are communicated where appropriate.

12. Compliance Monitoring

  • Compliance with this Procedure is sampled at internal audit (PROG-AUDIT-001).
  • The auditor will typically request evidence of screening for a sample of recent joiners.

13. Maintenance

This Procedure is reviewed at least annually and on material change to applicable law or to customer contractual screening requirements.

14. Acknowledgement

HR personnel and hiring managers acknowledge this Procedure on commencement, on material change, and on request.
