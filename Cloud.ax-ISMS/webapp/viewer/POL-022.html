1. Purpose

This Policy defines how Cloudax Ltd. secures its networks, network services, and the boundaries between environments.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.8.20, A.8.21, A.8.22, A.8.23, A.8.31

2. Scope

This Policy applies to all networks operated by or on behalf of Cloudax — cloud virtual networks, VPNs, the corporate Wi-Fi at the Manchester shared workspace, and any direct network connections.

3. Definitions

  • Network: a system that allows information to be transmitted between connected devices.
  • Segmentation: the practice of dividing a network into separate parts to limit the impact of a compromise.
  • Egress: traffic leaving a network or environment.

4. Principles

  • Zero-trust posture: access to internal services is gated on authenticated identity and device posture, not network location.
  • Default deny: ingress and egress are denied unless explicitly permitted.
  • Segmentation: production, non-production, and corporate environments are segregated.
  • Encrypted in transit: traffic carrying Cloudax information is encrypted (POL-023).
  • Logged: network events relevant to security are logged (POL-019).

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Chief Technology Officer: owns network architecture and operations for the Cloudax platform.
  • Information Security Manager: owns this Policy; approves material network security changes.
  • All personnel: comply with network access requirements; report concerns.

6. Network Architecture

  • Cloudax operates a cloud-native network architecture.
  • Network designs document trust zones, ingress/egress points, and required protections.
  • Internet-facing services are protected by appropriate boundary controls.

7. Segregation of Networks

  • Production environments are segregated from corporate, development, and test environments.
  • Customer tenant boundaries are enforced through technical isolation.
  • Privileged management interfaces are accessible only via authorised paths.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.8.22 Segregation of networks

8. Security of Network Services

  • Network services (DNS, NTP, gateways, load balancers) are configured to hardening baselines.
  • Service-level capabilities (security features, rate limits, TLS configurations) are documented.
  • Changes follow POL-025 Change Management.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.8.21 Security of network services

9. Web Filtering and Egress

  • Web filtering is applied to managed endpoints to reduce exposure to malicious sites and content.
  • Egress from production environments is restricted to required destinations.
  • Egress filtering supports DLP (POL-017).

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.8.23 Web filtering

10. Wireless Networks

  • Wireless networks at Cloudax-controlled premises are secured with strong encryption (WPA2/WPA3-Enterprise where supported) and segregated from production.
  • Guest wireless is segregated from corporate.
  • In the Manchester shared workspace, Cloudax personnel use the corporate VPN over the workspace Wi-Fi where the latter is not under Cloudax control.

11. Remote Access

  • Remote access uses the approved corporate VPN or zero-trust access broker, with MFA.
  • Direct exposure of administrative interfaces to the public internet is prohibited.

12. Separation of Development, Test, and Production

  • Development, test, staging, and production environments are logically separated, with their own network controls and identities.
  • Inter-environment traffic is restricted to defined, audited paths.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.8.31 Separation of development, test and production environments

13. Boundary Protection (DDoS, WAF, Rate Limiting)

  • Customer-facing services are protected by DDoS mitigation, web application firewalls, and rate limiting where appropriate.
  • Boundary protections are tested regularly.

14. Network Logging and Monitoring

  • Network events relevant to security are logged (POL-019) and monitored (POL-020).
  • Failures of network controls are alerted.

15. Compliance Monitoring

  • Network configuration is reviewed during internal audit and as part of continuous configuration monitoring (POL-015).

16. Review and Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually and on material change.

17. Acknowledgement

All personnel involved in network operations are required to acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
