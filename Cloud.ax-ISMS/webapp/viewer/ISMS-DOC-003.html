A.5 Organisational Controls (37)
5.1Policies for information securityOrganizationalYRequired to set management direction for the ISMS and demonstrate top-management commitment, providing the framework under which all other controls operate. Cloudax processes customer voice and personal data on behalf of enterprise customers, who require evidence of formal information security governance.PlannedPOL-001 Information Security PolicyISMR-001
5.2Information security roles and responsibilitiesOrganizationalYRequired to ensure clear accountability for the protection of information assets. In a small organisation, roles may be combined but must be assigned, documented and communicated.PlannedPOL-001 §4; ISMS-RACI-001ISMR-001
5.3Segregation of dutiesOrganizationalYRequired to mitigate risk of error or misuse where individuals could perform conflicting functions affecting customer data, source code or production systems. Where headcount limits full separation, compensating controls (peer review, deployment approvals) are documented.Partially ImplementedPOL-002 Access Control Policy §3.4CTOR-002
5.4Management responsibilitiesOrganizationalYRequired to ensure all personnel apply information security in accordance with established policies and procedures, with management visibly leading by example.PlannedPOL-001 §3; MGT-MR-LOGCEOR-001
5.5Contact with authoritiesOrganizationalYRequired to enable rapid notification under UK GDPR (72-hour ICO breach notification), liaison with NCSC and law enforcement, and demonstration of regulatory engagement.PlannedPRO-024 Breach Notification Procedure; REG-AUTH-001DPOR-003
5.6Contact with special interest groupsOrganizationalYRequired to maintain awareness of evolving threats and best practice in the conversational AI and contact-centre domain, and to enable rapid information exchange during incidents affecting the wider sector.PlannedPRO-025 Threat Intelligence Procedure §2ISMR-004
5.7Threat intelligenceOrganizationalYRequired to inform risk treatment, monitoring rules and supplier oversight in a domain where threat techniques (prompt injection, voice cloning, model abuse) evolve rapidly. Threat intelligence feeds detection and response capabilities under controls 8.16, 5.22 and 5.24.PlannedPRO-025 Threat Intelligence ProcedureISMR-004, R-020
5.8Information security in project managementOrganizationalYRequired to embed information security and privacy considerations into the design of new platform features, customer integrations and infrastructure changes, ensuring controls are designed in rather than retrofitted.PlannedPRO-008 Project Security ProcedureCTOR-005
5.9Inventory of information and other associated assetsOrganizationalYRequired to provide the foundation for risk assessment, access control, retention, encryption and incident response. Cloud-native asset discovery is supplemented by a manually maintained information asset register for non-discoverable assets such as fine-tuned models and prompt libraries.Partially ImplementedREG-IAR-001 Information Asset RegisterCTOR-006
5.10Acceptable use of information and other associated assetsOrganizationalYRequired to set explicit expectations for staff handling of customer data, corporate assets and generative AI tooling, particularly relevant given staff routinely interact with consumer AI services that may exfiltrate inputs.PlannedPOL-003 Acceptable Use PolicyISMR-007
5.11Return of assetsOrganizationalYRequired to recover company-issued laptops, peripherals and credentials at termination of employment or contract, despite the fully remote operating model.Partially ImplementedPRO-005 Joiner Mover Leaver Procedure §6HRR-008
5.12Classification of informationOrganizationalYRequired to enable proportionate protection of information assets ranging from public marketing material to special-category personal data within voice recordings.PlannedPOL-004 Information Classification PolicyDPOR-009
5.13Labelling of informationOrganizationalYRequired to enable consistent handling and to support automated controls (DLP, retention, encryption decisions) that depend on classification.PlannedPOL-004 §4 Labelling StandardsCTOR-009
5.14Information transferOrganizationalYRequired to control the secure exchange of information between Cloudax, customers, sub-processors (LLM providers, telephony partners, transcription engines) and third parties.Partially ImplementedPOL-005 Information Transfer PolicyISMR-010
5.15Access controlOrganizationalYRequired to provide the policy framework under which logical and physical access controls operate, ensuring access is granted on the basis of business need and least privilege.Partially ImplementedPOL-002 Access Control PolicyISMR-011
5.16Identity managementOrganizationalYRequired because identity is the primary perimeter in a fully cloud-hosted, remote-first organisation. Centralised identity provider with single sign-on covers all production and corporate systems.ImplementedPOL-002 §2; CFG-IDP-001CTOR-011
5.17Authentication informationOrganizationalYRequired to protect credentials, secrets and API keys used to access customer data, infrastructure, source code and third-party services. Compromise of authentication information is a primary attack vector.ImplementedPOL-002 §5; PRO-017 Secrets Management ProcedureCTOR-011, R-024, R-030
5.18Access rightsOrganizationalYRequired to ensure access is granted, modified and revoked in line with the joiner-mover-leaver process, with regular review to detect privilege creep.PlannedPRO-005 §3 Access ReviewsHRR-008
5.19Information security in supplier relationshipsOrganizationalYRequired because Cloudax's threat surface is largely inherited from suppliers (hyperscalers, LLM providers, telephony, CRM); supplier security failure is a top organisational risk.PlannedPOL-006 Supplier Security PolicyISMR-012
5.20Addressing information security within supplier agreementsOrganizationalYRequired to ensure security and data protection obligations (including UK GDPR Article 28 controller-processor terms) are contractually documented with all relevant suppliers.Partially ImplementedPOL-006 §5; CON-DPA-TEMPLATELegalR-010, R-012
5.21Managing information security in the ICT supply chainOrganizationalYRequired because software supply chain compromise (malicious dependencies, container images, model weights) is a primary attack vector for AI platforms and warrants specific controls beyond general supplier management.Partially ImplementedPOL-006 §7; PRO-021CTOR-013
5.22Monitoring, review and change management of supplier servicesOrganizationalYRequired to detect security-relevant changes by suppliers (model deprecations, API changes, security incidents at sub-processors) before they affect the Cloudax service.PlannedPRO-022 Supplier Monitoring ProcedureISMR-012
5.23Information security for use of cloud servicesOrganizationalYRequired because Cloudax operates entirely on cloud services with no on-premises infrastructure, making cloud-specific controls (shared responsibility, configuration baselines, exit strategy) central to the ISMS.Partially ImplementedPOL-007 Cloud Services PolicyCTOR-014
5.24Information security incident management planning and preparationOrganizationalYRequired to enable a coordinated response to incidents, given that an incident affecting Cloudax Connect simultaneously affects all customers' contact-centre operations.Partially ImplementedPLAN-IRP-001 Incident Response PlanISMR-015
5.25Assessment and decision on information security eventsOrganizationalYRequired to triage security events from cloud-native and application monitoring sources, distinguishing routine alerts from incidents requiring response.Partially ImplementedPLAN-IRP-001 §4 TriageISMR-015
5.26Response to information security incidentsOrganizationalYRequired to contain, eradicate and recover from incidents in a documented, repeatable manner that preserves evidence and meets contractual and regulatory deadlines.PlannedPLAN-IRP-001 §5 ResponseISMR-015
5.27Learning from information security incidentsOrganizationalYRequired to feed lessons learned back into risk treatment, control design and training, and to demonstrate continual improvement of the ISMS.PlannedPLAN-IRP-001 §8 Lessons LearnedISMR-015
5.28Collection of evidenceOrganizationalYRequired to support disciplinary, contractual or legal action following incidents, with particular attention to the constraints of cloud forensics (ephemeral resources, vendor-mediated access, log retention windows).PlannedPRO-026 Forensic Readiness ProcedureISMR-015
5.29Information security during disruptionOrganizationalYRequired to ensure that information security controls (access, encryption, logging) remain effective when normal operations are disrupted, preventing degraded-mode operations from creating exposure.PlannedPLAN-BCP-001 Business Continuity PlanISMR-016
5.30ICT readiness for business continuityOrganizationalYRequired because Cloudax Connect is a 24/7 critical service for customers' contact-centre operations, with contractual recovery time and recovery point objectives that must be technically achievable and tested.Partially ImplementedPLAN-DRP-001 Disaster Recovery PlanCTOR-016
5.31Legal, statutory, regulatory and contractual requirementsOrganizationalYRequired to identify and meet obligations arising from UK GDPR, Data Protection Act 2018, PECR (call recording disclosure and outbound consent), and contractual obligations to enterprise customers.Partially ImplementedREG-COMP-001 Compliance Obligations RegisterDPOR-003
5.32Intellectual property rightsOrganizationalYRequired to protect Cloudax's proprietary models, prompts and source code, to respect third-party software licences (including open-source obligations), and to safeguard customer intellectual property within knowledge bases.Partially ImplementedPOL-008 IP and Software Licence PolicyLegalR-017
5.33Protection of recordsOrganizationalYRequired to protect call recordings, transcripts, ISMS records and statutory records throughout their lifecycle, with retention aligned to legal and contractual obligations.Partially ImplementedPOL-009 Records Retention PolicyDPOR-009
5.34Privacy and protection of PIIOrganizationalYRequired because voice recordings and transcripts routinely contain personal and special-category data, and PECR imposes specific obligations for call-recording disclosure and outbound consent that text-based platforms do not face.Partially ImplementedPOL-010 Privacy Policy; REG-ROPA-001; REG-DPIA-001DPOR-003
5.35Independent review of information securityOrganizationalYRequired to provide objective assurance over the ISMS through internal audit, penetration testing and external certification, both for management oversight and for enterprise customer due-diligence requirements.PlannedPROG-AUDIT-001 Internal Audit ProgrammeISMR-018
5.36Compliance with policies, rules and standards for information securityOrganizationalYRequired to verify ongoing adherence to ISMS policies and applicable standards, with findings driving corrective action.PlannedPROG-COMP-001 Compliance Monitoring ScheduleISMR-018
5.37Documented operating proceduresOrganizationalYRequired to ensure consistent, secure operation of the platform regardless of personnel changes, particularly important in a small team where critical operational knowledge would otherwise be concentrated in a few individuals.Partially ImplementedWIKI-OPS Operations Runbook LibraryCTOR-019

A.7 Physical Controls (14)
7.1Physical security perimetersPhysicalYCloudax operates no offices or data centres of its own. Physical perimeter security for production infrastructure is inherited from cloud service providers under the shared-responsibility model and verified via their independent audit reports. The London registered office is a virtual address with no Cloudax personnel or assets.ImplementedPOL-007 §3; SUP-AWS-SOC2; SUP-AZURE-ISO27001ISMR-022
7.2Physical entryPhysicalYCloudax has no premises with physical entry controls. Physical entry to production infrastructure is governed by cloud service providers' certified access controls, evidenced through their independent audit reports.ImplementedHyperscaler audit reportsISMR-022
7.3Securing offices, rooms and facilitiesPhysicalYCloudax operates no offices, rooms or facilities of its own. Equivalent controls for production infrastructure are inherited from cloud service providers.ImplementedPOL-007 §3 Shared ResponsibilityISMR-022
7.4Physical security monitoringPhysicalYInherited from cloud service providers' 24/7 monitored facilities. No Cloudax-operated physical environment requires monitoring beyond home-working environments addressed under control 6.7.ImplementedHyperscaler audit reportsISMR-022
7.5Protecting against physical and environmental threatsPhysicalYInherited from cloud service providers (fire suppression, flood protection, seismic resilience, power redundancy). Reasonable-care guidance is provided to personnel for home-working environments.ImplementedHyperscaler audit reportsISMR-022
7.6Working in secure areasPhysicalYCloudax operates no secure physical areas. Logical equivalents — production cloud accounts and source-code repositories — are addressed by access controls under A.5 and A.8.ImplementedPOL-007 §3; POL-002ISMR-022
7.7Clear desk and clear screenPhysicalYRequired for remote workers who handle customer voice data in shared and home environments. Auto-lock is enforced through mobile device management; behaviour expectations are documented in the Remote Working Policy.Partially ImplementedPOL-012 §4; CFG-MDM-001ISMR-021
7.8Equipment siting and protectionPhysicalYApplies to staff laptops in home environments, with guidance provided in the Remote Working Policy. Siting and protection of server equipment is inherited from cloud service providers.Partially ImplementedPOL-012 §5ISMR-021
7.9Security of assets off-premisesPhysicalYAll Cloudax laptops are off-premises by default. Full-disk encryption, mobile device management, endpoint detection and response, and conditional access are applied to all managed devices, with remote-wipe capability in case of loss or theft.ImplementedPOL-012 §3; CFG-MDM-001ISMR-021
7.10Storage mediaPhysicalYRemovable media is restricted by policy and technical control. Where used, media must be encrypted and its use logged, with cryptographic erase or destruction at end of life.Partially ImplementedPOL-013 Removable Media PolicyISMR-023
7.11Supporting utilitiesPhysicalYInherited from cloud service providers (uninterruptible power, generators, redundant connectivity). Not applicable to home-working environments.ImplementedHyperscaler audit reportsISMR-022
7.12Cabling securityPhysicalYInherited from cloud service providers. Not applicable to Cloudax operations as no corporate network infrastructure is operated.ImplementedHyperscaler audit reportsISMR-022
7.13Equipment maintenancePhysicalYLaptop maintenance is delivered through vendor support and centralised patching via mobile device management. Server equipment maintenance is inherited from cloud service providers.ImplementedPRO-028 Asset Lifecycle Procedure; CFG-MDM-001ISMR-021
7.14Secure disposal or re-use of equipmentPhysicalYCloudax laptops are securely wiped using cryptographic erase prior to re-use or disposal, with certificates of destruction retained for end-of-life devices processed by an IT asset disposal partner.Partially ImplementedPRO-028 §5; CER-DISP-LOGISMR-021

8.22Segregation of networksTechnologicalYRequired to isolate production, non-production and corporate environments from one another, with cross-environment access paths controlled and monitored.ImplementedPOL-022 §4 SegregationCTOR-029
8.23Web filteringTechnologicalYRequired to protect personnel from access to malicious web content and to enforce policy on the use of consumer AI services that may exfiltrate inputs.Partially ImplementedPOL-022 §7; CFG-WEB-001CTOR-007, R-021
8.24Use of cryptographyTechnologicalYRequired to protect customer voice data and other sensitive information in transit and at rest, with cryptographic keys managed under documented controls.ImplementedPOL-023 Cryptographic Controls PolicyCTOR-030
8.25Secure development life cycleTechnologicalYRequired because Cloudax develops the Cloudax Connect platform in-house; the secure development lifecycle integrates security into design, implementation, testing and deployment.PlannedPOL-024 Secure Development LifecycleCTOR-005
8.26Application security requirementsTechnologicalYRequired to elicit and document security requirements for each release, including AI-specific requirements addressing prompt injection, output filtering and model access controls.PlannedPOL-024 §3 Security RequirementsCTOR-004, R-005
8.27Secure system architecture and engineering principlesTechnologicalYRequired because in-house development of the platform demands consistent application of secure-by-design principles (least privilege, fail-secure, defence in depth) across architectural decisions.Partially ImplementedPOL-024 §4 Architecture PrinciplesCTOR-005
8.28Secure codingTechnologicalYRequired because customer-facing AI services are developed in-house, with secure-coding standards, static analysis and peer review applied to mitigate common vulnerability classes.Partially ImplementedPOL-024 §5 Secure Coding StandardCTOR-005, R-013
