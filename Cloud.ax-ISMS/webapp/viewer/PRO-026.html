1. Purpose

This Procedure defines how Cloudax Ltd. extracts and acts on learning from information security incidents and significant events. It implements PLAN-IRP-001 Incident Response Plan and gives effect to A.5.27 Learning from information security incidents.

Learning is the difference between an organisation that responds well once and an organisation that responds well consistently.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.5.27 Learning from information security incidents; A.5.28 Collection of evidence (records support learning)

2. Scope

This Procedure applies to:

  • All confirmed information security incidents.
  • Major events that did not become incidents but are instructive (near-misses).
  • Activations of PLAN-BCP-001 / PLAN-DRP-001.
  • Major customer assurance findings or supplier incidents that affect Cloudax.
  • BCP / DRP / incident response exercises.

3. Definitions

  • Lessons learned: the structured extraction of learning from an event, focused on what to do differently next time.
  • Blameless: a culture and approach that focuses on contributing factors and systemic improvements rather than individual blame.

4. Principles

  • Blameless. The focus is on what happened, why, and how to improve, not on assigning blame.
  • Honest. Hard truths are surfaced; reviews that produce only comfortable findings are of limited value.
  • Concrete. Outputs are specific, owned, and tracked, not vague intentions.
  • Proportionate. The depth of review fits the materiality of the event.
  • Timely. Reviews are held while detail is fresh.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Incident Manager (Information Security Manager): convenes and chairs reviews of security incidents.
  • CTO: convenes and chairs reviews of platform-only events.
  • Privacy Lead: contributes to reviews involving personal data.
  • All involved personnel: contribute openly to the review.

6. When to Hold a Lessons Learned Review

  • All incidents classified Major or Critical: a formal review is held within 10 business days of stand-down.
  • Other incidents: a brief review is held; full formal review at the discretion of the Incident Manager.
  • BCP / DRP / incident response exercises: a review is held at the conclusion.
  • Near-misses with high learning value: a review is held at the discretion of the Information Security Manager.

7. The Review

7.1 Preparation

  • Timeline of events is compiled from logs, communications, and participants.
  • Participants are identified — those involved in the response, those affected, and where appropriate, customer representatives.
  • A facilitator is identified; for major reviews this is a person who was not involved in the response.

7.2 Conduct

The review covers:

  • Timeline — what happened, when, who knew.
  • Detection — how was the event detected; could it have been detected earlier.
  • Response — what worked; what slowed the response.
  • Communication — internal, customer, regulator (where relevant).
  • Containment, eradication, recovery — effectiveness and timing.
  • Underlying causes — technical, process, human, supplier, environmental.
  • Outcomes — customer impact, regulatory impact, financial, reputational, learning.
  • What to keep, what to change.

7.3 Outputs

Outputs are:

  • A short narrative of the event.
  • A list of contributing factors, systemic and immediate.
  • A list of specific improvement actions — owner, date, link to REG-CAR-001 entry.
  • A list of things that worked well, retained for institutional memory.

8. Recording and Communication

  • The review record is held in the ISMS document repository under Incident records.
  • A short summary is prepared for sharing with personnel — anonymised where appropriate — to disseminate learning.
  • Customer-relevant findings are shared with affected customers per contract.
  • Significant events are summarised at management review.

9. Tracking Improvements to Closure

  • Each improvement action is recorded in REG-CAR-001.
  • Closure requires evidence of effectiveness, not just completion.
  • Open actions from prior reviews are revisited at each subsequent review for the same event class.

10. Confidentiality and Tone

  • Reviews are confidential. Distribution of detailed findings is limited to those who need to know.
  • Personnel speak openly without fear of disciplinary consequence for honest mistakes.
  • Where deliberate or reckless conduct is identified, it is referred separately under POL-011 Disciplinary Policy — the lessons learned review is not the disciplinary process.
  • External communications about incidents are coordinated by the Chief Executive Officer; review participants do not discuss outside Cloudax.

11. Compliance Monitoring

  • Number and quality of reviews held are reported to management review.
  • Improvement actions arising from reviews are tracked under REG-CAR-001.
  • Effectiveness of learning is reviewed by sampling: have similar events recurred.

12. Maintenance

This Procedure is reviewed at least annually and on material change.

13. Acknowledgement

All personnel involved in incident response are required to acknowledge this Procedure on commencement, on material change, and on request.
