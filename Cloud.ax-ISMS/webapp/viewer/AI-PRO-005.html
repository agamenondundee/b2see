1. Purpose

This Procedure governs changes to AI systems within the AIMS scope. It extends the existing Change Management Policy (POL-025) for AI-specific considerations — model changes, training data changes, third-party provider changes, prompt changes, evaluation criteria changes.

ISO 27001:2022 references: ISO 42001 reference: A.6.2.5 AI system deployment; A.6.2.6 AI system operation and monitoring; A.6.1.3 Processes for responsible design and development of AI systems

2. Scope

This Procedure applies to all changes affecting AI systems within the AIMS scope, whether the change is to: code, model, training data, evaluation data, third-party provider, prompts, configuration, or monitoring.

3. Definitions

  • AI change: any change to an AI system within the AIMS scope, regardless of source.
  • Material AI change: an AI change that may alter system behaviour, accuracy, fairness, or impact — triggering AISIA refresh and full V&V.

4. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Change requester: identifies the change and provides the proposed change package.
  • Chief Technology Officer: change authority for AI changes; technical owner of execution.
  • AI Governance Lead: change authority for changes that trigger material AISIA implications; signs off material AI changes.
  • Privacy Lead: contributes review for changes affecting personal data processing.
  • Engineering: implements the change and the rollback path.

6. The AI Change Lifecycle

  • Request: change requester opens the change with: description, scope, expected impact, AI system(s) affected, materiality assessment.
  • Review: change authority confirms materiality classification and required pre-change activity (Section 7).
  • Pre-change activity: V&V refresh, AISIA refresh, customer notification (if required), supplier confirmation.
  • Approval: change authority signs off.
  • Execution: change is implemented per POL-025 process and AI-PRO-001 deployment stage.
  • Post-change verification: monitoring confirms expected behaviour; staged rollout where appropriate.
  • Closure: change record is closed in the change management system; AI System Inventory updated; post-implementation review where material.

7. AI-Specific Pre-Change Requirements

Material AI changes require:

  • AISIA refresh under AI-PRO-002 (light, standard, or enhanced as appropriate).
  • Verification and Validation refresh per AI-PRO-001 §8.
  • Bias evaluation refresh where the change may affect fairness.
  • Customer-facing documentation update per AI-POL-007.
  • Customer notification per the customer DPA.
  • Risk Register update per AIMS-RA-001.

8. Material AI Changes

The following AI changes are always treated as material and follow the full pre-change requirements:

  • New AI feature in customer-facing scope.
  • Replacement of an AI model with a different model architecture or training corpus.
  • Switch of foundation model provider.
  • Material change to system prompts or guardrails.
  • Expansion of an AI feature to a new user population, geography, or use case.
  • Change in data classification of inputs or outputs (e.g. moving from non-personal to personal data).
  • Removal of, or material change to, monitoring or human oversight.

9. Foundation Model Changes

  • Foundation model providers may issue model updates with limited notice. Cloudax monitors provider notifications under AI-POL-009.
  • Where Cloudax has the option to pin a model version, default is to pin and to test the new version before adopting.
  • Where pinning is not available and the provider transitions to a new version, Cloudax assesses behaviour change and may take mitigating action (containment under AI-PLAN-IRP-001 if material adverse change).
  • Customer notification of foundation model changes follows AI-POL-009 §7.3 and the customer DPA.

10. Roll-Out and Monitoring

  • Material AI changes are rolled out in stages where feasible — internal canary, limited customer pilot, broader rollout.
  • Each stage has go / no-go criteria based on monitoring signals.
  • Rollout is paused or rolled back if monitoring indicates material adverse behaviour.

11. Roll-Back

  • Every material AI change has a tested rollback path documented before deployment.
  • Roll-back may include: reverting code, switching to the previous model version, restoring previous configuration, falling back to a baseline model.
  • Roll-back decisions are taken by the CTO or AI Incident Manager based on monitoring signals or incident reports.

12. Records

  • Each AI change is recorded in the change management system with: scope, materiality, pre-change activity completed, approver, deployment record, monitoring outcomes.
  • Material AI changes are also reflected in: AI-REG-INV-001 (system version), AI-REG-AISIA-001 (AISIA refresh), AI-REG-DATA-001 (where data changed), AI-REG-3P-001 (where provider changed).

13. Coordination with POL-025

This Procedure does not replace POL-025 Change Management Policy. POL-025 governs all platform changes; this Procedure adds AI-specific requirements for AI changes. AI changes follow both: the POL-025 process plus the AI-specific pre-change requirements in Section 7.

14. Compliance Monitoring

  • Compliance is monitored under AI-PROG-COMP-001 and audited under AI-PROG-AUDIT-001.
  • Auditors will typically sample recent AI changes and verify the AI-specific pre-change activity occurred.

15. Maintenance

This Procedure is reviewed at least annually.

16. Acknowledgement

Engineering personnel and the CTO acknowledge this Procedure on commencement, on material change, and on request.
