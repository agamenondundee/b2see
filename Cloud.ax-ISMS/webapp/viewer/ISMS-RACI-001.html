1. Purpose

This document defines roles, responsibilities, and authorities within the Cloudax Information Security Management System using a RACI convention. It supports the Information Security Policy (POL-001) and gives effect to A.5.2 Information security roles and responsibilities and Cl. 5.3 Organizational roles, responsibilities and authorities.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.5.2 Information security roles and responsibilities; A.5.4 Management responsibilities

2. Scope

This document covers the full scope of the Cloudax ISMS as defined in ISMS-SCOPE-001.

3. Definitions

  • RACI: Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed.
  • Responsible (R): the person or role who performs the work.
  • Accountable (A): the person or role ultimately answerable for the work; only one A per activity.
  • Consulted (C): two-way communication required before the activity is performed or completed.
  • Informed (I): one-way communication after the activity is performed or completed.

4. RACI Convention

  • Every activity in the matrices below has exactly one Accountable (A).
  • Multiple Responsible (R) are permitted where work is genuinely shared.
  • A role may be both R and A for the same activity.
  • Empty cells indicate the role has no assigned RACI for that activity.

5. ISMS Roles at Cloudax

The principal roles relevant to the Cloudax ISMS are summarised below. Note that several roles are combined in single individuals at Cloudax’s current size; this document records role accountabilities, not headcount.

ROLE

PRINCIPAL ACCOUNTABILITIES

Chief Executive Officer (CEO)

Ultimate accountability for the ISMS. Approves POL-001, ISMS-SOA-001, ISMS-OBJ-001, and all top-level ISMS documents. Accepts Critical residual risk.

Chief Technology Officer (CTO)

Owns the technical implementation of controls in the Cloudax platform. Owns PLAN-DRP-001, secure development (POL-024), configuration (POL-015), network (POL-022).

Information Security Manager (ISM)

Day-to-day ownership of the ISMS, the policy framework, risk management, supplier security, audit, incident response, and management review preparation.

Privacy Lead

Compliance with UK GDPR and the DPA 2018. Owns POL-010, REG-ROPA-001, REG-DPIA-001, and the Privacy aspects of incident response.

Engineering Lead(s)

Within engineering, accountability for the SDLC, code review, deployment, and runbooks. Often combined with the CTO role at Cloudax.

Customer-facing Lead(s)

Accountability for customer-facing operations, including customer notification during incidents and the handling of customer support requests involving customer data.

Line Managers

Ensure team members understand and comply with the ISMS; complete required training; report concerns; participate in JML processes.

All personnel

Comply with policies; complete training; report events and weaknesses; safeguard credentials and customer data.

Internal Auditor (PROG-AUDIT-001)

Independent audit of the ISMS in accordance with the audit programme. Where in-house resource is insufficient, an external party may be engaged.

6. Working with a Small Team

Cloudax operates with a small team where ISMS roles are typically combined in single individuals. To preserve effective separation:

  • The CEO retains ultimate accountability for the ISMS but does not hold day-to-day operational ISMS responsibility.
  • The Information Security Manager and the Chief Technology Officer are distinct roles, even where one person performs both functions, and acts in the relevant capacity for each activity.
  • Where two-person review is required (e.g. code review, change approval, access grants), it is performed by a person other than the originator regardless of which roles each holds.
  • Where independence is required (e.g. internal audit), an external party is engaged where in-house separation is not feasible.

7. Segregation of Duties

Segregation of duties is operated under POL-002 Access Control Policy §18. Examples include:

  • Code review by a person other than the author before merge to protected branches.
  • Production deployment authorisation separate from code authorship.
  • Access reviews carried out by a person other than the access grantor.
  • Disciplinary investigation by an investigator other than the line manager where conflict of interest exists.
