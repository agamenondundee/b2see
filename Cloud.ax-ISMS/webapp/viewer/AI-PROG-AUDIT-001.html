1. Purpose

This Programme defines how Cloudax Ltd. plans, conducts, and manages internal audits of the AI Management System (AIMS) in accordance with Clause 9.2 of ISO/IEC 42001:2023.

ISO 27001:2022 references: ISO 42001 reference: Clause 9.2 Internal audit

2. Scope

This Programme covers internal audit of the full AIMS scope as defined in AIMS-SCOPE-001.

3. Definitions

  • Internal audit: a systematic, independent, and documented examination to determine the extent to which AIMS audit criteria are fulfilled.
  • Audit criteria: the policies, procedures, requirements, and controls against which the audit is conducted.
  • Auditor: the person performing the audit, independent of the activity being audited.

4. Principles

  • Independence: auditors are independent of the activity they audit.
  • Risk-based: audit depth and frequency are calibrated to risk.
  • Evidence-based: findings are supported by objective evidence.
  • Constructive: audits identify both areas for improvement and what is working well.
  • Confidential: audit work is treated with appropriate confidentiality.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • AI Governance Lead: owns this Programme; commissions audits; tracks findings to closure; reports to management review.
  • Internal auditor: conducts the audit independently; produces the audit report; explains findings to auditees.
  • Auditees: cooperate with the audit; provide evidence; agree corrective action where applicable.
  • Chief Executive Officer: receives audit reports; approves the audit programme.

6. Audit Independence

  • Audits are conducted by a person who is not responsible for the activity being audited.
  • Where in-house independence is not feasible (Cloudax is a small team), an external auditor is engaged.
  • Auditor selection considers competence (Section 13) and any conflict of interest.
  • The AI Governance Lead may not audit activities for which they are accountable; where the entire AIMS is in scope, an external auditor is engaged.

7. The Audit Cycle

  • The full AIMS is audited within each three-year cycle, with at least one audit per year.
  • The annual audit plan ensures all clauses (4 to 10) and all applicable Annex A controls are covered within the cycle.
  • Higher-risk areas are audited more frequently — typically annually.
  • The plan is approved by the Chief Executive Officer at the start of the cycle and reviewed annually.

8. Audit Planning

Each audit is planned in advance:

  • Scope: which clauses, controls, processes, AI systems, or sites.
  • Criteria: the policies, procedures, and standards against which conformity is assessed.
  • Method: document review, interviews, observation, sample testing.
  • Schedule: dates, auditees, expected duration.
  • Reporting: format, distribution, expected timeline.

9. Audit Conduct

  • Opening meeting confirms scope, criteria, schedule, and expectations.
  • Audit work covers documentation, interviews, observation, and sample testing.
  • Findings are discussed with auditees as they emerge — no surprises in the report.
  • Closing meeting summarises findings, agrees provisional classification, and confirms next steps.

10. Audit Reporting

  • A written audit report is issued within 10 business days of the audit closing meeting.
  • The report includes: scope, criteria, dates, auditor, methodology, findings (classified), positive observations, and recommended actions.
  • The report is distributed to the AI Governance Lead, the Chief Executive Officer, and audited process owners.

12. Tracking Findings to Closure

  • Findings are recorded in REG-CAR-001 (the corrective actions register, shared with the ISMS).
  • Each finding has a named owner and target closure date.
  • Closure requires evidence of effectiveness, verified by the AI Governance Lead or the original auditor.
  • Open and overdue findings are reported to AIMS management review.

13. Auditor Competence

  • Auditors must have demonstrable competence in: ISO/IEC 42001:2023 (or equivalent training), AI risk and governance, audit methodology, and the Cloudax AIMS context.
  • Where an external auditor is engaged, competence is verified through certification (e.g. PECB ISO 42001 Lead Auditor) and references.
  • Auditor competence is reviewed annually.

14. AI-Specific Audit Considerations

Auditing an AIMS differs from auditing an ISMS. AI audits include:

  • Sampling AI systems against the AIMS-SOA-001 to confirm controls are operating as documented.
  • Sampling AISIA records (AI-REG-AISIA-001) to confirm impact assessments have occurred and outputs have been actioned.
  • Sampling production AI monitoring evidence (drift, accuracy, fairness indicators) to confirm monitoring is operating.
  • Sampling third-party AI provider reviews to confirm assurance is current.
  • Sampling AI training records to confirm personnel competence.
  • Sampling AI incidents to confirm response and lessons-learned have run.

15. Coordination with the ISMS Audit Programme

  • AIMS and ISMS audits may be conducted concurrently for efficiency, particularly where they share infrastructure.
  • Findings are recorded in the same REG-CAR-001 register, classified by source (AIMS or ISMS) but managed under one corrective action discipline.
  • Audit reports clearly identify whether each finding relates to ISO/IEC 27001:2022, ISO/IEC 42001:2023, or both.

16. Records

  • Audit plans, reports, and findings are retained per POL-009 — typically 6 years.
  • Records are made available to external auditors and certification bodies on request.

17. Maintenance

This Programme is reviewed at least annually.

18. Acknowledgement

Personnel involved in audit (AI Governance Lead, internal auditor, Chief Executive Officer) acknowledge this Programme on commencement, on material change, and on request.
