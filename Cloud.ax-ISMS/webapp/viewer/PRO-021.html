1. Purpose

This Procedure defines how Cloudax Ltd. onboards new suppliers, contractors, and partners that have access to Cloudax information or that provide services that may affect Cloudax’s information security. It implements POL-006 Supplier Security Policy.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.5.21 Managing information security in the ICT supply chain

2. Scope

This Procedure applies to all new supplier engagements that fall within the supplier security scope as defined in POL-006.

3. Definitions

  • Supplier: any third party providing goods or services to Cloudax under a contractual arrangement.
  • Tier: the categorisation of a supplier per POL-006 §6.

4. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Supplier owner: the named individual who initiates and stewards the engagement.
  • Information Security Manager: approves Tier 1 and Tier 2 engagements; reviews assurance evidence.
  • Privacy Lead: approves engagements involving personal data; reviews DPA.
  • Chief Technology Officer: approves cloud service providers and platform suppliers.
  • Finance / Legal: support contracting.

5. The Onboarding Process

5.1 Stage 1 — Need Assessment and Classification

  • Identify the business need; consider whether existing suppliers can meet it.
  • Identify the categories and classification of Cloudax information involved (POL-004).
  • Identify whether personal data is involved and Cloudax’s role (controller / processor / joint).

5.2 Stage 2 — Tier Assignment

  • Assign tier per POL-006 §6 based on data sensitivity and business criticality.
  • Tier drives the depth of assurance required at Stage 3.

5.3 Stage 3 — Security Assurance Evidence

Evidence required by tier:

  • Tier 1 (Critical): ISO 27001 certificate (Statement of Applicability if requested), SOC 2 report (most recent), recent independent penetration test summary, incident history, BCP/DRP attestation.
  • Tier 2 (Important): ISO 27001 certificate or equivalent assurance report, supplier security questionnaire.
  • Tier 3 (Standard): supplier security questionnaire, evidence of security programme.
  • Tier 4 (Minimal): light-touch confirmation of confidentiality and security commitments.

Evidence is reviewed for currency, scope, and material findings.

5.4 Stage 4 — Risk Assessment

  • Identify residual risks not addressed by the supplier’s controls.
  • Identify dependencies (e.g. on the supplier’s sub-processors, geographic concentration, key personnel).
  • Record material risks in the Risk Register (REG-RISK-001) with appropriate treatment.

5.5 Stage 5 — Contractual

  • Use Cloudax’s standard supplier security clauses (POL-006 §8).
  • Where the supplier processes personal data, an Article 28 UK GDPR-compliant DPA is required.
  • Where the supplier is critical or processes Restricted information, additional clauses (audit rights, escrow, exit) are considered.
  • Contracts are signed by appropriately authorised individuals.

5.6 Stage 6 — Provisioning

  • Access for supplier personnel is granted only after onboarding is complete and contracts are signed.
  • Access follows POL-002 — named individuals, MFA, time-bound where appropriate, logged.
  • Cloudax-specific onboarding (security awareness, contact list, escalation paths) is delivered to supplier personnel where appropriate.

5.7 Stage 7 — Recording

  • The supplier is recorded in the Cloudax Supplier Inventory with: name, owner, tier, service description, key contacts, contract references, evidence references, sub-processors, hosting locations, review date.
  • Where personal data is involved, the supplier is recorded as a sub-processor in REG-ROPA-001 if appropriate, and customers are notified per the relevant DPA.

6. Tier 1 Critical Suppliers

Additional considerations apply to Tier 1 engagements:

  • Concentration risk — avoiding excessive dependency on a single provider.
  • Exit and portability — documented in advance.
  • Incident coordination — named contacts and 24x7 paths.
  • Continuity arrangements — alignment with PLAN-BCP-001 and PLAN-DRP-001.
  • Approval by the Chief Executive Officer in addition to the Information Security Manager.

7. Personal Data Processors

  • Article 28 UK GDPR DPA is mandatory.
  • Sub-processors are identified and approved by the Privacy Lead.
  • International transfer safeguards are documented (IDTA / SCCs / adequacy).
  • Where Cloudax customers must consent or be notified, this is coordinated by the Privacy Lead.

8. AI and Machine Learning Suppliers

  • Specific attention is given to: data submitted to the AI service; whether customer data may be used for training; hosting region; model behaviours and limitations.
  • AI suppliers are recorded in the AI Use Register and reflected in customer-facing documentation.
  • Customer DPAs are reviewed to confirm any AI-supplier sub-processing is compliant.

9. Documenting the Decision

  • Each onboarding produces a written record of the assessment, including evidence reviewed, residual risks identified, mitigations agreed, and approver(s).
  • The record is held with the Supplier Inventory.

10. Records and Retention

  • Onboarding records are retained for the duration of the engagement and per POL-009 thereafter.
  • Contracts are retained per POL-009.

11. Compliance Monitoring

  • Onboarding compliance is sampled at internal audit (PROG-AUDIT-001).
  • Findings are recorded in REG-CAR-001.

12. Maintenance

This Procedure is reviewed at least annually and on material change to supplier requirements.

13. Acknowledgement

All personnel involved in supplier engagements are required to acknowledge this Procedure on commencement, on material change, and on request.
