1. Purpose

This Policy governs Cloudax’s relationships with third parties in the context of AI — third-party AI providers, customers using Cloudax AI capabilities, and partners involved in AI activity. It implements ISO/IEC 42001:2023 Annex A.10.

ISO 27001:2022 references: ISO 42001 reference: A.10.2 Allocating responsibilities; A.10.3 Suppliers; A.10.4 Customers

2. Scope

This Policy applies to:

  • Third-party AI providers that Cloudax consumes capabilities from (LLMs, ASR/TTS, foundation models, embedding services, AI tooling).
  • Cloudax customers who consume the Cloudax platform with its AI capabilities.
  • Partners involved in AI development, integration, or operation.

It operates alongside the existing Supplier Security Policy (POL-006) and procedures (PRO-021, PRO-022); this Policy adds AI-specific requirements.

3. Definitions

  • Third-party AI provider: an external party providing AI capabilities consumed by Cloudax — e.g. an LLM API, an ASR service, a foundation model.
  • AI sub-processor: a third-party AI provider that processes customer personal data on Cloudax’s behalf as part of the platform.
  • Allocation of responsibilities: the documented division of accountability between Cloudax, customers, and providers in respect of AI outcomes.

4. Principles

  • Cloudax remains accountable to its customers and end-users for the AI capabilities it provides, regardless of the upstream providers it relies on.
  • Allocation of AI responsibilities is documented — between Cloudax and providers, and between Cloudax and customers.
  • Third-party AI providers are assessed before onboarding and monitored in life.
  • Concentration risk is managed — reliance on a single provider for a critical capability is a recognised risk.
  • Customer transparency about third-party AI providers is provided through the customer DPA and ongoing communication.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • AI Governance Lead: owns this Policy; coordinates third-party AI provider assessment and ongoing review.
  • Chief Technology Officer: contributes technical assessment of providers; owns the technical integration.
  • Privacy Lead: owns sub-processor disclosure to customers and DPA arrangements.
  • Customer-facing leads: own customer-side communication of third-party AI provider arrangements.
  • Information Security Manager: contributes security assessment via the existing supplier process (POL-006).

7. Third-Party AI Suppliers

7.1 Onboarding Assessment

Each third-party AI provider is assessed before onboarding. Assessment covers, in addition to standard supplier assurance (PRO-021):

  • Data handling commitments — data submitted to the provider is or is not used for training; retention windows; deletion.
  • Model behaviour assurance — provider-published evaluations, model cards, system cards, safety evaluations.
  • Hosting region and jurisdiction — alignment with customer data residency commitments.
  • Security posture — standard supplier assurance plus AI-specific concerns (e.g. multi-tenancy isolation, prompt injection resistance, output filter availability).
  • Operational track record — service availability, behaviour change history, incident handling.
  • Exit feasibility — ability to migrate to an alternative provider, time and effort required.

7.2 Contractual Requirements

  • Each provider contract addresses: data handling, sub-processing, security, incident notification, change notification, exit, audit / assurance rights, applicable law.
  • Where the provider processes customer personal data, an Article 28 UK GDPR-compliant DPA is in place.
  • Where the provider may handle customer voice or transcript data, the contract reflects Cloudax’s commitment that this data is not used for upstream training without customer permission.
  • International transfer safeguards (IDTA / SCCs / adequacy) are documented for any cross-border data flow.

7.3 Sub-Processor Disclosure

  • Cloudax customers are notified of material AI sub-processors per the customer DPA.
  • New AI sub-processors are notified to customers within the timeframe specified in the DPA.
  • Customer objection process is operated per the DPA.
  • The Third-Party AI Provider Register (AI-REG-3P-001) records the canonical list of AI sub-processors.

7.4 In-Life Monitoring

  • Tier 1 AI providers (those whose behaviour materially affects Cloudax service or customer outcomes) are monitored continuously for status, behaviour change, and incident notifications.
  • Periodic review per PRO-022 is extended for AI providers to cover: data handling commitment refresh, model behaviour change, evaluation refresh, sub-processor changes.
  • Provider behaviour changes that affect production AI systems trigger AI change management (AI-PRO-005) and may trigger AISIA refresh.

7.5 Exit and Continuity

  • Tier 1 AI providers have a documented exit plan covering: notification timeline, data return / destruction, migration to alternative provider, customer communication.
  • Where feasible, the Cloudax architecture supports provider abstraction so that an alternative provider can be substituted with reasonable effort.
  • Provider failure or unilateral capability withdrawal is a recognised AI risk in AI-REG-RISK-001.

8. Customer-Facing AI Responsibilities

  • Customer responsibilities for the use of Cloudax AI capabilities are documented in the customer contract and customer-facing documentation.
  • Customer obligations include: configuring the platform within its intended use; providing required information to end-users where regulation requires; cooperating in incident response; raising AI concerns through the agreed channels.
  • Cloudax supports customers in meeting their own AI obligations to their regulators where the support is feasible and appropriate.

9. Foundation Model Providers

  • Foundation model providers (e.g. providers of large s or large speech models) receive enhanced attention because of their material role in Cloudax’s AI behaviour.
  • Onboarding assessment includes review of: model card, system card, safety evaluations, alignment commitments, abuse policies.
  • In-life monitoring includes tracking: model version changes; behaviour change announcements; safety policy changes; capability additions or withdrawals.
  • Migration between foundation model providers is recognised as a substantial change requiring AISIA refresh, evaluation, and customer notification.

10. Concentration Risk

  • Cloudax avoids dependence on a single AI provider for capabilities critical to customer service unless there is documented justification.
  • Where dependence on a single provider is unavoidable, mitigations include: contractual continuity terms, provider behaviour monitoring, technical abstraction, and a documented contingency plan.
  • Concentration risk is reviewed annually as part of supplier assurance.

11. Records

  • AI-REG-3P-001 holds the master Third-Party AI Provider Register.
  • Provider assessment records, contracts, and review records are held with the supplier entry.
  • Customer-facing AI sub-processor disclosures are held with customer DPA records.
  • Records are retained per POL-009.

12. Compliance Monitoring

  • Compliance is monitored under AI-PROG-COMP-001 and audited under AI-PROG-AUDIT-001.
  • Auditors will typically sample Tier 1 AI providers and request the corresponding assessment, contract, and review evidence.

13. Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually and on material change to the AI provider landscape or customer obligations.

14. Acknowledgement

Personnel involved in AI provider engagement, customer engagement, and customer DPA management acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
