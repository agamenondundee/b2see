1. Purpose

This Compliance Monitoring Schedule defines how Cloudax Ltd. monitors compliance with its information security policies, procedures, controls, and applicable legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.5.36 Compliance with policies, rules and standards

2. Scope

This Schedule applies to all systems, processes, and personnel within the Cloudax ISMS scope (ISMS-SCOPE-001).

3. Definitions

  • Compliance: the state of conforming to applicable obligations.
  • Monitoring: the systematic examination of activities to assess conformity.
  • Finding: an observation arising from monitoring that requires recording, classification, and where appropriate corrective action.

4. Principles

  • Risk-based: monitoring focuses on the highest-risk areas and on obligations with the most material consequences.
  • Continuous where possible: automated, continuous monitoring supplements scheduled review.
  • Proportionate: monitoring is calibrated to Cloudax’s size and the small team that operates it.
  • Independent where it matters: high-stakes monitoring is performed by someone other than the activity owner.
  • Action-oriented: findings drive corrective action, not just reporting.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Information Security Manager: owns this Schedule; coordinates monitoring; consolidates findings; reports to management review.
  • Privacy Lead: owns monitoring of personal data obligations under UK GDPR / DPA 2018.
  • Chief Technology Officer: owns monitoring of technical control effectiveness.
  • Process owners: cooperate with monitoring of processes within their domain.
  • Internal auditors (PROG-AUDIT-001): conduct independent compliance audits as part of the audit programme.

6. Compliance Domains Covered

  • ISO/IEC 27001:2022 conformance, including the controls in ISMS-SOA-001 v4.0.
  • UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018.
  • PECR, Computer Misuse Act, IP and software licence law.
  • Customer contractual security and privacy obligations.
  • Cloudax internal policies and procedures.

7. Monitoring Methods

  • Automated technical monitoring: alerts, configuration scans, vulnerability scans, DLP events, log review (POL-019, POL-020).
  • Sample-based review: sampled access reviews, change reviews, training records.
  • Walkthrough: guided demonstration that a procedure operates as documented.
  • Internal audit: structured assessment under PROG-AUDIT-001.
  • Management review: regular review of ISMS performance against objectives, risk, and incidents.

9. Recording Findings

  • Findings are recorded with date, source, description, classification, owner, and target closure date.
  • Findings are entered in REG-CAR-001 Corrective Actions Register where corrective action is required.
  • Where the finding indicates a material risk, the Risk Register (REG-RISK-001) is updated.
  • Where the finding indicates a personal data breach, PRO-024 Breach Notification Procedure is invoked.

10. Escalation and Corrective Action

  • Findings classified as material are escalated to the Information Security Manager within one business day.
  • Critical findings (potential breach, active exploitation, regulator interest) are escalated to the Chief Executive Officer immediately.
  • Corrective actions are tracked to closure under Cl. 10.1 / Cl. 10.2 in REG-CAR-001.

11. Reporting

  • A consolidated compliance summary is reported to management review at least every six months.
  • Significant findings are reported promptly between cycles.
  • Trends are tracked over time as a leading indicator of ISMS health.

12. Maintenance

This Schedule is reviewed at least annually and on material change to obligations or to the Cloudax operating model. Material changes are approved by the Chief Executive Officer and recorded in Annex B.

13. Acknowledgement

All personnel involved in compliance monitoring are required to acknowledge this Schedule on commencement, on material change, and on request.
