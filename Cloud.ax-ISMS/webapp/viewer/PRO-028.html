1. Purpose

This Procedure defines how Cloudax Ltd. disposes of equipment containing Cloudax information so that the information is irrecoverable. It implements POL-013 Removable Media Policy and supports POL-009 Records Retention.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.7.13 Equipment maintenance (where disposal follows from maintenance); A.7.14 Secure disposal or re-use of equipment

2. Scope

This Procedure applies to all equipment owned by, leased by, or under the control of Cloudax that may contain Cloudax information — laptops, mobile devices, removable media, peripherals, and (where applicable) any on-premises servers or storage.

3. Definitions

  • Sanitisation: the process of removing data from media so it cannot be retrieved.
  • Cryptographic erasure: rendering encrypted data inaccessible by destroying the encryption key.
  • Certificate of destruction: a written attestation by a disposal provider that the equipment has been securely destroyed.

4. Principles

  • No equipment leaves Cloudax control with recoverable Cloudax information.
  • The disposal method is appropriate to the equipment and its content.
  • Disposal is recorded; certificates are obtained where applicable.
  • Re-use is preferred to destruction where it can be done safely and is environmentally responsible.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • IT support: performs disposal of endpoints and removable media.
  • CTO: authorises disposal of platform equipment (where any).
  • Information Security Manager: owns this Procedure; spot-checks compliance; approves third-party disposal providers.

6. Triggers for Disposal

  • End of life of equipment.
  • End of lease.
  • Equipment failure beyond economic repair.
  • Loss of suitability (e.g. supplier no longer trusted, performance unfit).
  • Reissue between personnel — sanitisation between users.

7. Sanitisation of Storage

Approved sanitisation methods, in order of preference where applicable:

  • Cryptographic erasure where the device was encrypted with a managed key (preferred for SSDs and most modern endpoints).
  • Secure overwrite using approved tools (suitable for spinning media; SSDs accept single-pass overwrite).
  • Physical destruction (suitable where data is highly sensitive, where overwrite is impractical, or where contractual requirements demand).

8. Disposal Methods

8.1 In-House Sanitisation and Reissue

  • Equipment to be reissued internally is sanitised using cryptographic erasure or secure overwrite.
  • After sanitisation, the device is rebuilt from the standard image (POL-015).
  • IT support records the sanitisation.

8.2 Manufacturer Take-Back / Trade-In

  • Where the manufacturer offers take-back or trade-in, equipment is sanitised by Cloudax before dispatch.
  • Where the manufacturer’s programme requires the original disk, the disk is replaced with a sanitised disk where practicable.

8.3 Approved Third-Party Disposal

  • Approved third-party disposal providers are recorded in the Cloudax Supplier Inventory (POL-006).
  • Providers must be ADISA-certified or equivalent.
  • Equipment is sanitised by Cloudax before handover where reasonable.
  • Certificates of destruction are obtained for each disposal batch and held against this Procedure.

8.4 Physical Destruction

  • Where physical destruction is required, the device is shredded, disintegrated, or otherwise rendered unrecoverable, by an approved provider.
  • Witnessed destruction may be required for highly sensitive disposals.
  • Certificates of destruction are obtained.

9. Special Cases

9.1 End-of-Life Encrypted Devices

  • Devices encrypted with full-disk encryption with managed keys are sanitised by destroying the encryption key (cryptographic erasure).
  • Where the key cannot be reliably destroyed, the device is overwritten or physically destroyed.

9.2 Storage Media in Decommissioned Cloud Resources

  • Cloudax does not directly destroy provider-managed storage media.
  • Cloudax relies on the cloud provider’s media handling controls, validated through their certifications and assurance reports (POL-006, POL-007).
  • Logical deletion is performed under PRO-017.

9.3 Devices Beyond Re-Use

  • Failed equipment is disposed of via approved third-party disposal with physical destruction where the device cannot be sanitised.

10. Disposal of Paper Records

  • Paper records are disposed of through the secure shredding facility at the Manchester shared workspace.
  • Highly sensitive paper records are shredded immediately after use rather than held in waste bins.

11. Records and Certificates

  • Each disposal is recorded with: device asset tag, type, owner, sanitisation method, date, person performing, and (where third-party) certificate of destruction reference.
  • Records are retained per POL-009 — typically 6 years from disposal.

12. Environmental Considerations

  • Disposal is performed via WEEE-compliant providers.
  • Reuse and refurbishment are preferred where compatible with sanitisation requirements.

13. Compliance Monitoring

  • Disposal records are reviewed at internal audit (PROG-AUDIT-001).
  • Findings are recorded in REG-CAR-001.

14. Maintenance

This Procedure is reviewed at least annually and on material change to disposal providers or methods.

15. Acknowledgement

Personnel involved in disposal (IT support, CTO, ISM) are required to acknowledge this Procedure on commencement, on material change, and on request.
