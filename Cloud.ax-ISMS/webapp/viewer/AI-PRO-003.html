1. Purpose

This Procedure operationalises the Data for AI Systems Policy (AI-POL-006). It defines how Cloudax acquires, prepares, uses, stores, and disposes of data for AI systems.

ISO 27001:2022 references: ISO 42001 reference: A.7.2 Data for development and enhancement of AI systems; A.7.3 Acquisition of data; A.7.4 Quality of data for AI systems; A.7.5 Data provenance; A.7.6 Data preparation

2. Scope

This Procedure applies to all data used in AI systems within the AIMS scope, including training, evaluation, fine-tuning, reference, embedding, and prompt data.

3. Definitions

Definitions are per AI-POL-006 §3.

4. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Chief Technology Officer: owns this Procedure; accountable for AI data discipline.
  • AI Governance Lead: reviews material data decisions; escalates concerns.
  • Privacy Lead: accountable for personal data aspects.
  • AI Builders: maintain dataset records; apply this Procedure in their work.

5. Process Overview

Data flows through five operational stages: Acquisition → Quality Assessment → Provenance Tracking → Preparation → Use. At each stage, records are added to AI-REG-DATA-001.

6. Acquisition

  • Data sources are documented before acquisition: source identity, lawful basis, classification, intended AI use, retention plan.
  • Customer data is acquired through the platform under the customer DPA.
  • Public datasets are acquired with attention to licence terms and source reliability; the licence is recorded.
  • Commercial data is acquired under contract with supplier assurance per POL-006.
  • Personnel-generated data (e.g. internal evaluation labelling) is treated as Cloudax confidential.
  • Acquisition decisions for novel sources are reviewed by the AI Governance Lead.

7. Quality Assessment

Quality is assessed against the dimensions in AI-POL-006 §9: accuracy, completeness, consistency, timeliness, representativeness, validity. Practical steps:

  • Sample a representative subset; spot-check against ground truth where available.
  • Calculate distribution statistics for key features (categorical, numerical, temporal).
  • Identify representativeness gaps relative to the deployment context (e.g. accent coverage, demographic distribution).
  • Identify likely sources of bias in the data.
  • Document quality assessment outputs in the dataset record (AI-REG-DATA-001).
  • Quality issues identified are addressed (additional acquisition, augmentation, exclusion) before use.

8. Provenance Tracking

  • Each dataset has a provenance record: source(s), acquisition date, acquisition method, contract or licence reference, transformations applied, version of preparation pipeline used.
  • Provenance is traceable from source to the AI system that consumes the dataset.
  • Where AI-generated data is used in further AI development, the AI system that generated it is recorded as a source.
  • Provenance records support audit, customer enquiry, incident investigation, and reproducibility.

9. Preparation

  • Preparation pipelines are version-controlled (Infrastructure-as-Code where feasible).
  • Preparation steps are documented per dataset: cleaning, filtering, labelling, augmentation, transformation, splitting (train / validation / test).
  • Preparation that may introduce bias is reviewed for that effect (e.g. filtering by language or geographic origin).
  • Labelling processes are documented; inter-annotator agreement is measured for subjective labels.
  • Test sets are kept separate from training data and are not used for model selection.

10. Use in Training and Evaluation

  • Each training run records: dataset version(s) used, preparation pipeline version, hyperparameters, output model artefact, evaluation results.
  • Evaluation results record performance overall and across population segments where bias is a risk.
  • Models are not promoted to deployment without documented evaluation against current criteria.
  • Reproducibility — a documented training run can be re-executed to produce a comparable model.

11. Personal Data and Privacy

  • Personal data in datasets is processed under POL-010 and recorded in REG-ROPA-001.
  • Pseudonymisation, anonymisation, or synthetic substitution is applied wherever the AI purpose can be achieved without identifying data.
  • DPIA under POL-010 / REG-DPIA-001 is initiated for AI uses involving personal data with material risk.
  • AISIA under AI-PRO-002 covers data subject impact.
  • Data subject erasure requests applicable to AI training data are handled under PRO-024 / PRO-017 with awareness that retraining may be necessary in some cases.

12. Customer Voice and Transcript Data

  • Customer voice and transcript data is classified Restricted under POL-004.
  • Use of customer data for purposes beyond delivering the contracted service requires explicit customer permission expressed in contract.
  • Where the customer contract permits use for customer-specific model improvement, the use is technically isolated to that customer’s benefit.
  • Customer data shared with third-party AI providers is governed by AI-POL-009 and the customer DPA — sub-processor disclosure, no-training commitment where required.
  • Customer data deletion at end of customer contract follows the customer DPA and PRO-017.

13. Synthetic Data

  • Synthetic data is generated under documented methods recorded in the dataset record.
  • Source data (if any) used to generate synthetic data is recorded.
  • Synthetic data validity is assessed before use (does it support the intended AI purpose comparably to real data).
  • Synthetic data does not eliminate bias — bias in source data may persist or be amplified.
  • Where synthetic data is , the generating AI system is itself in scope of the AIMS.

14. Storage, Access, and Encryption

  • Datasets are stored on Cloudax-approved systems with classification-appropriate controls (POL-002, POL-004).
  • Restricted datasets (including customer voice and transcript data) are encrypted at rest using approved cryptography (POL-023).
  • Access is least-privilege and logged (POL-019).
  • Backups are governed by POL-018 with appropriate retention.
  • Cross-region replication respects customer data residency commitments.

15. Retention and Deletion

  • Each dataset has a defined retention period per POL-009 and the dataset record.
  • Datasets retained for reproducibility but not in current use are held with appropriate access controls.
  • Datasets are deleted at end of retention per PRO-017.
  • Customer data deletion at end of customer contract follows the customer DPA and PRO-017; cross-region backups are addressed within their retention windows.

16. Records

  • AI-REG-DATA-001 holds the master AI Data and Models Register.
  • Personal data processing for AI is recorded in REG-ROPA-001.
  • Records are retained per POL-009.

17. Compliance Monitoring

  • Compliance is monitored under AI-PROG-COMP-001 and audited under AI-PROG-AUDIT-001.

18. Maintenance

This Procedure is reviewed at least annually.

19. Acknowledgement

AI Builders, the CTO, the AI Governance Lead, and the Privacy Lead acknowledge this Procedure on commencement, on material change, and on request.
