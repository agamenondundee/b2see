1. Purpose

This document records the AIMS roles, accountabilities, and the RACI matrix for the principal AIMS activities. It implements ISO/IEC 42001:2023 Clause 5.3 (Roles, responsibilities and authorities) and supports A.3.2 (AI roles and responsibilities).

ISO 27001:2022 references: ISO 42001 reference: Cl. 5.3 Roles, responsibilities and authorities; A.3.2 AI roles and responsibilities

2. Scope

This document covers the principal AIMS activities. Detailed per-procedure RACI is held within the relevant procedure or document; this document is the master.

3. Definitions

RACI:

  • R — Responsible: does the work.
  • A — Accountable: answerable for completion and quality. There is exactly one A per activity.
  • C — Consulted: provides input before decisions; two-way communication.
  • I — Informed: kept informed of progress; one-way communication.

4. Role Definitions

Cloudax operates with a small team. Roles below describe the distinct accountabilities; a single individual may hold more than one role. The current allocation of role-to-individual is recorded in the live AIMS RACI workbook.

ROLE

DESCRIPTION

CEO

Chief Executive Officer. Ultimate accountability for the AIMS.

AIGL

AI Governance Lead. Day-to-day owner of the AIMS.

CTO

Chief Technology Officer. Technical accountability for AI systems.

PL

Privacy Lead. Personal data and data subject impact accountability.

ISM

Information Security Manager. ISMS owner; coordinates with the AIMS where activities overlap.

ENG

Engineering / AI Builders. Develop, deploy, operate AI systems.

CFL

Customer-facing leads. Customer success, sales, support.

LM

Line managers. Operational management of personnel.

AUD

Internal auditor (in-house or external).

5. How to Read the RACI

  • Each row is an activity. Each column is a role. Cells contain R / A / C / I.
  • Activities below are grouped by domain. The detailed per-procedure RACI is held within the relevant procedure document.
  • Where Cloudax’s small team means a single person holds multiple roles, the RACI still distinguishes the accountabilities; that person acts in each role distinctly.

7. Cloudax-Sized Realities

  • At Cloudax’s current size, the AI Governance Lead and Information Security Manager may be the same individual. Where this is the case, that individual acts distinctly in each role.
  • Internal audit independence: where in-house separation is not feasible, an external auditor is engaged (per AI-PROG-AUDIT-001 §6).
  • As the team grows, role-to-individual allocations are reviewed and updated in the live RACI workbook.

8. Coordination with the ISMS RACI

  • This RACI is reconciled with ISMS-RACI-001.
  • Where activities span both management systems (e.g. incident response with both AI and security dimensions, supplier review for AI providers), the RACIs reference each other.
  • Reconciliation is performed annually and on material change to either RACI.

9. Maintenance

This document is reviewed at least annually and on material change to AIMS roles, structure, or activities. Material changes are approved by the Chief Executive Officer and recorded in Annex B.
