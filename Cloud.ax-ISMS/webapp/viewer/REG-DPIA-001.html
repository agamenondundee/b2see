1. Purpose

This DPIA Register records all Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) carried out by Cloudax Ltd. in accordance with Article 35 of the UK GDPR and POL-010 Privacy Policy.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.5.34 Privacy and protection of PII; Article 35 UK GDPR

2. Scope

This Register covers all DPIAs carried out by Cloudax in any role under the UK GDPR. Where Cloudax acts as processor, DPIAs may be carried out by the customer-controller; Cloudax records its supporting role and the customer’s DPIA reference where this is provided.

3. Definitions

  • DPIA: Data Protection Impact Assessment — a process to identify and minimise the data protection risks of a project.
  • High risk: the threshold at which a DPIA becomes mandatory under Article 35.

4. When a DPIA is Required

A DPIA is mandatory under Article 35 where processing is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons. Triggers include but are not limited to:

  • Systematic and extensive automated decision-making, including profiling.
  • Large-scale processing of special category or criminal offence data.
  • Systematic monitoring of publicly accessible areas.
  • Use of innovative technology, including new AI, ML, or biometric processing.
  • Combining or matching datasets in unexpected ways.
  • Processing involving children or other vulnerable data subjects.

Cloudax also conducts a DPIA where its own assessment indicates significant risk, even where not strictly mandatory, in line with the ICO’s good-practice guidance.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Privacy Lead: owns this Register; reviews and signs off DPIAs.
  • Project owner / process owner: leads the DPIA, ensures the assessment captures all relevant facts.
  • Information Security Manager: contributes the security perspective.
  • CTO / Engineering Lead: contributes the technical perspective.
  • Chief Executive Officer: notified of high residual risk; approves ICO consultation if required.

6. How to Use This Register

  • Each DPIA is given a unique identifier (DPIA-NNN).
  • Status values: Triggered, In progress, Approved, Approved with conditions, Rejected, Closed (no DPIA required).
  • This document holds a representative sample; live DPIAs are completed using the Cloudax DPIA template and stored alongside this Register.

The DPIA template covers, at minimum: a systematic description of the envisaged processing operations and purposes; an assessment of the necessity and proportionality of the processing; an assessment of the risks to the rights and freedoms of data subjects; the measures envisaged to address the risks. The template is held with this Register.

8. ICO Consultation

  • Where a DPIA indicates high residual risk that cannot be mitigated, prior consultation with the ICO is required under Article 36.
  • Consultation is prepared and submitted by the Privacy Lead; the Chief Executive Officer is informed and signs off the submission.
  • ICO responses and any required changes are recorded against the DPIA entry.

9. Maintenance

  • This Register is reviewed by the Privacy Lead at least annually.
  • Each DPIA is reviewed at the date set in its record, and on material change to the processing it covers.
