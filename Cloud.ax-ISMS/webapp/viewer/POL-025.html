1. Purpose

This Policy defines how Cloudax Ltd. manages changes to its information systems, services, and supporting infrastructure to preserve security, stability, and compliance.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.8.32 Change management

2. Scope

This Policy applies to all changes to information systems, services, configurations, and supporting infrastructure within the Cloudax ISMS scope, including changes implemented via code, configuration, manual action, or third-party intervention.

3. Definitions

  • Change: the addition, modification, or removal of an asset, configuration, or service.
  • Standard change: a pre-approved, low-risk change with a defined and tested procedure.
  • Normal change: a non-standard change subject to assessment, approval, and scheduling.
  • Emergency change: a change requiring expedited handling to address an incident or imminent risk.

4. Principles

  • Every change has an owner.
  • Every change is recorded.
  • Every change is reviewed by an appropriate second person before deployment.
  • Risk-significant changes are assessed and approved at an appropriate level.
  • Changes are reversible, or have documented mitigations for the risk of failure.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Chief Technology Officer: owns this Policy and the change framework.
  • Information Security Manager: approves security-significant changes.
  • Change owners: drive changes through approval, implementation, and review.
  • Reviewers / approvers: provide independent review and approval.

6. Categories of Change

  • Standard / pre-approved: low-risk, repeatable, with a documented procedure.
  • Normal: requires assessment, approval, and scheduling.
  • Emergency: required to respond to an incident or imminent risk; subject to retrospective review.

7. Change Approval

  • Code changes are approved through pull-request review by a separate person and required CI/CD checks.
  • Infrastructure-as-Code changes are approved in the same way.
  • Material architecture changes are approved by the Chief Technology Officer.
  • Security-significant changes (e.g. authentication, encryption, network boundaries, customer data handling) are reviewed by the Information Security Manager.

8. Change Records

  • Change records include the description, rationale, risk assessment, rollback plan, approver, implementer, and outcome.
  • Records are retained per POL-009.

9. Standard / Pre-Approved Changes

  • Standard changes follow a published procedure that has been risk-assessed and approved in advance.
  • Standard change procedures are reviewed at least annually.

10. Normal Changes

  • Normal changes are submitted with description, rationale, impact, and rollback.
  • Approval is granted by an appropriately authorised role.
  • Production deployments occur in defined windows or via continuous deployment with appropriate guardrails.

11. Emergency Changes

  • Emergency changes are authorised by the on-call lead in line with PLAN-IRP-001.
  • Emergency changes are recorded retrospectively and reviewed at the post-incident review.

12. Production Changes via CI/CD

  • Production changes are deployed through CI/CD pipelines with controlled access (POL-024).
  • Pipeline guardrails enforce required checks (review, tests, security scans).
  • Manual production changes outside the pipeline are exceptional, authorised, and documented.

13. Change to Security-Significant Configurations

  • Changes to identity providers, MFA configuration, network boundaries, encryption settings, or DLP rules require Information Security Manager review.
  • Changes to logging or monitoring that would reduce coverage require Information Security Manager approval.

14. Change Communication

  • Material changes affecting customers are communicated in line with customer contracts and operational procedures.
  • Internal changes affecting working practices are communicated through the appropriate channels.

15. Post-Implementation Review

  • Outcomes of significant changes are reviewed; failures are treated as events under PRO-027.
  • Lessons learned feed continual improvement.

16. Review and Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually and on material change.

17. Acknowledgement

All personnel involved in implementing changes are required to acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
