1. Purpose

This Policy governs the resources Cloudax allocates to its AI systems — data, tooling, system and computing infrastructure, and human resources. It supports the AI Policy (AI-POL-001) and gives effect to A.4 of ISO/IEC 42001:2023.

Data resources are governed in detail by the Data for AI Systems Policy (AI-POL-006); this Policy provides the inventory and documentation requirement that frames AI-POL-006.

ISO 27001:2022 references: ISO 42001 reference: A.4.2 Resource documentation; A.4.3 Data resources; A.4.4 Tooling resources; A.4.5 System and computing resources; A.4.6 Human resources

2. Scope

This Policy applies to all resources used in the AI systems within the AIMS scope (AIMS-SCOPE-001).

3. Definitions

  • AI resource: any data, tool, system, computing capability, or human capability used in the development or operation of an AI system.
  • Tooling: software libraries, frameworks, MLOps platforms, monitoring tooling, and development tools used to build and operate AI systems.
  • Computing resource: compute, storage, and networking used in AI training, fine-tuning, inference, and operation.

4. Principles

  • Resources are inventoried — you cannot govern what you cannot see.
  • Adequacy is assessed — resources match the demands of the AI systems they support.
  • Sustainability is considered — lower-cost (in compute, energy) options are preferred where they meet requirements.
  • Resource decisions are documented — with rationale, alternatives considered, and review cadence.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Chief Technology Officer: owns this Policy; accountable for AI tooling, system, and computing resources.
  • AI Governance Lead: accountable for AI resource inventory completeness; reviews resource decisions for AIMS implications.
  • Privacy Lead: contributes input on data resources, particularly personal data.
  • Engineering: maintains the AI System Inventory (AI-REG-INV-001) and the AI Data and Models Register (AI-REG-DATA-001) for resources within their domain.

6. Tooling Resources

  • AI tooling — ML frameworks, training pipelines, evaluation harnesses, MLOps platforms, monitoring tools — is recorded in AI-REG-INV-001.
  • New tooling is approved through the existing supplier onboarding process (PRO-021) extended for AI considerations under AI-POL-009.
  • Tooling is maintained at supported versions; obsolescence is tracked.
  • Tooling that processes Cloudax data is subject to the Information Classification Policy (POL-004) and the Data for AI Systems Policy (AI-POL-006).

7. System and Computing Resources

  • AI compute is provisioned through the existing cloud provider arrangements governed by POL-007 Cloud Services Policy.
  • Specialised AI accelerators (GPU, TPU, custom silicon) are inventoried separately where their availability or change is material to AI service continuity.
  • AI storage — datasets, model artefacts, evaluation outputs — is provisioned with classification, encryption, and access controls per POL-002, POL-004, POL-018, POL-023.
  • AI networking is governed under POL-022 Network Security Policy.
  • Capacity is forecast and managed under PRO-029 Capacity Management Procedure (cross-reference).
  • Sustainability — compute and energy footprint of AI training and inference — is considered when material decisions are taken (e.g. model selection, batch processing scheduling).

8. Human Resources and Competence

  • AI roles requiring specific competence are documented (Section 5; AI-POL-002).
  • Competence is developed through the AI Awareness and Training Programme (AI-PROG-TRAIN-001).
  • Recruitment for AI-critical roles considers AI-specific experience and qualifications.
  • Where in-house AI competence is insufficient, external advisers, contractors, or consultancy support is engaged under POL-006 Supplier Security.
  • AI competence requirements are reviewed annually as part of the AIMS resource adequacy review (Section 10).

9. Resource Documentation

Resource documentation is maintained across:

  • AI System Inventory (AI-REG-INV-001) — the AI systems themselves, their tooling, and the compute they consume.
  • AI Data and Models Register (AI-REG-DATA-001) — the data and model resources used in those systems.
  • Third-Party AI Provider Register (AI-REG-3P-001) — where the resource is consumed from a third party.
  • Cloudax Supplier Inventory (ISMS) — where the resource is from a commercial supplier.

Each resource record includes: identifier, description, owner, classification, dependencies, and review cadence.

10. Resource Adequacy

  • Resource adequacy is reviewed annually by the AI Governance Lead and the Chief Technology Officer.
  • Review covers: data sufficiency for current and planned AI systems, tooling capability gaps, compute headroom, human competence depth, third-party provider concentration.
  • Material gaps are escalated to the Chief Executive Officer for resource decisions in the next budget cycle.
  • Findings are recorded in REG-CAR-001 where corrective action is needed.

11. Records

  • AI-REG-INV-001 holds the master AI system inventory.
  • AI-REG-DATA-001 holds the master AI data and models register.
  • Records are retained per POL-009.

12. Compliance Monitoring

  • Compliance is monitored under AI-PROG-COMP-001 and audited under AI-PROG-AUDIT-001.

13. Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually.

14. Acknowledgement

All personnel involved in AI resource decisions acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
