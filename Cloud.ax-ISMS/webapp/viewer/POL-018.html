1. Purpose

This Policy defines how Cloudax Ltd. backs up information so it can be restored after loss, corruption, or compromise, supporting business continuity and disaster recovery.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.8.13 Information backup

2. Scope

This Policy applies to all information within the Cloudax ISMS scope where loss would have business, contractual, or regulatory impact — customer data, source code, configuration, identity data, financial records, ISMS evidence, and other business records.

3. Definitions

  • Backup: a copy of information made for the purpose of restoration following loss or corruption.
  • RPO: recovery point objective — the maximum acceptable amount of data loss measured in time.
  • RTO: recovery time objective — the maximum acceptable time to restore service.

4. Principles

  • Backups are made of all information whose loss would have material impact.
  • Backups follow a 3-2-1 approach where practicable: at least three copies, on at least two different media or services, with at least one off-site or in a separate cloud region or account.
  • Backups are protected from unauthorised access, alteration, deletion, and ransomware (immutability where supported).
  • Backups are tested through restoration.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Chief Technology Officer: owns the backup strategy and operations for the Cloudax platform.
  • Information Security Manager: owns this Policy.
  • System owners: ensure systems they own are backed up; participate in restoration testing.

6. Backup Strategy

  • Production data is backed up continuously where supported, with point-in-time recovery.
  • Configuration and source code are version-controlled and replicated.
  • SaaS data (Microsoft 365, identity provider, source code platform) is backed up using third-party backup tooling or provider-native facilities.
  • Backups span multiple cloud regions or accounts where supported.

7. Backup Frequency and Retention

Backup frequency and retention are documented per system, aligned to RPO/RTO from PLAN-BCP-001 and the Records Retention Schedule (POL-009). Default targets:

  • Customer-facing platform data: continuous / point-in-time, retained for the period defined in the customer contract.
  • Configuration and source code: every commit; retained for the life of the system.
  • Business records and ISMS evidence: daily; retained per POL-009.

8. Storage and Geographic Distribution

  • Backups for customer data are stored in regions consistent with customer contractual data residency requirements.
  • Off-site and multi-region storage protects against region-level failure.

9. Encryption of Backups

  • Backups are encrypted at rest and in transit using approved cryptography (POL-023).
  • Encryption keys are managed in approved key management services.

10. Access Controls

  • Access to backups follows least privilege; restoration is performed by named, authorised personnel.
  • Backup credentials are separate from production credentials.
  • Backups are immutable or tamper-evident where supported, to mitigate ransomware.

11. Restoration Testing

  • Restoration is tested at least annually for each material backup; tests are documented.
  • Critical systems are tested more frequently as defined in PLAN-DRP-001.
  • Tests verify both data integrity and the ability to meet RTO.

12. Backup of Source Code, Configuration, and IaC

  • Source code repositories are backed up using third-party backup tooling and replication.
  • Infrastructure-as-Code is held in version control with offsite replication.

13. Backup of Customer Data

  • Customer data backup arrangements are described to customers in customer-facing documentation and contractual terms.
  • Restoration to a point in time is supported within the platform’s capability.

14. SaaS and Microsoft 365 Backup

  • Microsoft 365 mailboxes, OneDrive, SharePoint, and Teams content are backed up.
  • Identity provider configuration is backed up.
  • Cloudax recognises that provider-native retention is not a substitute for backup.

15. Disposal of End-of-Life Backups

  • Expired backups are deleted in accordance with PRO-017 and POL-009.
  • Retired backup media is sanitised under POL-013.

16. Compliance Monitoring

  • Backup coverage, success rate, and restoration test outcomes are reported to management review.

17. Review and Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually and on material change.

18. Acknowledgement

All personnel involved in backup operations are required to acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
