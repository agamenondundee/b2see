1. Purpose

This Access Control Policy defines how Cloudax Ltd. (“Cloudax”) controls access to information, systems, networks, applications, source code, and information processing facilities. It supports the Information Security Policy (POL-001) and gives effect to the Annex A controls listed in Annex A of this Policy and recorded in the Statement of Applicability (ISMS-SOA-001 v4.0).

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.5.3, A.5.15, A.5.16, A.5.17, A.7.6, A.8.2, A.8.3, A.8.5, A.8.18

2. Scope

This Policy applies to all access to Cloudax information assets, including endpoints, identity providers, cloud tenancies, production and non-production environments, SaaS applications, source code repositories, customer data, and Cloudax facilities.

It applies to all personnel, contractors, suppliers, and third parties who require access to Cloudax information assets.

3. Definitions

  • Identity: a unique digital representation of a person, system, or service.
  • Authentication: the process of verifying that a claimed identity is genuine.
  • Authorisation: the process of granting an authenticated identity permission to perform actions or access resources.
  • Privileged access: access rights that allow an identity to perform actions that materially affect the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of information or systems.
  • Privileged utility program: a program able to override system or application controls (e.g. system administration tools, debuggers, command-line system utilities, network sniffers).

4. Principles

  • Least privilege — identities receive the minimum access required to perform their role.
  • Need to know — access is granted only where there is a legitimate business need.
  • Segregation of duties — conflicting duties are segregated where risk requires it.
  • Default deny — access is denied unless explicitly granted.
  • Personal accountability — every action is attributable to an individual identity; shared accounts are prohibited.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.5.3, A.5.15

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Information Security Manager: owns this Policy and the access management framework.
  • Chief Technology Officer: owns the technical implementation of access controls in production and supporting systems.
  • System and application owners: define the access model for systems they own; approve access; perform reviews.
  • Line managers: approve access for direct reports; ensure access is removed promptly on role change or termination.

6. Identity Management

  • Each user is issued a unique, named identity in the corporate identity provider.
  • Shared and generic accounts are prohibited. Where unavoidable (e.g. legacy system), an exception is documented, time-bound, and compensated.
  • Service accounts are uniquely named, owned by a named human, documented, and rotated according to their threat model.
  • Identities are linked to authoritative sources (e.g. HR systems for employees) and provisioned through automated workflows where possible.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.5.16 Identity management

7. Authentication

  • Passwords or passphrases must be at least 14 characters and unique to each system. The approved password manager must be used.
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for the corporate identity provider, Microsoft 365, VPN, privileged consoles, source code repositories, and production environments.
  • Phishing-resistant MFA factors (e.g. WebAuthn / FIDO2) are required for privileged accounts.
  • Single sign-on (SSO) is the default for SaaS applications.
  • Service-to-service authentication uses short-lived credentials, certificates, or workload identities.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.5.17 Authentication information; A.8.5 Secure authentication

8. Authorisation and Access Rights

  • Access rights are granted based on documented role definitions and least privilege.
  • New access requests are submitted through the access management workflow (PRO-005), approved by line manager and system owner.
  • Access rights are time-bound where appropriate, particularly for contractors and privileged access.
  • Self-granted or self-approved access is prohibited.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.8.3 Information access restriction

9. Privileged Access and Privileged Utility Programs

  • Privileged accounts are separate from standard accounts. Privileged actions are not performed from standard user accounts.
  • Privileged access requires phishing-resistant MFA and is logged in detail.
  • Just-in-time elevation is preferred over standing privileged access.
  • Privileged utility programs (system administration tools, debuggers, network analysers) are restricted to authorised personnel and their use is logged.
  • Break-glass accounts are documented, secured, and used only with explicit authorisation.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.8.2 Privileged access rights; A.8.18 Use of privileged utility programs

10. Application and System Access

  • Applications enforce role-based or attribute-based access control.
  • Inactive sessions are terminated automatically after a defined period.
  • Concurrent sessions are restricted where required by risk profile.
  • Source addresses, device posture, and contextual signals may be used as part of access decisions.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.5.15, A.8.3, A.8.5

11. Source Code Access

  • Source code repositories require SSO and MFA.
  • Branch protection rules require code review by a separate person before merge to protected branches.
  • Direct push to protected branches is prohibited.
  • Repository access is reviewed at least quarterly.

12. Customer Data Access

Access to customer data — including voice recordings, transcripts, and derived data — is treated as Restricted under POL-004 Information Classification Policy.

  • Access is granted only on a least-privilege, need-to-know basis with documented business reason.
  • Customer data access is logged in detail; logs are protected against tampering and reviewed.
  • Customer-specific support access (impersonation, break-glass) requires explicit authorisation, is time-bound, and is recorded.

13. Third-Party and Supplier Access

  • Access for third parties is granted only after supplier approval under POL-006 Supplier Security Policy.
  • Third-party access is granted to named individuals (never shared accounts) and is time-bound.

14. Working in Secure Areas

Where Cloudax personnel work in secure areas — including the Cloudax-controlled zones within the shared workspace and any customer-controlled secure areas — the following requirements apply:

  • Access to secure areas is restricted to personnel whose role requires it.
  • Photography, video, and audio recording in secure areas requires authorisation.
  • Visitors to secure areas are signed in, escorted, and not left unattended.
  • Lone working in secure areas is avoided where reasonably practicable; where it occurs, defined check-in procedures apply.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.7.6 Working in secure areas

15. Access Reviews

Access rights are reviewed:

  • At least annually for all systems within the ISMS scope.
  • At least quarterly for production environments, source code repositories, and customer data.
  • Whenever a person changes role or leaves Cloudax.
  • In response to incidents or material control changes.

16. Joiner, Mover, and Leaver Process

Joiner, Mover, Leaver (JML) is operated under PRO-005.

  • Joiner: identities provisioned only after the joiner workflow is complete (including signed acknowledgement of relevant policies).
  • Mover: access reassessed when a person changes role; access no longer required is removed promptly.
  • Leaver: access revoked on termination, in coordination with HR, no later than end of the working day on which employment or engagement ends.

17. Logging and Monitoring of Access

  • Authentication, authorisation, and privileged access events are logged for all in-scope systems (POL-019 Logging Policy).
  • Alerts are configured for indicators of credential compromise, anomalous access, and policy violations (POL-020 Monitoring Policy).
  • Suspected credential compromise triggers immediate password reset, MFA reset, and incident review.

18. Segregation of Duties

Conflicting duties are segregated where risk requires it. In a small organisation, full separation may not always be feasible; in such cases compensating controls (peer review, deployment approvals, four-eyes checks) are documented and applied. Examples of segregations operated at Cloudax include:

  • Code review by a separate person before merge to protected branches.
  • Deployment approval by a separate person from the change author for production deployments.
  • Independent access reviews carried out by a person other than the access grantor.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.5.3 Segregation of duties

19. Policy Breaches

Breaches are managed in accordance with POL-011 Disciplinary Policy and may result in disciplinary action, termination of contract, or legal action where appropriate.

20. Review and Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually and on material change. Material changes are approved by the Chief Executive Officer and recorded in Annex B.

21. Acknowledgement

All personnel are required to acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
