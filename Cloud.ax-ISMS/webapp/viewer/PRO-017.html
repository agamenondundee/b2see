1. Purpose

This Procedure defines how Cloudax Ltd. securely deletes information when it is no longer required, in line with POL-009 Records Retention Policy and POL-010 Privacy Policy.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.8.10 Information deletion; A.5.17 Authentication information (rotation); supports A.5.33 Protection of records

2. Scope

This Procedure applies to all Cloudax information held in any form, including in cloud services, on endpoints, on removable media, in physical records, and in third-party services on Cloudax’s behalf.

3. Definitions

  • Deletion: the removal of information such that it cannot reasonably be recovered.
  • Cryptographic erasure: rendering encrypted data inaccessible by destroying the encryption key.
  • Sanitisation: the process of removing data from media so it cannot be retrieved.

4. Principles

  • Delete what is no longer required, when it is no longer required.
  • Use the deletion method appropriate to the storage medium and the data classification.
  • Verify deletion where the data classification or contractual obligation requires.
  • Record deletion where required for evidence, regulatory compliance, or contractual obligation.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Information owners: identify when information should be deleted and trigger deletion.
  • Privacy Lead: leads deletion in response to data subject requests and privacy obligations.
  • CTO: ensures platform deletion controls are effective.
  • IT support: performs deletion on endpoints and removable media.
  • Information Security Manager: owns this Procedure; spot-checks compliance.

6. Triggers for Deletion

Deletion is triggered by:

  • Expiry of retention period in the Records Retention Schedule (POL-009).
  • End of customer engagement and data export / handover (per customer DPA).
  • Successful data subject erasure request under Article 17 UK GDPR.
  • End of life of equipment containing data.
  • Decommissioning of a system or service.
  • Withdrawal of consent (where consent was the lawful basis).

7. Deletion Methods

7.1 Cryptographic Erasure

  • Where data is encrypted at rest with managed keys, deletion of the key destroys the data.
  • Cryptographic erasure is the preferred method for cloud-resident encrypted data and for end-of-life encrypted endpoints.
  • Key destruction is recorded in the cloud key management service or HSM.

7.2 Secure Overwrite

  • For unencrypted media, secure overwrite is performed using approved utilities.
  • Multi-pass overwrite is used for highly sensitive data on traditional spinning media; single-pass overwrite is sufficient for SSDs (which lack predictable physical mapping).

7.3 Physical Destruction

  • Physical destruction (shredding, disintegration) is used for media that cannot be securely overwritten or where contractual requirements demand it.
  • Where third-party disposal is used, certificates of destruction are obtained per PRO-028.

7.4 Cloud Provider Deletion

  • Cloud-resident data is deleted using provider-supported deletion features.
  • Where contractual or regulatory requirements demand verification, provider attestation of deletion is obtained.
  • Awareness is maintained of provider retention windows for deleted resources (e.g. soft-delete grace periods, snapshot retention).

9. Verification of Deletion

  • Where the data classification is Restricted, deletion is verified.
  • Where customer contracts require certificate or attestation of deletion, this is provided.
  • Verification methods include provider attestation, manual sample-check, or automated audit.

10. Backup Considerations

  • Deletion in primary systems may not be immediately reflected in backups.
  • Where deletion must propagate to backups (e.g. erasure requests), backup retention is taken into account in the response timeline; the data subject is informed where there is a delay.
  • Where backups expire on a fixed schedule, the latest expiry is the effective deletion date for backed-up data.

11. Records of Deletion

  • Routine deletion (per retention schedule) is not individually recorded except where regulation requires (e.g. payments data).
  • Erasure under data subject requests is recorded with date, scope, and confirming role.
  • Customer-data deletion at end of engagement is recorded with confirmation to the customer.
  • Equipment disposal is recorded under PRO-028.

12. Customer Data Deletion

  • On termination of a customer contract, customer data is exported / returned where required, then deleted within the timeframe specified in the customer contract.
  • Deletion covers production data, backups (within their retention window), and any internal copies (e.g. test data, analytics).
  • Customer is provided with confirmation of deletion.
  • The customer ROPA entry (REG-ROPA-001) is updated to reflect deletion.

13. Compliance Monitoring

  • Compliance is monitored through internal audit and Privacy Lead review.
  • Findings are recorded in REG-CAR-001.

14. Maintenance

This Procedure is reviewed at least annually and on material change to retention obligations, technology, or supplier base.

15. Acknowledgement

All personnel involved in information deletion (CTO, ISM, Privacy Lead, IT support) are required to acknowledge this Procedure on commencement, on material change, and on request.
