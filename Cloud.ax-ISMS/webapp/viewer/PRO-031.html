1. Purpose

This Procedure defines how Cloudax Ltd. raises, manages, and closes corrective actions arising from nonconformities, audit findings, incidents, and improvement opportunities. It implements Clauses 10.1 and 10.2 of ISO/IEC 27001:2022 and operationalises REG-CAR-001 Corrective Actions Register.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.8.34 Protection of information systems during audit testing (operational underpinning); Cl. 10.1 Continual improvement; Cl. 10.2 Nonconformity and corrective action

2. Scope

This Procedure applies to all corrective actions affecting the Cloudax ISMS, regardless of source.

3. Definitions

  • Nonconformity: non-fulfilment of a requirement.
  • Correction: immediate action to address a nonconformity — fixing the symptom.
  • Corrective action: action to eliminate the cause of a nonconformity to prevent recurrence — fixing the root cause.

4. Principles

  • Address symptoms quickly; address causes deliberately.
  • Owners are named and empowered.
  • Actions are tracked to evidenced effectiveness, not just to claimed completion.
  • Trends drive continual improvement.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Information Security Manager: owns this Procedure and REG-CAR-001; classifies actions; tracks closure; reports to management review.
  • Action owners: implement actions; record evidence; propose closure.
  • Verifiers: independent confirmation of effectiveness before closure.
  • Chief Executive Officer: receives major nonconformity escalations.

6. The Corrective Action Lifecycle

6.1 Identification

  • Nonconformities and improvement opportunities are identified through the sources listed in Section 8.
  • The person identifying the issue raises it through the appropriate channel — audit report, incident record, monitoring tooling, customer enquiry, management review.

6.2 Recording

  • The Information Security Manager records the action in REG-CAR-001 with description, source, severity, owner, target closure date.
  • Linked records (incident, audit finding, customer ticket) are cross-referenced.

6.3 Initial Correction

  • Where the nonconformity is causing ongoing harm, immediate correction is taken to address the symptom.
  • Initial correction is recorded against the action.

6.4 Root Cause Analysis

  • For Major and Serious actions, formal root cause analysis is performed.
  • Tools include 5-Whys, fishbone, and timeline-based analysis (Section 9).
  • Analysis distinguishes proximate cause, contributing factors, and underlying systemic causes.

6.5 Defining the Corrective Action

  • Corrective actions are designed to address root causes, not just symptoms.
  • Where multiple actions are needed, they are recorded individually and linked.
  • Each action has a clear acceptance criterion: how will we know it has worked?

6.6 Implementation

  • Action owner implements the action and records evidence.
  • Implementation is tracked against the target date; slippage is escalated.

6.7 Verification of Effectiveness

  • Once implemented, effectiveness is verified — by retesting, by sampling, or by observed absence of recurrence over a defined period.
  • Verification is performed by a person other than the action implementer.
  • Where the action is ineffective, it is reopened or a new action is raised.

6.8 Closure

  • Once verified, the action is closed in REG-CAR-001 with closure date, closing role, and evidence reference.
  • Closure is reported in the next management review summary.

7. Severity and Targets

Severity classification follows REG-CAR-001 §7:

  • Major nonconformity — target closure 90 days; CEO informed.
  • Minor nonconformity — target closure 90 days.
  • Observation / opportunity — tracked but no fixed target.

8. Sources of Corrective Actions

  • Internal audit (PROG-AUDIT-001).
  • External audit (certification body, customer audits).
  • Compliance monitoring (PROG-COMP-001).
  • Information security incidents (PLAN-IRP-001) and lessons learned (PRO-026).
  • Personal data breaches (PRO-024).
  • Customer assurance findings.
  • Supplier assurance findings.
  • Management review (MGT-MR-LOG).
  • Operational events and weaknesses reported under PRO-027.

9. Tools and Techniques

  • 5-Whys for proximate causation.
  • Fishbone (Ishikawa) diagrams for multi-factor causation.
  • Timeline-based analysis for incidents.
  • Pareto analysis for trend identification.

Tools used are recorded against the action where formal RCA was performed.

10. Reporting and Escalation

  • Open and overdue actions are reported to management review at least every six months.
  • Major nonconformities are escalated to the Chief Executive Officer immediately.
  • Critical findings (potential breach, active exploitation, regulator interest) are escalated immediately.

11. Trends and Continual Improvement

  • Patterns across actions — by source, by domain, by root cause class — are reviewed quarterly.
  • Recurring patterns are addressed by upstream improvements (policy, training, tooling).
  • Closure rate, time-to-close, and reopen rate are tracked as ISMS health indicators.

12. Records and Retention

  • REG-CAR-001 holds the master record of all corrective actions.
  • Supporting evidence (RCA notes, implementation evidence, verification records) is held with the action.
  • Records are retained per POL-009 — typically 6 years from closure.

13. Compliance Monitoring

  • Compliance with this Procedure is reviewed at internal audit.
  • Effectiveness of the corrective action programme is reported to management review.

14. Maintenance

This Procedure is reviewed at least annually and on material change.

15. Acknowledgement

Personnel involved in raising or owning corrective actions (ISM, CTO, Privacy Lead, line managers) are required to acknowledge this Procedure on commencement, on material change, and on request.
