Annex — Version History

1. Purpose

This Master Document List is the controlled, authoritative inventory of every document that forms the Cloudax AI Management System (AIMS). It identifies, classifies, and tracks the version of each document, and records the relationship of each document to the AIMS map (AIMS-MAP-001), the AIMS Statement of Applicability (AIMS-SOA-001), and the controls and clauses of ISO/IEC 42001:2023.

It supports Clause 7.5 of ISO/IEC 42001:2023 (Documented information — control of documented information) by providing a single point of reference for the existence, currency, and version of every AIMS document.

ISO 27001:2022 references: ISO 42001 reference: Cl. 7.5 Documented information

2. Scope

This list covers all documented information that forms the Cloudax AIMS, including:

  • The apex AI Policy (AI-POL-001).
  • Core AIMS documents required by ISO/IEC 42001:2023 Clauses 4 to 10.
  • Plans, programmes, and policies giving effect to the controls in the AI Statement of Applicability.
  • Procedures, registers, and governance records that operationalise the AI policy framework.

It does not list operational records produced by the AIMS in life (e.g. individual AISIA documents, individual AI incident records); these are governed by their parent register.

3. Definitions

  • Document: a controlled item of documented information that forms part of the AIMS, e.g. a policy, procedure, register, plan, or programme.
  • Document ID: a unique controlled identifier in the form TYPE-NUMBER (e.g. AI-POL-001, AI-PRO-002, AI-REG-INV-001).
  • Tier: the level of the document in the AIMS hierarchy as defined in AIMS-MAP-001 v1.0.
  • Version: the controlled version number of the document.

4. How to Use This List

  • This list is the authoritative reference for whether a document exists in the Cloudax AIMS, what version it is at, and which control or clause it gives effect to.
  • When citing a document, always use the Document ID, the title, and the version (e.g. AI-POL-005 AI System Lifecycle Policy v1.0).
  • When introducing a new document, add it to this list at issue and update the AIMS SoA where applicable.
  • When a document is superseded or withdrawn, the entry is updated in this list with the closure date — entries are not deleted.
  • Auditors and certification bodies may be provided this list as the index to the AIMS evidence pack.

7. Status Conventions

  • Approved: the document is in force and is the authoritative reference.
  • Draft: the document is under preparation and not yet authoritative.
  • Superseded: the document has been replaced by a later version. The superseded version remains in this list with its closure date.
  • Withdrawn: the document is no longer applicable; the closure date is recorded.

8. Roles and Responsibilities

  • AI Governance Lead: owns this list; updates entries on issue, revision, supersession, or withdrawal of any AIMS document.
  • Document owners: notify the AI Governance Lead when their document is updated.
  • Chief Executive Officer: approves Tier 1 to Tier 4 documents.
  • All personnel: refer to this list as the authoritative reference for AIMS document existence and version.

13. Maintenance

  • This list is reviewed on every issue, revision, supersession, or withdrawal of any AIMS document. Updates are recorded in the version history.
  • A scheduled quarterly review confirms the list against the actual document repository.
  • An annual full review aligns the list with the AIMS map (AIMS-MAP-001) and the AI Statement of Applicability.
  • Material structural changes (e.g. addition of a new tier, change in numbering convention) are approved by the Chief Executive Officer.

14. Acknowledgement

This list is communicated to all personnel as the authoritative reference for AIMS documents. Personnel are required to use the controlled documents listed here — not personal copies, drafts, or superseded versions.
