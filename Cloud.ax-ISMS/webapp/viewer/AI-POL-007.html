1. Purpose

This Policy governs how Cloudax communicates information about its AI systems to interested parties — customers, end-users, regulators, and the public. It implements ISO/IEC 42001:2023 Annex A.8 and supports the AI Policy commitment to honesty about AI capability and limitations.

ISO 27001:2022 references: ISO 42001 reference: A.8.2 System documentation and information for users; A.8.3 External reporting; A.8.4 Communication of incidents; A.8.5 Information for interested parties

2. Scope

This Policy applies to all communications about Cloudax AI systems made by or on behalf of Cloudax, including product documentation, customer-facing materials, regulator communications, marketing, and public statements.

3. Definitions

  • Interested party: any party with a stake in or affected by Cloudax’s AI systems — customers, end-users, regulators, partners, the public.
  • System documentation: the documentation of an AI system’s purpose, capabilities, limitations, and intended use.
  • External reporting: formal communications to authorities or other external bodies about an AI system or its operation.

4. Principles

  • Honest — capabilities are not overstated; limitations are not concealed.
  • Plain language — communications are accessible to the intended audience, not gated by technical jargon.
  • Timely — material information is provided in good time for the recipient to act on it.
  • Coordinated — a single coherent voice across customer, regulator, and public communications.
  • Privacy-respecting — transparency does not disclose customer data or personal data without lawful basis.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • AI Governance Lead: owns this Policy; coordinates AI-related external communications.
  • Chief Executive Officer: approves material public communications and regulator engagement.
  • Chief Technology Officer: contributes technical accuracy review for AI system documentation.
  • Privacy Lead: contributes privacy review; leads ICO communications under PRO-024.
  • Customer-facing leads: own customer-facing AI documentation and customer communication.
  • All personnel: refer external AI enquiries to the appropriate role; do not communicate externally outside their authority.

6. System Documentation and Information for Users

  • Each AI system has documentation describing: purpose; intended use; intended users; capabilities; limitations; data inputs; expected behaviour; performance characteristics in operationally relevant conditions; known failure modes; how the system is monitored.
  • Documentation distinguishes what the system can do from what users may infer it can do; common misunderstandings are explicitly addressed.
  • Documentation is reviewed and refreshed at material change to the AI system.
  • Documentation is held with the AI system entry in AI-REG-INV-001.

7. External Reporting Channels

Cloudax maintains the following external channels for AI-related matters:

  • Customer-facing channel for AI questions, issues, and feedback (typically through the customer success team).
  • Privacy-related channel coordinated by the Privacy Lead (privacy@cloudax.io or equivalent).
  • Security-related channel for AI security issues (security@cloudax.io).
  • Regulator engagement channel through the AI Governance Lead and CEO.
  • Whistleblowing channel for serious concerns (cross-reference to POL-011 §12).

Authority contacts including the ICO, NCSC, and any future AI regulator are recorded in REG-AUTH-001.

8. Communication of Incidents

  • AI incident communication follows AI-PLAN-IRP-001.
  • Affected customers are notified per their contract and the AI Policy commitments.
  • Regulator notification (ICO and others) follows PRO-024 for personal-data-related incidents and AI-PLAN-IRP-001 for other regulatory engagement.
  • Public communication of material AI incidents is approved by the CEO and follows the principles in Section 4 — honest, plain, timely.
  • Internal incident communication follows AI-PLAN-IRP-001 §10.1.

9. Information for Other Interested Parties

Beyond customers and end-users, Cloudax may need to provide AI information to:

  • Investors and partners — typically through commercial channels under appropriate confidentiality.
  • Regulators — through the channels in Section 7.
  • Researchers and the public interested in responsible AI — through summary public materials.
  • Future certification bodies — through the AIMS evidence pack.

10. Customer-Facing AI Documentation

  • Customer-facing AI documentation is prepared for every AI feature in the Cloudax platform that materially affects customer outcomes.
  • Customer-facing documentation covers: what the feature does, what it does not do, how to interpret outputs, known limitations, how to escalate problems, where outputs may be wrong.
  • Customer-facing documentation is published before customer-facing release of the feature.
  • Customer DPAs reference the AI components and any third-party AI sub-processors.

11. End-User Awareness of AI

  • Where Cloudax’s platform interacts with end-users (callers in customer conversations), Cloudax supports the customer in providing appropriate awareness that AI is involved.
  • Where regulation requires AI awareness disclosure to end-users (e.g. in certain jurisdictions, sectors, or future EU AI Act provisions), the platform supports compliance.
  • Cloudax does not actively conceal the use of AI from end-users where awareness would reasonably matter.

12. Public Position

  • Cloudax’s public position on AI is consistent with the AI Policy principles.
  • Marketing claims about AI capabilities are reviewed for accuracy by the CTO and AI Governance Lead before publication.
  • Public engagement on AI standards, regulation, and good practice is encouraged where it does not breach customer confidentiality.

13. Records

  • AI system documentation is held with the AI system entry in AI-REG-INV-001.
  • Customer-facing AI documentation is held in the customer-facing knowledge base / documentation system.
  • Material external AI communications (regulator submissions, public statements) are recorded.
  • Records are retained per POL-009.

14. Compliance Monitoring

  • Compliance is monitored under AI-PROG-COMP-001 and audited under AI-PROG-AUDIT-001.
  • Auditors will typically sample customer-facing AI documentation for currency and accuracy.

15. Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually and on material change to AI regulatory expectations or the Cloudax AI portfolio.

16. Acknowledgement

Personnel involved in customer-facing communication and external engagement acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
