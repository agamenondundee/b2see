1. Purpose

This Disciplinary Policy sets out the formal disciplinary process applied at Cloudax Ltd. for breaches of information security policy. It complements the Cloudax HR disciplinary procedure and provides a documented, consistent, and proportionate framework that reinforces the importance of information security.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.6.4 Disciplinary process

2. Scope

This Policy applies to all Cloudax personnel — directors, employees, contractors, consultants, and other workers — in respect of breaches of information security policies, procedures, and standards. It is read together with the Cloudax HR disciplinary procedure, which governs general workplace conduct.

3. Definitions

  • Breach: an act or omission that contravenes an information security policy, procedure, standard, or contractual obligation.
  • Investigation: the structured fact-finding activity to determine whether a breach has occurred and the appropriate response.

4. Principles

  • Fair, consistent, and proportionate application.
  • Distinction between honest mistakes (handled through coaching and training) and deliberate or reckless conduct (subject to formal action).
  • Prompt reporting in good faith is rewarded, never punished.
  • Process respects the rights of the individual, including a right of representation.
  • Confidentiality is maintained throughout.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Chief Executive Officer: final authority for sanctions involving senior personnel and for major incidents.
  • Information Security Manager: assesses information security breach reports; recommends investigation and outcomes.
  • Line managers: support investigation; implement coaching and training outcomes.
  • HR (where engaged): coordinates the formal disciplinary procedure; ensures procedural fairness and legal compliance.

6. Categories of Breach

Breaches are categorised by severity to drive a proportionate response:

  • Minor: isolated, unintentional, low-impact (e.g. screen left unlocked once). Coaching and refresher training.
  • Moderate: repeated minor breaches, single significant lapse with no material harm (e.g. unauthorised software installation). Formal warning and remedial actions.
  • Serious: breach causing material harm or risk (e.g. transmission of customer data via unauthorised channel). Formal warning, role change, or dismissal as appropriate.
  • Gross: deliberate, malicious, or reckless breach causing serious harm (e.g. theft of data, intentional bypass of controls, fraud). Summary dismissal and referral to authorities where appropriate.

7. Investigation Process

  • Reports are received by the Information Security Manager.
  • An initial assessment determines whether to proceed with informal handling, formal investigation, or referral to HR.
  • Where formal investigation is required, an investigation officer is appointed; the individual is informed (subject to integrity of investigation) and given the opportunity to respond.
  • Findings are recorded and a recommendation is made to the appropriate decision-maker.
  • Suspension during investigation is used only where necessary and is non-disciplinary.

8. Sanctions and Outcomes

Sanctions, applied proportionately, may include:

  • Coaching, refresher training, or written learning notes.
  • Verbal warning, written warning, or final written warning.
  • Removal of access rights or temporary loss of privileges.
  • Demotion, role change, or change of reporting line.
  • Dismissal (with or without notice) for serious or gross breaches.
  • Termination of contract for contractors or third parties.
  • Recovery of losses through civil action.
  • Referral to law enforcement or regulators.

9. Right of Appeal

Personnel subject to formal sanction have a right of appeal as set out in the Cloudax HR disciplinary procedure.

10. Confidentiality and Records

  • Investigations are conducted with appropriate confidentiality.
  • Records are retained in accordance with POL-009 Records Retention Policy.
  • Information shared with witnesses is limited to what is necessary.

11. Cooperation with Authorities and Regulators

Where a breach involves potentially criminal conduct, Cloudax cooperates with law enforcement and regulators in accordance with applicable law and the Authority Contacts Register (REG-AUTH-001).

12. Whistleblowing and Protection from Retaliation

Personnel reporting information security concerns or suspected breaches in good faith are protected from retaliation, in line with Cloudax’s whistleblowing arrangements.

13. Review and Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually and on material change.

14. Acknowledgement

All personnel are required to acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
