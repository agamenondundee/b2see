1. Purpose

This Register holds the record of every AI incident handled under AI-PLAN-IRP-001 and AI-PRO-004. It is the audit trail of AI incident handling and the input to lessons-learned and continual improvement.

ISO 27001:2022 references: ISO 42001 reference: A.6.2.6 AI system operation and monitoring; A.8.4 Communication of incidents; Cl. 10.1 Continual improvement

2. Scope

This Register covers AI incidents (including near-misses and weaknesses material enough to record) affecting any AI system within the AIMS scope.

3. Definitions

Definitions are per AI-PLAN-IRP-001 §3.

4. Roles and Responsibilities

  • AI Governance Lead (AI Incident Manager): owns this Register; ensures completeness.
  • AI Builders / on-call: contribute incident detail at the time of handling.
  • Privacy Lead: contributes detail on personal-data-related incidents.
  • Internal auditor: samples this Register at audit.

5. How to Use This Register

  • Each incident has a unique identifier (AI-INC-NNN).
  • Records are opened at triage (Stage 2 of AI-PRO-004) and updated through the lifecycle.
  • The closed record holds the full audit trail of the incident.
  • Where the incident is also a security incident or personal data breach, cross-references to PLAN-IRP-001 records and PRO-024 records are maintained.
  • This document holds the schema and a starter template; live incidents are recorded in the operational incident management system.

6. Incident Schema

Each incident record contains:

  • Identifier (AI-INC-NNN).
  • Date of detection; date of triage; date of stand-down; date of closure.
  • AI system(s) affected (cross-reference to AI-REG-INV-001).
  • Category (per AI-PLAN-IRP-001 §4).
  • Severity (Critical / Major / Moderate / Minor).
  • Description of the incident.
  • Root cause (post-investigation).
  • Actions taken (containment, eradication, recovery).
  • Communication (internal, customer, regulator).
  • Lessons learned summary.
  • Actions arising (cross-reference to REG-CAR-001).
  • Cross-references (PLAN-IRP-001 record, PRO-024 record, AI-REG-RISK-001 entries, AISIA refresh).
  • Status (Open / Contained / Investigating / Recovered / Closed).

8. Coordination with Other Registers

  • REG-CAR-001 (Corrective Actions): actions arising from incidents are recorded for tracking to closure.
  • AI-REG-RISK-001: incidents may trigger updates to risk likelihood / impact ratings.
  • AI-REG-AISIA-001: incidents involving inadequately assessed impact trigger AISIA refresh.
  • PLAN-IRP-001 records (ISMS): security-dimension incidents reference both registers.
  • PRO-024 records: personal-data-related incidents reference both.

9. Reporting

  • AI incident metrics (count, severity distribution, time-to-stand-down) are reported to AIMS management review at least every six months.
  • Material incidents are reported promptly to the CEO and to management review out of cycle.
  • Trends across categories are reviewed annually for systemic improvement opportunities.

10. Maintenance

  • This Register is updated continuously as incidents arise.
  • This document structure is reviewed at least annually and after every Major or Critical incident.
