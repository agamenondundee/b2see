1. Purpose

This Policy defines how Cloudax Ltd. controls the use of removable storage media to protect the confidentiality and integrity of information.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.7.10 Storage media

2. Scope

This Policy applies to all removable storage media — USB flash drives, external hard drives and SSDs, memory cards, optical media, tapes, and other portable storage — used to store, transport, or transfer Cloudax information, by any personnel or authorised third party.

3. Definitions

  • Removable media: a portable storage device that can be inserted into and removed from a computer.
  • Sanitisation: the process of irreversibly removing data from media so it cannot be recovered.

4. Principles

  • Cloud-first: cloud storage and approved transfer channels (POL-005, POL-007) are the default for storing and transferring Cloudax information.
  • Removable media is used only by exception, where business need cannot be met through cloud services.
  • Removable media containing Cloudax information is encrypted.
  • Use is logged and tracked.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Information Security Manager: owns this Policy; approves use of removable media for Confidential or Restricted information.
  • Chief Technology Officer: approves use of removable media in production environments.
  • All personnel: comply with this Policy; report loss or unauthorised use.

6. Default Position: Use Cloud

Cloudax operates a cloud-first model. Storing or transferring Cloudax information using removable media is discouraged. Personnel should use approved cloud services or transfer channels in preference.

7. Approval to Use Removable Media

  • Internal use of removable media for Internal information requires no specific approval but follows this Policy.
  • Use of removable media for Confidential or Restricted information requires prior approval by the Information Security Manager.
  • Use in production environments requires CTO approval.

8. Encryption Requirements

  • All removable media used for Cloudax information must be encrypted in accordance with POL-023 Cryptographic Controls Policy.
  • Hardware-encrypted devices with PIN-based unlock are preferred.
  • Software-based encryption (e.g. BitLocker To Go, VeraCrypt) is acceptable where hardware encryption is not available.

9. Permitted Storage Media Types

  • Approved hardware-encrypted USB flash drives and external SSDs from approved suppliers.
  • Where alternative media is unavoidable (e.g. SD cards in cameras), encryption and access control measures are documented.
  • Optical media (CDs, DVDs) and tapes are not permitted for Cloudax information.

10. Logging and Tracking

  • Approved removable media is recorded in an inventory.
  • Issuance and return are logged.
  • Endpoint controls log device-insertion events and may block unapproved devices.

11. Transfer of Removable Media

  • Physical transfer of removable media is governed by POL-005 Information Transfer Policy.
  • Tracked courier or hand-delivery is used for Restricted information; postal services are not used.
  • Receipt is confirmed by the recipient.

12. Personal Removable Media

Personal removable media must not be used to store, transport, or transfer Cloudax information.

13. Disposal and Sanitisation

  • End-of-life removable media is sanitised in accordance with PRO-017 Information Deletion Procedure.
  • Approved methods include cryptographic erasure, secure overwrite (multiple passes), or physical destruction (shredding, incineration).
  • Where third-party disposal is used, certificates of destruction are obtained.

14. Lost or Stolen Media

  • Lost or stolen removable media must be reported immediately under PRO-027.
  • Where the media contained personal data, the Privacy Lead is engaged for breach assessment.

15. Compliance Monitoring

  • Endpoint protection monitors removable media use.
  • Inventory is reconciled at least annually.

16. Review and Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually and on material change.

17. Acknowledgement

All personnel are required to acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
