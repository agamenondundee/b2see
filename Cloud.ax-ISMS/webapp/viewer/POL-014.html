1. Purpose

This Policy defines how Cloudax Ltd. identifies, evaluates, and remediates technical vulnerabilities in its information systems and the Cloudax platform.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.8.8 Management of technical vulnerabilities

2. Scope

This Policy applies to all assets within the ISMS scope, including endpoints, servers, cloud infrastructure, container images, applications, source code, third-party libraries, and network components.

3. Definitions

  • Vulnerability: a weakness in an asset or control that can be exploited by a threat.
  • CVSS: the Common Vulnerability Scoring System used to assign severity.
  • CVE: a Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures identifier.
  • SLA: the service-level agreement defining remediation timeframes by severity.

4. Principles

  • Continuous identification: vulnerabilities are identified through scanning, intelligence feeds, and disclosure channels.
  • Risk-based prioritisation: severity is informed by CVSS, exposure, exploitability, and asset criticality.
  • Time-bound remediation: defined SLAs apply to remediation by severity.
  • Verification: remediation is verified by re-scanning or re-testing.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Information Security Manager: owns this Policy and the vulnerability management programme.
  • Chief Technology Officer: ensures remediation across the platform and supporting systems.
  • System and application owners: remediate vulnerabilities in systems they own.
  • All personnel: report observed weaknesses through PRO-027.

6. Asset Coverage and Identification

  • Vulnerability management covers assets recorded in REG-IAR-001 Asset Register.
  • New assets are added to the scanning programme on commissioning.

7. Vulnerability Sources and Intelligence

  • Vulnerability information is gathered from CVE feeds, vendor advisories, NCSC and ICO bulletins, threat intelligence feeds, and bug bounty / responsible disclosure reports.
  • Cloudax maintains contact with relevant authorities and special interest groups (REG-AUTH-001).

8. Vulnerability Scanning

  • Endpoints are scanned continuously.
  • Cloud infrastructure is scanned at least weekly.
  • Container images are scanned on build and on rebuild of the base image.
  • Source code and dependencies are scanned via SAST and SCA on every change (POL-024).
  • External-facing services are scanned at least monthly.

9. Penetration Testing

  • Independent penetration testing is performed on the Cloudax platform at least annually and after material change.
  • Findings are triaged and remediated within SLAs by severity.

10. Severity Classification

Vulnerabilities are classified using CVSS as a baseline, adjusted for exposure and exploitability:

  • Critical (CVSS 9.0–10.0).
  • High (CVSS 7.0–8.9).
  • Medium (CVSS 4.0–6.9).
  • Low (CVSS 0.1–3.9).

11. Remediation Timeframes (SLAs)

Default remediation SLAs (from confirmation of validity):

  • Critical: 7 calendar days.
  • High: 30 calendar days.
  • Medium: 90 calendar days.
  • Low: as part of normal maintenance.

Actively-exploited vulnerabilities are treated as Critical regardless of CVSS.

12. Patch Management

  • Operating systems and applications are patched on documented cadences.
  • Emergency patches follow expedited change management (POL-025).
  • Where patching introduces risk (compatibility, downtime), a documented risk decision is made.

13. Compensating Controls and Risk Acceptance

  • Where remediation cannot be achieved within SLA, compensating controls are implemented and documented.
  • Risk acceptance for vulnerabilities requires sign-off by an appropriately authorised role and is recorded in the Risk Register (REG-RISK-001).

14. Coordinated Disclosure and Researcher Reports

Cloudax maintains a security.txt and a published responsible disclosure channel. Reports from external researchers are triaged through the same vulnerability management process and acknowledged within the timeframes published on the disclosure page.

15. Reporting and Metrics

  • Open vulnerabilities, mean-time-to-remediate, and SLA compliance are reported to management review (PROG-COMP-001).
  • Significant vulnerabilities trigger ad-hoc reporting.

16. Review and Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually and on material change.

17. Acknowledgement

All personnel are required to acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
