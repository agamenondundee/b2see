1. Purpose

This Business Continuity Plan (the “Plan”) sets out how Cloudax Ltd. will maintain or rapidly resume the delivery of its services and the operation of its business during and after a disruption.

It supports the Information Security Policy (POL-001) and gives effect to A.5.29 Information security during disruption and A.5.30 ICT readiness for business continuity.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.5.29 Information security during disruption; A.5.30 ICT readiness for business continuity

2. Scope

This Plan applies to disruptions affecting the Cloudax conversational AI platform, supporting business operations, the Manchester shared workspace, key suppliers, and the Cloudax workforce. It is read together with the Disaster Recovery Plan (PLAN-DRP-001), which addresses the recovery of ICT services in detail.

3. Definitions

  • Disruption: an event causing significant deviation from normal operations.
  • Critical activity: an activity whose loss would, within a defined period, materially impair Cloudax’s ability to deliver its services or meet its obligations.
  • MTPD: Maximum Tolerable Period of Disruption — the longest period a critical activity can be unavailable before unacceptable harm occurs.
  • RTO: Recovery Time Objective — the target time within which an activity is to be resumed.
  • RPO: Recovery Point Objective — the maximum acceptable amount of data loss measured in time.

4. Assumptions and Dependencies

This Plan rests on the following assumptions:

  • Cloudax operates a cloud-native platform, hosted on established cloud service providers with multi-region capability and provider-native resilience features.
  • Cloudax personnel work hybrid; the Manchester shared workspace is a primary but not sole working location.
  • Critical SaaS services (identity, source code, communications, productivity) are independent of the Cloudax platform and may remain available even when the platform is disrupted.
  • The Cloudax team is small (six employees as at issue); workforce continuity is a material risk addressed in Section 12.
  • Suppliers and cloud providers maintain their own continuity arrangements; Cloudax’s controls focus on supplier monitoring (POL-006) and on diversification where feasible.

8. Continuity Strategies

Cloudax’s continuity strategies emphasise prevention through resilient architecture, paired with rapid response when disruption occurs:

  • Cloud-native, multi-region architecture for the Cloudax platform, with documented failover (PLAN-DRP-001).
  • Backups and immutable copies in line with POL-018 Backup Policy, tested annually.
  • Diverse SaaS providers for identity, communications, source code, and productivity; no single provider whose failure halts response.
  • Hybrid working as the default — reducing single-point dependence on the Manchester workspace.
  • Documented runbooks for the Cloudax platform, accessible to the on-call rota.
  • Cross-training across roles and documented ‘single point of knowledge’ reduction.
  • Supplier monitoring and tier-based assurance (POL-006).
  • Pre-agreed escalation paths and authority delegation (Section 5).

9. Activation and Escalation

This Plan may be activated by the Information Security Manager or, in their absence, the Chief Technology Officer or the Chief Executive Officer.

Activation is appropriate when:

  • A disruption is impacting, or imminently likely to impact, a critical activity beyond a few minutes.
  • A cyber-incident exceeds the in-shift response capability.
  • A workforce or facilities disruption affects the ability of multiple personnel to perform critical activities.
  • A regulatory or contractual notification is or may be required.

Activation triggers convening of the response team via established channels, opening of an incident record (in PLAN-IRP-001 where the disruption is security-related), and time-stamped event logging.

10. Communications During Disruption

  • Internal communications use a dedicated channel (Microsoft Teams or alternative), with a hold-channel established if the primary platform is affected.
  • All communications are led by the Incident Manager. Personnel must not communicate externally outside agreed channels and approved messaging.
  • External communications are approved by the Chief Executive Officer or designate.
  • A short, factual status template is used for both internal and external updates, refreshed at agreed cadence (typically hourly during a major incident).

11. Customer Notification

  • Customers affected by a disruption are notified in accordance with their contracts and any applicable Service Level Agreement (SLA).
  • Notifications are coordinated by the Incident Manager with input from customer-facing leads, the Privacy Lead, and the Chief Executive Officer.
  • Notifications follow a plain-language structure: what is known, what is being done, what next, when the next update will be provided.
  • Personal data breach notifications follow PRO-024 Breach Notification Procedure and POL-010 Privacy Policy.

12. Workforce Continuity

Cloudax operates with a small team, where the temporary loss of any one person is material. Mitigations include:

  • Documented runbooks and procedures, designed to be operable by any suitably qualified team member.
  • Cross-training and pair-working across the platform on-call rota.
  • Pre-arranged contractor and partner relationships that can supplement capacity at short notice.
  • Authority and access provisioning that does not depend on any single individual.
  • Up-to-date contact list for personnel and key external parties (REG-AUTH-001).

13. Supplier and Cloud Provider Disruption

  • Disruption to Tier 1 and Tier 2 suppliers triggers contact with the Cloudax supplier owner and engagement under POL-006.
  • Cloud provider status pages are monitored continuously; provider incidents that affect Cloudax services trigger this Plan and PLAN-DRP-001.
  • Where a supplier failure is sustained, the Information Security Manager and Chief Executive Officer evaluate the engagement of alternatives previously identified during onboarding assessment.

14. Stand-Down and Recovery

  • The Incident Manager declares stand-down once critical activities are restored and operating within tolerance.
  • Stand-down is communicated through the channels used during the incident.
  • Outstanding remediation, post-incident actions, and improvement opportunities are recorded in REG-CAR-001.

15. Post-Disruption Review

  • Every Plan activation is followed by a blameless post-incident review.
  • The review identifies what worked, what did not, contributing factors, and concrete improvements to the Plan or supporting controls.
  • Findings feed continual improvement (Cl. 10.1) and management review (Cl. 9.3).

16. Testing and Exercising

  • This Plan is exercised at least annually, including a tabletop exercise covering one or more disruption scenarios.
  • Disaster recovery technical tests are described in PLAN-DRP-001.
  • Exercise outcomes, including findings and improvement actions, are recorded and reported to management review.

17. Maintenance

This Plan is reviewed at least annually and on material change to the business, the platform, the workforce, or the supplier base. Material changes are approved by the Chief Executive Officer and recorded in Annex B.

18. Acknowledgement

All personnel are required to acknowledge this Plan on commencement, on material change, and on request.
