1. Purpose

This Policy defines how Cloudax Ltd. masks personal data and other sensitive information to reduce its exposure when used outside its primary processing context.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.8.11 Data masking

2. Scope

This Policy applies to all use of personal data and Confidential or Restricted information in non-production environments, support, demonstrations, training, analytics, and AI / ML training and evaluation.

3. Definitions

  • Masking: the process of obscuring sensitive information in data, while preserving its structure and utility for the intended purpose.
  • Pseudonymisation: the processing of personal data in such a way that it can no longer be attributed to a specific data subject without additional information held separately.
  • Anonymisation: the irreversible removal of identifying information so that data subjects cannot be re-identified.

4. Principles

  • Use the minimum data necessary.
  • Where personal or sensitive data is not required, mask, pseudonymise, anonymise, or use synthetic data.
  • Masked data is treated according to its residual sensitivity, not the sensitivity of the unmasked source.
  • Re-identification of pseudonymised data requires authorisation.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Chief Technology Officer: ensures masking is applied in development and test environments.
  • Privacy Lead: advises on masking adequacy in respect of personal data.
  • Information Security Manager: owns this Policy.

6. When Masking is Required

Masking, pseudonymisation, or anonymisation is required when:

  • Personal data is used in non-production environments.
  • Confidential or Restricted information is used in demonstrations or training material.
  • Data is shared with third parties for analysis or evaluation, except where the underlying contract permits unmasked use.
  • Data is used in AI / ML training or evaluation, except where the customer DPA permits use of unmasked data.

7. Masking Techniques

  • Substitution (replace values with realistic alternatives).
  • Redaction (remove or replace with placeholder).
  • Tokenisation (replace with non-sensitive token mapped to original value held separately).
  • Generalisation (reduce precision, e.g. age range instead of date of birth).
  • Hashing with appropriate salt for de-duplication while preventing reversal.

8. Pseudonymisation and Anonymisation

  • Pseudonymisation is used where reversibility may be required for legitimate purposes; the mapping is held with strict access controls.
  • Anonymisation requires assessment of re-identification risk, taking account of all reasonably available means.
  • Anonymised data is no longer personal data under the UK GDPR but should still be treated with care given residual disclosure risk.

9. Use of Production Data in Non-Production

  • Production personal data must not be used in non-production environments without masking, pseudonymisation, or anonymisation, or unless the customer contract explicitly permits unmasked use for the purpose.
  • Where unmasked production data is required for diagnostic purposes, it is used in time-bound, authorised access in production-equivalent environments, with logging and minimisation.

10. Synthetic Data

  • Synthetic data generation is preferred where realistic data is required without using real customer data.
  • Synthetic data must not inadvertently re-create real personal data; quality is assessed.

11. Masking in Customer Demonstrations and Support

  • Demonstrations use synthetic or anonymised data.
  • Support sessions involving customer data follow the customer’s authorisation; screen-shares of customer data are minimised.

12. Logging and Audit Considerations

  • Logs are masked or redacted where they would otherwise contain personal data or credentials, in line with POL-019 Logging Policy.

13. Compliance Monitoring

  • Compliance is monitored through code review, internal audit, and Privacy Lead oversight.

14. Review and Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually and on material change.

15. Acknowledgement

All personnel are required to acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
