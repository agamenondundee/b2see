1. Purpose

This Policy governs the data used in Cloudax’s AI systems. Data is the foundation on which AI behaviour rests; the quality, provenance, and lawful basis of data shape the quality, fairness, and lawfulness of AI outputs.

ISO 27001:2022 references: ISO 42001 reference: A.7.2 Data for development and enhancement of AI systems; A.7.3 Acquisition of data; A.7.4 Quality of data for AI systems; A.7.5 Data provenance; A.7.6 Data preparation; A.4.3 Data resources

2. Scope

This Policy applies to all data used in any AI system within the AIMS scope, including:

  • Training, evaluation, and fine-tuning datasets.
  • Customer voice and transcript data and data derived from it.
  • Reference data, embeddings, knowledge bases, and prompts.
  • Output data systems where it feeds back into AI development.

3. Definitions

  • Training data: data used to train, fine-tune, or otherwise improve an AI model.
  • Evaluation data: data used to assess AI system performance and behaviour.
  • Provenance: the documented history of data — source, collection method, transformations, lineage to the AI system.
  • Synthetic data: data generated artificially (including by AI) for use in development or evaluation.

4. Principles

  • Lawful basis first — data is used only on a documented lawful basis (consent, contract, legitimate interest, or other Article 6 basis where personal data is involved).
  • Minimisation — use only the data needed for the AI purpose; remove or pseudonymise where feasible.
  • Quality — data fitness for AI use is assessed before training or evaluation.
  • Provenance — every dataset is traceable from source to AI system.
  • Customer separation — customer data is used to serve the customer; broader use requires explicit customer permission.
  • Bias awareness — dataset composition is reviewed for representation and potential bias amplification.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Chief Technology Officer: owns this Policy; accountable for AI data discipline.
  • AI Governance Lead: contributes governance perspective; reviews material data decisions.
  • Privacy Lead: accountable for personal data aspects; coordinates with POL-010.
  • AI Builders: maintain dataset records; apply this Policy in their work.

6. Data for Development and Enhancement

  • Datasets used for training, fine-tuning, or evaluation are recorded in AI-REG-DATA-001.
  • Each dataset entry includes: source, lawful basis, classification (POL-004), provenance, preparation steps, intended use, and review date.
  • Datasets are never used for purposes outside their documented intended use without re-assessment.
  • Dataset selection considers diversity, representativeness, and potential for bias amplification.

7. Data Acquisition

Data is acquired through the following channels, each with its own controls:

  • Customer data — acquired through the Cloudax platform under customer contract; usage governed by the contract and Section 8.
  • Public datasets — acquired with attention to licence terms, source reliability, and potential biases.
  • Commercial data — acquired under contract; supplier assurance per POL-006 and AI-POL-009.
  • Synthetic data — generated under Section 14.
  • Personnel-generated data — e.g. internal evaluation data; treated as Cloudax confidential.

Data acquisition decisions are recorded with the lawful basis and any constraints.

8. Customer Voice and Transcript Data

Customer voice recordings, transcripts, and derived metadata are the most sensitive data Cloudax handles. The following constraints apply:

  • Customer data is processed under the customer’s contract and DPA. Cloudax acts as processor for this data.
  • Customer data is used to deliver the contracted service. Use for any other purpose — including training general-purpose models, improving Cloudax’s capabilities for other customers, or sharing with third parties — requires explicit customer permission expressed in contract.
  • Where the contract permits use beyond delivery (e.g. customer-specific model improvement), the use is limited to the customer’s own benefit and to the scope expressed in the contract.
  • Customer voice and transcript data is classified Restricted under POL-004.
  • Customer data sharing with third-party AI providers (LLMs, ASR/TTS) is governed by AI-POL-009 and the Cloudax customer DPA — sub-processor disclosures, data handling commitments, no-training commitments where required.

9. Data Quality

  • Data quality dimensions assessed: accuracy, completeness, consistency, timeliness, representativeness, validity.
  • Quality is assessed before use in training or evaluation; assessment is recorded with the dataset entry.
  • Quality issues identified post-deployment trigger investigation and may invalidate prior evaluations.
  • Quality assurance includes spot-checking of label correctness, sampling for bias indicators, and review of edge cases.

10. Data Provenance

  • Every dataset is traceable from source through preparation to AI system use.
  • Lineage is documented in AI-REG-DATA-001 with sufficient detail to support: reproduction, audit, customer enquiry, and incident investigation.
  • Where source data is acquired from third parties, the third-party’s assurance of provenance is recorded.
  • Where AI-generated data is used in further AI development, this is documented to avoid undetected feedback loops.

11. Data Preparation

  • Preparation steps — cleaning, filtering, labelling, augmentation, transformation — are documented per dataset.
  • Preparation that may introduce bias (e.g. filtering by language, geographic origin, or demographic indicator) is reviewed for that effect.
  • Labelling processes are documented; inter-annotator agreement is measured where labels are subjective.
  • Preparation pipelines are version-controlled (Infrastructure-as-Code where feasible).

12. Personal Data and Privacy

  • Personal data in AI datasets is processed under POL-010 Privacy Policy and recorded in REG-ROPA-001.
  • Pseudonymisation, anonymisation, or synthetic-data substitution is applied wherever the AI purpose can be achieved without identifying data.
  • Lawful basis is documented for each AI use of personal data.
  • Data subject rights apply; AISIA (AI-POL-004) and DPIA (POL-010) are coordinated for AI uses involving personal data.
  • Re-identification risk is assessed for pseudonymised AI datasets and mitigated where material.

13. Sensitive Data Categories

  • Special category data under UK GDPR Article 9 is used in AI development only where there is a clear lawful basis and condition under Article 9 — typically explicit consent or substantial public interest with safeguards.
  • Children’s data, biometric data, and data relating to vulnerable individuals receive enhanced protection per POL-010.
  • Sensitive data in customer voice/transcript data is handled with additional minimisation — e.g. redaction or scrubbing where feasible without compromising the AI purpose.

14. Synthetic Data

  • Synthetic data may be used for development and evaluation where it provides comparable utility to real data.
  • Synthetic data is documented per dataset including: generation method, source data (if any), validity assessment, and intended use.
  • Synthetic data does not eliminate bias; bias in source data may be reproduced or amplified in synthetic data.
  • Where synthetic data is , the generating AI system itself is in scope of the AIMS.

15. Data Lifecycle and Retention

  • Datasets have a defined retention period per POL-009 Records Retention Policy.
  • Data not in current use but retained for reproducibility is held with appropriate access controls.
  • Data is deleted or destroyed at end of retention per PRO-017 Information Deletion Procedure.
  • Customer data deletion at end of customer contract follows the customer DPA and PRO-017.

16. Coordination with the ISMS and Privacy

AI data discipline inherits security from the ISMS (POL-002 Access Control, POL-004 Information Classification, POL-018 Backup, POL-023 Cryptographic Controls) and privacy from POL-010 Privacy Policy. This Policy adds AI-specific data concerns; it does not replace the security or privacy policies.

17. Records

  • AI-REG-DATA-001 holds the master AI Data and Models Register.
  • Personal data processing for AI is recorded in REG-ROPA-001.
  • AISIA records reference the datasets they assess.
  • Records are retained per POL-009.

18. Compliance Monitoring

  • Compliance is monitored under AI-PROG-COMP-001 and audited under AI-PROG-AUDIT-001.
  • Auditors will typically sample datasets in current use and request the corresponding AI-REG-DATA-001 entries and provenance evidence.

19. Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually and on material change to AI data practice, customer contractual obligations, or regulatory expectations.

20. Acknowledgement

AI Builders, the CTO, the AI Governance Lead, and the Privacy Lead acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
