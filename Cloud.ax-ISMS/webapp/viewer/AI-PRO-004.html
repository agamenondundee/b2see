1. Purpose

This Procedure operationalises the AI Incident Response Plan (AI-PLAN-IRP-001). It defines the practical steps for handling an AI incident from detection to closure.

ISO 27001:2022 references: ISO 42001 reference: A.6.2.6 AI system operation and monitoring; A.8.4 Communication of incidents; A.10.4 Customers

2. Scope

This Procedure applies to AI incidents affecting any AI system within the AIMS scope.

3. Definitions

Definitions are per AI-PLAN-IRP-001 §3.

4. Roles and Responsibilities

  • AI Incident Manager (AI Governance Lead by default): coordinates response, escalation, decisions, communication.
  • Chief Technology Officer: leads technical investigation and recovery.
  • Privacy Lead: assesses personal data implications; coordinates with PRO-024.
  • Customer-facing leads: coordinate customer communication.
  • On-call engineer(s): execute containment and recovery; report progress.
  • Chief Executive Officer: receives major incident escalations; authorises material decisions.

5. The Incident Lifecycle

AI incidents are handled in 10 stages. Stages 1–3 are time-critical; stages 4–7 are managed in parallel where appropriate; stages 8–10 follow stand-down.

6. Stage 1 — Detection and Initial Report

  • Detection sources include: production monitoring alerts, customer reports, end-user complaints, personnel reports under PRO-027, external signals.
  • Initial report is captured in the on-call channel with: what was observed, when, by whom, which AI system, what is currently impacted.
  • On-call confirms detection within 1 business hour during business hours; immediate response out of hours for material indicators.

7. Stage 2 — Triage and Classification

  • AI Incident Manager (or on-call) confirms incident scope and category (per AI-PLAN-IRP-001 §4).
  • Severity is assessed (per AI-PLAN-IRP-001 §8): Critical / Major / Moderate / Minor.
  • Determine whether the incident is also a security incident (invokes PLAN-IRP-001), a personal data breach (invokes PRO-024), both, or neither.
  • Open an AI incident record in AI-REG-INC-001 with reference (AI-INC-NNN).
  • Activate the appropriate response team based on severity.

8. Stage 3 — Containment

  • Where the AI system is producing harmful output, containment options include: disabling the feature, falling back to a safe default, increasing human review thresholds, removing the affected system from production.
  • Containment decisions are taken by the AI Incident Manager and CTO.
  • For Critical and Major incidents the CEO is informed and may direct.
  • Containment actions are recorded with timestamps.
  • Where containment requires customer notification (e.g. taking a feature offline), customer-facing leads coordinate.

9. Stage 4 — Investigation

  • Investigation determines root cause and contributing factors.
  • Sources reviewed include: model behaviour, input data, monitoring signals, change history, third-party provider behaviour, configuration changes.
  • Investigation respects the integrity of evidence; logs and signals are preserved without alteration.
  • Investigation duration is proportionate to severity — hours for Major, longer for complex Critical incidents.

10. Stage 5 — Eradication

  • Eradication addresses the root cause: model retraining, data correction, prompt redesign, monitoring improvements, provider change, process change, or combinations.
  • Eradication is verified before recovery to production.
  • Where eradication requires a substantial change, AI-PRO-005 (AI change management) applies and the AI System Lifecycle Procedure (AI-PRO-001) re-engages at the appropriate stage.

11. Stage 6 — Recovery

  • Recovery to production is staged where feasible — limited rollout, monitoring, broader rollout.
  • Customer-facing communication confirms recovery and any residual mitigations.
  • Recovery is verified by automated checks, sample inspection, and customer impact monitoring.

12. Stage 7 — Communication

  • Communication discipline follows AI-PLAN-IRP-001 §10 — internal, customer, regulator, public.
  • All communications are coordinated by the AI Incident Manager.
  • ICO notification is led by the Privacy Lead under PRO-024 if applicable.
  • Material customer-facing or public communications are approved by the CEO.

13. Stage 8 — Stand-Down

  • Stand-down is declared by the AI Incident Manager once recovery is verified.
  • Stand-down is communicated through the channels used during the incident.
  • Outstanding remediation, post-incident actions, and improvement opportunities are recorded in REG-CAR-001.

14. Stage 9 — Post-Incident Review

  • Every Major and Critical incident is followed by a blameless post-incident review.
  • Review covers: timeline, contributing factors, what worked, what did not, lessons learned, improvements to controls and procedures.
  • Review is held within 10 business days of stand-down for Major incidents; within 5 for Critical.
  • Review attendees include the AI Incident Manager, CTO, Privacy Lead (where applicable), CEO (Critical only), and on-call engineers involved.

15. Stage 10 — Lessons Learned and Closure

  • Lessons feed: continual improvement (Cl. 10.1), AISIA refresh under AI-PRO-002, AI Risk Register update, policy / procedure revision.
  • Improvement actions are recorded in REG-CAR-001.
  • Closure of the AI incident record requires verification that all actions are tracked and that affected parties have been informed.
  • Material lessons are summarised at AIMS management review.

16. Coordination with PLAN-IRP-001 and PRO-024

  • Where the incident has both AI and security dimensions, this Procedure and PLAN-IRP-001 (ISMS) operate in parallel under a single Incident Manager.
  • Where the incident involves personal data, PRO-024 Breach Notification Procedure applies in addition. The Privacy Lead coordinates the regulatory notification path; the AI Incident Manager coordinates the technical and AI-specific response.
  • A single incident record cross-references all applicable registers.

17. Records

  • AI incidents are recorded in AI-REG-INC-001.
  • Records include: detection, triage, classification, containment, investigation, eradication, recovery, communication, lessons learned.
  • Records are retained per POL-009 — minimum 6 years from closure.

18. Compliance Monitoring

  • AI incident handling is monitored under AI-PROG-COMP-001.
  • Time-to-detect, time-to-triage, time-to-stand-down metrics are tracked.

19. Maintenance

This Procedure is reviewed at least annually and after every Major or Critical incident.

20. Acknowledgement

Personnel involved in incident response acknowledge this Procedure on commencement, on material change, and on request.
