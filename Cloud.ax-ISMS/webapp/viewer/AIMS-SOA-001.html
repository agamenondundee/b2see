1. Purpose

This AI Statement of Applicability (SoA) records the applicability of each control in Annex A of ISO/IEC 42001:2023 to Cloudax Ltd.'s AI Management System (AIMS), the justification for inclusion or exclusion of each control, and the document(s) that implement applicable controls.

This SoA is a mandatory output of the AI risk treatment process under Clause 6.1.3 of ISO/IEC 42001:2023 and is the authoritative reference for determining which AIMS controls Cloudax operates.

ISO 27001:2022 references: ISO 42001 reference: Clause 6.1.3 AI risk treatment

2. Scope

This SoA covers all AI systems developed, operated, or used by Cloudax within the AIMS scope as defined in AIMS-SCOPE-001. The scope includes the Cloudax conversational AI platform (RentSense, RepairSense, and any other AI features), AI capabilities provided to customers, and AI tools used internally by Cloudax personnel.

3. Definitions

  • AI system: a system that infers, from the input it receives, how to generate outputs such as predictions, content, recommendations, or decisions that can influence physical or virtual environments (ISO/IEC 22989:2022).
  • Applicability: the determination that a control is required, given the AI risks and the context of Cloudax's AI systems.
  • AISIA: AI System Impact Assessment — the structured assessment of how an AI system may affect individuals, groups, and society.

4. Methodology

Each Annex A control was evaluated against:

  • The AI risks identified in the AI Risk Register (AI-REG-RISK-001) under the methodology in AIMS-RA-001.
  • Cloudax's role in the AI lifecycle (developer, operator, supplier to customers; see Section 6).
  • Applicable legal, regulatory, contractual, and ethical obligations.
  • The size and operating model of Cloudax (six employees, hybrid working, fully cloud-native).

A control is marked applicable if it addresses an identified risk or obligation, regardless of whether the risk is high or low. Controls are excluded only where they are clearly not relevant to Cloudax's role or operating context, with reasoned justification.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • AI Governance Lead: owns this SoA; reviews entries; submits for approval.
  • Chief Technology Officer: contributes technical input on lifecycle, data, and platform controls.
  • Privacy Lead: contributes input on impact assessment, data, and transparency controls.
  • Chief Executive Officer: approves this SoA on issue and on material revision.
  • Internal auditor: reviews SoA against operational reality during the audit programme (AI-PROG-AUDIT-001).

6. Cloudax Position on the AI Lifecycle

Cloudax operates across multiple roles in the AI lifecycle, all of which inform the applicability assessment:

ROLE

DESCRIPTION

KEY IMPLICATIONS

AI Developer

Cloudax designs, develops, trains, and validates the AI components of its conversational AI platform.

Full applicability of A.6 (life cycle) and A.7 (data) controls.

AI Operator

Cloudax operates the AI systems in production on its own infrastructure (with cloud and third-party providers).

Full applicability of A.6.2.6 (operation and monitoring), A.8 (transparency to interested parties).

AI User (internal)

Cloudax personnel use third-party AI tools (e.g. coding assistants, document assistants) for internal work.

Applicability of A.9 (use) controls extends to internal AI use.

AI Supplier (to customers)

Cloudax provides AI capabilities to its customers under contract; customers integrate Cloudax outputs into their operations.

Full applicability of A.10 (third-party and customer relationships).

AI Customer (of providers)

Cloudax consumes AI capabilities (e.g. LLMs, ASR, TTS) from third-party providers as part of its platform.

Cloudax is a customer of upstream AI providers; A.10.3 applies.

Because Cloudax operates across all five lifecycle positions, all 9 control objectives in Annex A are applicable. Within each objective, every control is assessed individually below.

7. The Statement

The full Annex A applicability assessment follows. Status “Yes” means the control is applicable and implemented; “No” means excluded (with justification); “Yes (planned)” means applicable but implementation in progress.
