1. Purpose

This Register is the authoritative inventory of every AI system within the Cloudax AIMS scope. It supports A.4.2 (Resource documentation) and provides the anchor that other AI registers (data, AISIA, providers, incidents) point back to.

ISO 27001:2022 references: ISO 42001 reference: A.4.2 Resource documentation; A.4.4 Tooling resources; A.4.5 System and computing resources

2. Scope

This Register covers all AI systems within the AIMS scope per AIMS-SCOPE-001 — the Cloudax conversational AI platform components, third-party AI services consumed by the platform, and AI tools used internally by Cloudax personnel where they carry organisational risk.

3. Definitions

  • AI system: a system that infers, from inputs, outputs that influence environments (per ISO/IEC 22989:2022).
  • AI component: an identifiable part of an AI system that has independent governance attention (e.g. ASR component, LLM-mediated summarisation component).

4. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Chief Technology Officer: owns this Register; ensures completeness.
  • AI Governance Lead: reviews entries for governance completeness; uses the Register as the entry point to AISIA, data, provider, and incident records.
  • AI Builders: maintain entries for the AI systems they own.

5. How to Use This Register

  • Each AI system has a unique identifier (AI-SYS-NNN).
  • Entries persist across the lifecycle — status reflects current stage.
  • This document holds a representative set of worked examples; the live register is maintained as a structured store (Excel, Notion, or equivalent) with the same column structure.
  • Cross-references to AI-REG-AISIA-001, AI-REG-DATA-001, AI-REG-3P-001, and AI-REG-INC-001 use the AI-SYS-NNN identifier.

6. Inventory Schema

Each AI system entry contains:

  • Identifier (AI-SYS-NNN).
  • Name and brief description.
  • Lifecycle stage (Concept / Development / V&V / Deployed / Decommissioning / Retired).
  • Owner (named individual).
  • Lifecycle role (Developer / Operator / User / Supplier / Customer of providers, per AIMS-SCOPE-001 §5).
  • Customer-facing (Yes / No / Internal-only).
  • Personal data involved (Yes / No / Pseudonymised).
  • Latest AISIA reference (AISIA-NNN) and date.
  • Risk rating (overall, derived from AISIA + AI Risk Register).
  • Third-party providers involved (cross-reference to AI-REG-3P-001).
  • Datasets involved (cross-reference to AI-REG-DATA-001).
  • Monitoring summary (link to dashboards).
  • Review date.

8. Relationships with Other Registers

RELATED REGISTER

RELATIONSHIP

AI-REG-AISIA-001 — Impact Assessments

Each AI system has at least one AISIA; AISIA records reference AI-SYS-NNN.

AI-REG-DATA-001 — Data and Models

Each AI system has associated datasets and models recorded in AI-REG-DATA-001 with cross-reference.

AI-REG-3P-001 — Third-Party Providers

Each AI system’s third-party AI providers are recorded; cross-reference.

AI-REG-INC-001 — AI Incidents

Incidents reference the affected AI system(s).

AI-REG-RISK-001 — AI Risk Register

Risks reference affected AI system(s) where system-specific.

REG-IAR-001 — Information Asset Register (ISMS)

AI systems that are also Cloudax information assets are cross-referenced.

REG-ROPA-001 — Record of Processing Activities

AI systems processing personal data are reflected in ROPA entries.

9. Review Cadence

  • Each entry has a review date based on risk rating: High systems quarterly, Moderate annually, Low annually.
  • Out-of-cycle review on material change to the AI system or following an incident.
  • Full register review at least annually for completeness.

10. Maintenance

  • This Register is updated continuously as AI systems are added, change stage, or are decommissioned.
  • This document structure is reviewed at least annually.
