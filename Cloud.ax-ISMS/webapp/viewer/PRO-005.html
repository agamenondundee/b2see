1. Purpose

This Procedure defines how Cloudax Ltd. manages the information security aspects of the joiner, mover, and leaver (JML) lifecycle for personnel and contractors. It implements the requirements of POL-002 Access Control Policy (§16) and supports the Information Security Policy (POL-001).

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.5.11 Return of assets; A.5.18 Access rights; A.6.5 Responsibilities after termination or change of employment

2. Scope

This Procedure applies to all personnel engaged by Cloudax in any capacity — employees, contractors, consultants, interns, apprentices, and agency workers — and to third parties granted access to Cloudax information or systems.

3. Definitions

  • Joiner: an individual commencing engagement with Cloudax.
  • Mover: an individual whose role, team, or access requirements change during their engagement.
  • Leaver: an individual whose engagement with Cloudax ends.

4. Roles and Responsibilities

  • HR / People function: initiates JML events; maintains the master record of personnel.
  • Line manager: triggers and approves access changes; ensures handover.
  • Information Security Manager: owns this Procedure; spot-checks evidence; addresses exceptions.
  • Chief Technology Officer: implements technical access changes for production environments.
  • IT support: provisions and de-provisions accounts and devices.
  • Joiner / mover / leaver: cooperates with the Procedure; signs acknowledgements; returns assets.

5. Joiner Process

5.1 Pre-Joining

  • HR confirms identity and right to work; verifies references where required by role.
  • HR records the joiner in the master personnel record.
  • Line manager identifies the role profile and the systems and access required.
  • IT support provisions account in the corporate identity provider, prepares laptop and any required devices.
  • Account is created in a disabled state until first day.

5.2 First Day

  • Joiner signs employment / engagement agreement including confidentiality and IP assignment clauses.
  • Joiner acknowledges receipt and reading of POL-001, POL-003, and other policies relevant to role.
  • Joiner enrolls MFA on the corporate identity provider.
  • Account is enabled with baseline access — communications, productivity, internal documentation.
  • Laptop is issued; full-disk encryption, MDM, EDR, and managed software are confirmed in place.
  • Joiner is briefed on workplace expectations — the Manchester shared workspace, hybrid working, clean-desk.

5.3 First Week

  • Joiner completes induction training under PROG-TRAIN-001, including information security awareness, privacy basics, phishing recognition, and event reporting.
  • Role-specific access is provisioned through the access management workflow, approved by line manager and system owner.
  • For privileged or production access, additional approvals are obtained per POL-002 §9.
  • Line manager assigns a buddy or onboarding partner.

5.4 First 90 Days

  • Joiner completes any remaining role-specific training within 30 days.
  • Line manager reviews joiner’s actual access against intended access at 30 days.
  • Probation review at 90 days includes confirmation that all training is complete.

6. Mover Process

When a person changes role, team, or material responsibility:

  • Line manager (current or new) initiates the mover workflow with HR and IT support.
  • Access required for the new role is identified; access no longer required is identified for removal.
  • New access is provisioned through standard access management with appropriate approvals.
  • Old access is removed within 5 business days unless required for a documented handover.
  • Where the change involves elevated privileges (e.g. promotion to engineering on-call), additional onboarding to privileged-access expectations is delivered.
  • Where the change involves customer-facing responsibilities, customer data handling and breach response responsibilities are briefed.

7. Leaver Process

7.1 Notice Received

  • HR records the leaver date and notifies line manager, Information Security Manager, IT support, and CTO (if production access).
  • Line manager plans handover; ensures critical knowledge is documented.
  • Outstanding actions, accesses, and assets in the leaver’s name are identified.

7.2 Final Day

  • By end of working day on the leaver’s last day, the following are completed:
    • — Account is disabled in the corporate identity provider.
    • — Active sessions are terminated; refresh tokens are invalidated.
    • — MFA tokens are de-registered.
    • — SaaS application access is revoked or downgraded as appropriate.
    • — Source code repository, CI/CD, and production access is revoked.
    • — VPN / zero-trust access is revoked.
    • — Building / workspace access is revoked.
    • — Cloudax-issued laptop, mobile device, and any peripherals are returned and confirmed received.
    • — Returned device is reset and re-imaged before reissue.
    • — Personal data on devices is identified and handled per POL-009 / POL-010.
  • Line manager confirms completion.

7.3 Post-Departure

  • Mailbox is retained for a defined period (typically 30–90 days) under licence and access control, with delegated access to line manager only where required.
  • Files in personal storage are reviewed for business records that should be retained per POL-009.
  • Final pay and any contractual matters are processed by HR.
  • ISMS records (training records, access history) are retained per POL-009.
  • Reminders of post-employment confidentiality obligations are issued in writing by HR.
  • Spot-check by Information Security Manager confirms all access has been revoked.

8. Special Cases

8.1 Summary Termination

  • Where summary termination occurs (e.g. gross misconduct), access revocation occurs immediately, before or in parallel with the termination meeting.
  • Information Security Manager is notified urgently.
  • Logging review is performed for the period preceding termination if there is reason to believe data exfiltration may have occurred.
  • HR and Information Security Manager coordinate communications.

8.2 Long-Term Absence

  • Where a person is on long-term leave (sickness, parental, sabbatical), access is reviewed.
  • Privileged access and production access are typically suspended for the duration.
  • Suspension is reversed on return to work via the mover process.

8.3 Contractor Engagement End

  • End of contract is the leaver process.
  • Contractor agreement may include extended confidentiality post-engagement.
  • Where the contractor used their own device under approved BYOD, Cloudax data is removed and confirmed (typically through MDM selective wipe).

8.4 Mutually Agreed Departure

  • Standard leaver process applies.
  • Where settlement terms allow extended access (e.g. for handover), this is documented and time-bound.

9. Records and Evidence

  • HR maintains the master personnel record.
  • Information Security Manager maintains the JML log, recording each event with date, person, type (joiner/mover/leaver), and confirmation of completion of the steps in this Procedure.
  • Records are retained per POL-009 Records Retention Policy.

10. Compliance and Audit

  • This Procedure is sampled at internal audit (PROG-AUDIT-001) and during compliance monitoring (PROG-COMP-001).
  • The auditor will typically request a sample joiner and a sample leaver and ask for evidence the steps were completed.
  • Findings are recorded in REG-CAR-001.

11. Maintenance

This Procedure is reviewed at least annually and on material change to the Cloudax operating model. Material changes are approved by the Information Security Manager and recorded in Annex B.

12. Acknowledgement

All personnel involved in JML activity (HR, line managers, IT support, ISM, CTO) are required to acknowledge this Procedure on commencement, on material change, and on request.
