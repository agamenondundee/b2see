1. Purpose

This Compliance Monitoring Schedule defines how Cloudax Ltd. monitors compliance with its AI policies, procedures, controls, and applicable legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations relating to AI.

ISO 27001:2022 references: ISO 42001 reference: Clause 9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation; A.6.2.6 AI system operation and monitoring

2. Scope

This Schedule applies to all AI systems, processes, and personnel within the AIMS scope.

3. Definitions

  • Compliance: the state of conforming to applicable AI obligations.
  • Monitoring: the systematic examination of AI activities to assess conformity and effectiveness.
  • Finding: an observation arising from monitoring that requires recording, classification, and where appropriate corrective action.

4. Principles

  • Risk-based: monitoring focuses on the highest-risk AI systems and on obligations with the most material consequences.
  • Continuous where possible: production AI behaviour is monitored continuously; periodic review supplements.
  • Proportionate: monitoring is calibrated to Cloudax’s size.
  • Independent where it matters: high-stakes monitoring is performed by someone other than the activity owner.
  • Action-oriented: findings drive corrective action, not just reporting.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • AI Governance Lead: owns this Schedule; coordinates AI monitoring; consolidates findings; reports to AIMS management review.
  • Chief Technology Officer: owns monitoring of AI system technical performance and behaviour.
  • Privacy Lead: owns monitoring of AI privacy and data subject impact.
  • Process owners: cooperate with monitoring of processes within their domain.
  • Internal auditor: conducts independent compliance audits as part of AI-PROG-AUDIT-001.

6. Compliance Domains Covered

  • ISO/IEC 42001:2023 conformance, including the controls in AIMS-SOA-001 v1.0.
  • UK GDPR and DPA 2018 in respect of AI processing.
  • Future UK and EU AI regulation as it comes into effect (e.g. EU AI Act high-risk system obligations where applicable).
  • Customer contractual AI obligations.
  • Cloudax internal AI policies and procedures.

7. Monitoring Methods

  • Automated technical monitoring: AI accuracy, drift, fairness indicators, monitoring of third-party provider behaviour, output filtering alerts.
  • Sample-based review: sampled AISIA records, AI training records, AI incident records, model evaluation outputs.
  • Walkthrough: guided demonstration that an AI procedure operates as documented.
  • Internal audit: structured assessment under AI-PROG-AUDIT-001.
  • Management review: regular review of AIMS performance against AI objectives, risk, and incidents.

9. Recording Findings

  • Findings are recorded with date, source, description, classification, owner, and target closure date.
  • Findings are entered in REG-CAR-001 Corrective Actions Register where corrective action is required.
  • Where the finding indicates a material AI risk, AI-REG-RISK-001 is updated.
  • Where the finding indicates a personal data breach, PRO-024 applies; where it is also an AI incident, AI-PLAN-IRP-001 applies.

10. Escalation and Corrective Action

  • Findings classified as material are escalated to the AI Governance Lead within one business day.
  • Critical findings (potential breach, active harm, regulator interest) are escalated to the Chief Executive Officer immediately.
  • Corrective actions are tracked to closure under AI-PRO-031 (cross-reference to PRO-031) and REG-CAR-001.

11. Reporting

  • A consolidated AI compliance summary is reported to AIMS management review at least every six months.
  • Significant findings are reported promptly between cycles.
  • Trends are tracked over time as a leading indicator of AIMS health.

12. Maintenance

This Schedule is reviewed at least annually and on material change.

13. Acknowledgement

Personnel involved in compliance monitoring are required to acknowledge this Schedule on commencement, on material change, and on request.
