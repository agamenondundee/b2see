1. Purpose

This Compliance Obligations Register identifies the legal, statutory, regulatory, and contractual requirements applicable to Cloudax Ltd. and the approach taken to meet them. It supports POL-001 Information Security Policy and gives effect to A.5.31 Legal, statutory, regulatory and contractual requirements.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.5.31 Legal, statutory, regulatory and contractual requirements

2. Scope

This Register covers all compliance obligations relevant to information security, data protection, and the operation of the Cloudax conversational AI platform within the United Kingdom and any other jurisdictions in which Cloudax operates or processes data.

3. Definitions

  • Obligation: a duty arising from law, regulation, contract, or voluntary commitment.
  • Owner: the named role with primary accountability for ensuring the obligation is met.

4. Categories of Obligation

  • Statutory — UK primary and secondary legislation (e.g. UK GDPR, DPA 2018).
  • Regulatory — obligations imposed by regulators (e.g. ICO codes of practice).
  • Sector / industry — voluntary or industry-mandated commitments.
  • Standards — obligations arising from certifications and frameworks adopted (e.g. ISO/IEC 27001).
  • Contractual — obligations arising from customer and supplier contracts.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Information Security Manager: owns this Register; maintains entries; coordinates compliance monitoring.
  • Privacy Lead: owns data protection obligations and customer DPA requirements.
  • Chief Executive Officer: ultimate accountability for legal compliance.
  • Customer-facing leads: identify customer contractual obligations and ensure their fulfilment.

6. How to Use This Register

  • Each obligation has a unique identifier (COMP-NNN) and is mapped to the policies and procedures that give effect to it.
  • Obligations are reviewed at least annually and on material change to law, regulation, or contracts.
  • Where new legislation or guidance is issued, the Information Security Manager assesses applicability and updates this Register within 60 days.

8. Sector and Industry Obligations

Cloudax is a developer of conversational AI; sector-specific obligations may arise where customers operate in regulated sectors. The principal categories include:

  • Financial services customers — FCA / PRA outsourcing and operational resilience requirements may flow down through contract.
  • Health and care customers — NHS Data Security and Protection Toolkit (DSPT) requirements may flow down.
  • Public sector customers — Cyber Essentials / Cyber Essentials Plus, Crown Commercial Service framework requirements may apply.

Sector obligations are tracked through customer contracts (Section 10) rather than as separate entries unless universal.

10. Customer Contractual Obligations

Each customer contract carries specific information security and privacy obligations. Each customer engagement is recorded in REG-ROPA-001 (Article 30(2) section) with reference to its DPA. The principal recurring obligation themes are:

  • Article 28 UK GDPR clauses (where personal data is processed).
  • Security incident notification timeframes (typically within 24 or 48 hours of awareness).
  • Sub-processor change notification requirements.
  • International transfer safeguards (IDTA / SCCs).
  • Data residency commitments.
  • Audit and assurance rights.
  • Service level commitments and reporting.
  • Customer-specific assurance obligations (e.g. ISO certification, SOC 2 reporting, customer security questionnaires).

Customer-facing leads track contract-specific obligations in the customer record; this Register holds the universal themes.

11. Supplier Contractual Obligations

Cloudax’s supplier contracts include security and privacy obligations flowed down to suppliers in line with POL-006. Tier 1 and Tier 2 supplier contracts are tracked in the Cloudax Supplier Inventory; this Register holds the universal themes:

  • Confidentiality and NDA obligations.
  • Article 28 UK GDPR processor clauses (where the supplier processes personal data).
  • Security incident notification.
  • Sub-processing controls.
  • Audit / assurance reporting rights.
  • Return or destruction of Cloudax data on termination.

12. Compliance Monitoring and Evidence

  • Monitoring of ongoing compliance is performed under PROG-COMP-001 Compliance Monitoring Schedule.
  • Findings of non-compliance are recorded in REG-CAR-001 and tracked to closure.
  • This Register, in conjunction with the supporting evidence referenced in each entry, demonstrates continued conformity to A.5.31.

13. Maintenance

  • This Register is reviewed at least annually by the Information Security Manager and the Privacy Lead.
  • Material changes (new legislation, new regulatory guidance, new sector requirements arising from customer engagements) are added within 60 days.
  • Significant changes are reported to management review.
