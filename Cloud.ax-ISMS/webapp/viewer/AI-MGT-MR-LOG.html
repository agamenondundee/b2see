1. Purpose

This Log records AIMS management reviews held under Clause 9.3 of ISO/IEC 42001:2023. It is the audit trail of top management’s engagement with the AIMS — confirming continuing suitability, adequacy, effectiveness, and alignment with strategic direction.

ISO 27001:2022 references: ISO 42001 reference: Cl. 9.3 Management review; Cl. 10.1 Continual improvement

2. Scope

This Log covers all AIMS management reviews. The first entry is the issue of the AIMS itself; subsequent entries record each review.

3. Definitions

  • Management review: the structured review of the AIMS by top management to confirm suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness.

4. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Chief Executive Officer: chairs the review; takes decisions on continuing suitability and resource.
  • AI Governance Lead: prepares the inputs; produces the agenda; records the outputs.
  • Chief Technology Officer: contributes operational AI status.
  • Privacy Lead: contributes privacy-dimension AI status.
  • Other invited contributors: as appropriate to the agenda (e.g. customer-facing leads, internal auditor).

5. Review Frequency and Format

  • Full management review at least annually.
  • Interim reviews at least every six months focused on AIMS performance.
  • Out-of-cycle review on material AIMS change, material AI incident, or significant AI regulatory change.
  • Reviews are held in person or via video conference; minutes are recorded; the Log entry summarises.

6. Required Inputs

Each review considers, at minimum, the inputs required by Clause 9.3.2 of ISO/IEC 42001:2023:

  • Status of actions from previous reviews.
  • Changes in external and internal issues affecting the AIMS.
  • Information on AIMS performance, including: nonconformities and corrective actions; monitoring and measurement results; audit results; AISIA outputs; AI incidents.
  • Feedback from interested parties (customers, end-users, regulators).
  • Status of AI risks (AI-REG-RISK-001) and risk treatment (AIMS-RTP-001).
  • Opportunities for continual improvement.

7. Required Outputs

Each review produces, at minimum, decisions on:

  • Continuing suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the AIMS.
  • Continual improvement opportunities and the actions to take them up.
  • Any need for changes to the AIMS, including resource needs.
  • Risk acceptance for Critical residual AI risks.

8. Standing Agenda

The standing agenda for AIMS management reviews:

  • (1) Review of actions from previous review.
  • (2) Changes in context (regulatory, threat, customer, internal).
  • (3) Status of AIMS objectives (AIMS-OBJ-001).
  • (4) AI risk overview (AI-REG-RISK-001 — High and Critical risks).
  • (5) AISIA pipeline status.
  • (6) AI incidents in period.
  • (7) Compliance monitoring summary (AI-PROG-COMP-001).
  • (8) Audit findings status (AI-PROG-AUDIT-001 + REG-CAR-001).
  • (9) Third-party AI provider status (AI-REG-3P-001 Tier 1).
  • (10) Customer feedback and customer-facing communications.
  • (11) AIMS resource adequacy.
  • (12) Continual improvement opportunities.
  • (13) Decisions and actions for next period.

10. Action Tracking

  • Actions arising from reviews are recorded in REG-CAR-001 (shared with the ISMS) for tracking to closure.
  • Open actions from prior reviews are reviewed at the next review per the standing agenda.
  • Material slippage is escalated to the CEO between reviews.

11. Coordination with the ISMS Management Review

  • AIMS and ISMS management reviews may be conducted in a single combined session for efficiency.
  • Where conducted together, this Log and MGT-MR-LOG (ISMS) both record the relevant agenda items.
  • Cross-cutting items (e.g. shared resource adequacy, shared incident response performance) are recorded once with cross-reference.

12. Records

  • This Log entry is the summary of the review.
  • Detailed minutes, supporting papers, and presentations are held with the entry.
  • Records are retained per POL-009.

13. Maintenance

  • This Log is updated after every review.
  • This document structure is reviewed at least annually.
