1. Purpose

This Supplier Security Policy defines how Cloudax Ltd. manages information security risk in relationships with suppliers, partners, and other third parties, including outsourced development.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.5.19, A.5.20, A.5.21, A.8.30

2. Scope

This Policy applies to all suppliers and third parties that process, store, transmit, or have access to Cloudax information; provide infrastructure, platforms, or applications used by Cloudax; develop or operate components of the Cloudax platform; have logical or physical access to Cloudax facilities, systems, or accounts; or whose personnel may be exposed to Cloudax information.

3. Definitions

  • Supplier: any third party that provides goods or services to Cloudax under a contractual arrangement.
  • Sub-processor: a processor engaged by Cloudax to process personal data on behalf of customers.
  • Tier: the categorisation of a supplier based on the criticality of the service and sensitivity of information involved.

4. Principles

  • Risk-based: supplier security activity is proportionate to the risk presented.
  • Lifecycle-wide: supplier security is addressed at selection, onboarding, in-life, and termination.
  • Contractual: information security obligations are flowed down through written contracts.
  • Verifiable: supplier security claims are verified through evidence proportionate to tier.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Information Security Manager: owns this Policy; approves engagements at Tier 1 and Tier 2.
  • Privacy Lead: approves engagements involving personal data; ensures data processing agreements are in place (POL-010).
  • Chief Technology Officer: identifies technical security requirements for cloud and platform suppliers.
  • Supplier owners: named individuals responsible for day-to-day relationship management.

6. Supplier Inventory and Tiering

Cloudax maintains a Supplier Inventory recording each supplier, the service provided, the supplier owner, the data classification involved, and the assigned tier:

  • Tier 1 — Critical: severe disruption or material data loss potential (primary cloud service provider, AI model provider hosting customer data).
  • Tier 2 — Important: processes Confidential or Restricted information, supports business-critical operations.
  • Tier 3 — Standard: limited access to Cloudax information; non-critical role.
  • Tier 4 — Minimal: no access to Cloudax information.

7. Supplier Selection and Onboarding

All new suppliers in scope are onboarded through a documented process that includes:

  • Identification of data classification, processing role, and tier.
  • Collection of supplier security evidence proportionate to tier (ISO/IEC 27001 certificate, SOC 2 report, security questionnaire, penetration test summary).
  • Review of any open material findings, certifications, or breaches.
  • For Tier 1 and Tier 2, written security assessment with sign-off by the Information Security Manager.
  • For suppliers processing personal data, Privacy Lead review of the DPA and sub-processors.

8. Contractual Security Requirements

Cloudax’s standard supplier contracts include security clauses appropriate to tier, covering:

  • Confidentiality and non-disclosure.
  • Information security responsibilities, including alignment with recognised standards.
  • Data protection (Article 28 UK GDPR clauses where applicable, including international transfer safeguards).
  • Security incident notification with defined timeframes.
  • Sub-processing controls, including prior approval and flow-down requirements.
  • Audit and assurance rights, or equivalent third-party audit reporting.
  • Return or secure deletion of Cloudax information on termination.
  • Liability and indemnities for security and data protection failures.

9. Cloud Service Providers (cross-reference)

Cloud service providers are also governed by POL-007 Cloud Services Policy.

10. AI and Machine Learning Suppliers

AI and ML suppliers introduce specific risks. Additional controls apply:

  • Data submitted to the AI service is identified, classified, and assessed against contractual permissions.
  • Whether the supplier may use Cloudax inputs or customer data for training is documented and governed contractually — default-off where supported.
  • Hosting region, data residency, and international transfer safeguards are documented.
  • Use is recorded in the Cloudax AI Use Register and reflected in customer-facing documentation where appropriate.

11. Sub-Processors

Where Cloudax engages sub-processors, customers are informed in accordance with the relevant DPA. Cloudax maintains a current list of sub-processors and notifies customers of changes within the timeframe required by the DPA.

12. Outsourced Development

Where Cloudax outsources development, the supplier is required to:

  • Apply security practices at least equivalent to POL-024 Secure Development Lifecycle Policy.
  • Make their development environment, source code controls, testing, and incident management arrangements available to Cloudax for assurance.
  • Honour intellectual property, confidentiality, and data protection clauses in the contract.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.8.30 Outsourced development

13. Ongoing Monitoring and Assurance

  • Tier 1: review of certifications and assurance reports at least annually; review of incidents and material changes within 30 days.
  • Tier 2: review of certifications and assurance reports at least annually.
  • Tier 3 and 4: light-touch review at contract renewal or on material change.
  • Subscription to security advisories, status pages, and threat intelligence relevant to key suppliers.

14. Incident Management with Suppliers

Suppliers are required to notify Cloudax of incidents and personal data breaches within contractual timeframes. Incidents are managed in accordance with PLAN-IRP-001 Incident Response Plan.

15. Change Management of Supplier Services

Material changes to supplier services — controls, sub-processors, hosting regions, ownership, jurisdiction — are reviewed by the Information Security Manager and, where applicable, the Privacy Lead. Mitigations are agreed before the change takes effect.

16. Termination and Off-Boarding

  • On termination, supplier access is revoked promptly.
  • Cloudax information held by the supplier is returned or securely deleted; written confirmation is obtained.
  • The Supplier Inventory and related registers are updated.

17. Review and Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually and on material change.

18. Acknowledgement

All personnel involved in supplier engagements are required to acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
