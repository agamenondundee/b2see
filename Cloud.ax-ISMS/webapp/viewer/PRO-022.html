1. Purpose

This Procedure defines how Cloudax Ltd. monitors suppliers in life to maintain assurance that contractual and security obligations continue to be met. It implements POL-006 Supplier Security Policy and gives effect to A.5.22.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.5.22 Monitoring, review and change management of supplier services

2. Scope

This Procedure applies to all suppliers within the supplier security scope, with depth of monitoring proportionate to tier.

3. Definitions

  • Monitoring: ongoing oversight of a supplier’s performance, posture, and changes.
  • Periodic review: scheduled review on a defined cadence.

4. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Supplier owner: ongoing relationship management; first to notice changes.
  • Information Security Manager: leads monitoring and review of Tier 1 and Tier 2 suppliers.
  • Privacy Lead: monitors data protection aspects, including DPA compliance and sub-processor changes.
  • Chief Technology Officer: monitors technical performance of platform-critical suppliers.

6. Monitoring Activities

6.1 Continuous Monitoring

  • Subscription to Tier 1 supplier status pages and security advisories.
  • Subscription to threat intelligence feeds covering Cloudax’s technology stack.
  • Monitoring of public information about Tier 1 and Tier 2 suppliers (incidents, governance changes, ownership changes).

6.2 Periodic Review

Periodic review covers, at the cadence per Section 5:

  • Refresh of certifications, assurance reports, and pen test summaries.
  • Review of incidents and material changes since last review.
  • Review of sub-processors.
  • Review of supplier performance against contractual KPIs.
  • Review of any open issues identified previously.
  • Confirmation of currency of contact details and escalation paths.

6.3 Event-Driven Review

Out-of-cycle review is triggered by:

  • A material supplier incident.
  • A sub-processor change.
  • A change of supplier ownership, jurisdiction, or material business model.
  • Public threat intelligence indicating compromise or vulnerability.
  • Customer enquiry.

7. Supplier Performance Indicators

  • Availability against contractual SLA.
  • Incident frequency and severity.
  • Notification timeliness vs contractual obligations.
  • Open vulnerabilities relevant to Cloudax’s use.
  • Currency of certifications and assurance reports.

8. Sub-Processor Changes

  • Notifications of sub-processor changes are tracked by the Privacy Lead.
  • New sub-processors are reviewed for risk; objections are raised within contractual timeframes if appropriate.
  • Cloudax customers are notified of relevant changes per the customer DPA.

9. Supplier Incidents

  • Supplier-side incidents are tracked in the Information Security Event Log.
  • Cloudax’s incident response (PLAN-IRP-001) coordinates with the supplier.
  • Customer impact is assessed and customers are notified per contract.
  • Lessons learned feed continual improvement (Cl. 10.1).

10. Material Change to Supplier Posture

  • Material changes — acquisitions, change of jurisdiction, change of platform architecture, ownership, leadership of security function — trigger event-driven review.
  • Mitigations are agreed before the change takes effect, where feasible.
  • Where mitigations are not feasible, alternatives may be considered.

11. Findings and Escalation

  • Findings are recorded against the supplier and tracked to closure.
  • Material findings are recorded in REG-CAR-001.
  • Critical findings are escalated to the Chief Executive Officer.

12. Supplier Off-Boarding

  • On termination, supplier access is revoked promptly.
  • Cloudax information held by the supplier is returned or securely deleted; written confirmation is obtained.
  • Customer-facing implications (sub-processor changes) are managed by the Privacy Lead.
  • Supplier Inventory and related registers are updated.

13. Records and Retention

  • Monitoring records are held against the supplier in the Supplier Inventory.
  • Retention is per POL-009.

14. Compliance Monitoring

  • This Procedure is sampled at internal audit (PROG-AUDIT-001).
  • The auditor will typically request a Tier 1 supplier and ask for evidence of in-life monitoring.

15. Maintenance

This Procedure is reviewed at least annually and on material change to supplier requirements.

16. Acknowledgement

All personnel involved in supplier monitoring are required to acknowledge this Procedure on commencement, on material change, and on request.
