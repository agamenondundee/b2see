1. Purpose

This Policy sets out Cloudax’s ethical commitments in respect of AI and the human oversight that underpins them. It is cross-cutting — every other AI policy operates within the ethical commitments set out here. It supports the AI Policy (AI-POL-001) and complements the assessment discipline in AI-POL-004 (AISIA).

ISO/IEC 42001:2023 does not have a single Annex A control titled “ethics”, but ethical considerations run through Annex A — in policy alignment (A.2.3), impact assessment (A.5), responsible use (A.9), and customer relationships (A.10). This Policy makes those considerations explicit and operational.

ISO 27001:2022 references: ISO 42001 reference: A.2.3 Alignment with other organizational policies; A.5.4 Assessing AI system impact on individuals or groups of individuals; A.5.5 Assessing societal impacts of AI systems; A.9.2 Processes for responsible use of AI systems

2. Scope

This Policy applies to all AI activity within the AIMS scope (AIMS-SCOPE-001) and to Cloudax personnel, contractors, and partners.

3. Definitions

  • Human oversight: the ability of qualified humans to monitor, intervene in, override, or stop the operation of an AI system.
  • Ethical concern: an observation that an AI activity may breach the ethical commitments in this Policy.

4. Principles

  • Ethics is an operational discipline at Cloudax, not a marketing claim.
  • Human accountability for AI outcomes is preserved; AI is not a justification for absent humans.
  • Concerns are heard and acted on; cultural permission to raise concerns is foundational.
  • Where law and ethics diverge, ethics is the higher bar Cloudax meets.
  • Cloudax is humble about ethical questions — they are often hard, contested, and evolving.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Chief Executive Officer: ultimate accountability for ethical conduct of the company; arbitrates contested ethical decisions.
  • AI Governance Lead: owns this Policy; coordinates ethical review; brings concerns to the CEO and management review.
  • Chief Technology Officer: operationalises human oversight in technical design.
  • Privacy Lead: contributes ethical perspective on data subject impact.
  • All personnel: act consistently with the ethical commitments; raise concerns through Section 10.

6. Ethical Commitments

6.1 Human Dignity

  • Cloudax’s AI systems treat users with dignity and respect.
  • AI is not used to demean, manipulate, or coerce users.
  • Where AI mediates emotionally significant conversations (e.g. support, complaints, vulnerable people), design considers the user’s emotional context.

6.2 Fairness and Non-Discrimination

  • Cloudax’s AI systems are designed and operated to avoid systematic disadvantage to protected groups.
  • Bias is monitored, measured, and mitigated through the AI System Lifecycle (AI-POL-005).
  • Disparate impact — where the same AI behaviour produces different outcomes for different populations — is treated as a risk to address, not an inherent property to accept.

6.3 Honest Communication

  • Cloudax does not overstate AI capabilities in marketing, sales, customer-facing documentation, or public communication.
  • Limitations of AI systems are communicated alongside capabilities.
  • Where AI is used in a Cloudax communication or output, this is honestly represented — e.g. AI-generated images are not passed off as photographs.

6.4 Beneficial Use

  • Cloudax’s AI is designed to serve customer and end-user interests — helping conversations be better, not exploiting them for unrelated commercial gain.
  • AI features are evaluated against the question “does this make things better for the people on the other end of the conversation?”

6.5 Avoidance of Harm

  • Cloudax actively considers harm — to individuals, to groups, to society — in design, deployment, and monitoring.
  • Harm includes: financial harm, dignity harm, exclusion, dependency, erosion of autonomy, environmental harm.
  • Where potential harm cannot be reduced to acceptable levels, the AI activity is not pursued or is reshaped.

7. Human Oversight

7.1 Oversight at Design

  • Each AI system’s design includes human oversight mechanisms appropriate to its impact — from “human-on-the-loop” monitoring to “human-in-the-loop” decisions.
  • Oversight design choices are documented in AISIA and the AI system specification.
  • Oversight that exists only nominally (e.g. a human present but unable to actually intervene) does not satisfy this requirement.

7.2 Oversight at Operation

  • Production AI systems can be observed by qualified personnel.
  • Production AI systems can be paused, reverted, or disabled by qualified personnel without disproportionate effort.
  • Personnel with oversight responsibility have the competence to exercise it.

7.3 Oversight at Decision

  • Where AI output influences a decision affecting an individual, the decision-making path is documented and reviewable.
  • Where law (e.g. UK GDPR Article 22) requires human involvement in automated decisions, that requirement is met by design.
  • Affected persons can question or appeal AI-influenced decisions through documented channels.

8. Use Cases Cloudax Will Not Pursue

Cloudax does not develop or operate AI systems for the following purposes:

  • Mass surveillance of populations.
  • Social scoring of individuals by general characteristics.
  • Subliminal manipulation of behaviour to a person’s detriment.
  • Exploitation of vulnerabilities of specific groups (children, persons with disabilities, persons in financial or emotional distress).
  • Generation of deceptive content (deepfakes) intended to defraud or harm.
  • Use cases prohibited by applicable law (UK or EU AI Act prohibited practices, where they apply).

Customer requests inconsistent with this list are declined; the AI Governance Lead and Chief Executive Officer make the determination.

9. Use Cases Requiring Enhanced Review

The following use cases are not prohibited but require enhanced AISIA review and CEO awareness:

  • AI affecting access to essential services (healthcare, social services, finance, employment).
  • AI affecting children or vulnerable groups.
  • AI in safety-critical contexts.
  • AI making automated decisions with significant individual impact (e.g. service approval, eligibility determination).
  • AI processing biometric data for identification.
  • Use cases that may bring Cloudax into the scope of high-risk system obligations under future AI regulation.

10. Ethical Concerns Process

  • Personnel can raise ethical concerns through any channel listed in AI-POL-002 §8.
  • Ethical concerns are reviewed promptly by the AI Governance Lead with the relevant role(s).
  • Where the concern is material, it is brought to the Chief Executive Officer.
  • The originator is informed of the outcome where appropriate.
  • Concerns and outcomes are recorded; trends are reviewed at AIMS management review.

11. Engagement with the Wider AI Community

  • Cloudax engages with the wider AI community on responsible AI — industry bodies, standards work, regulator consultation, peer learning.
  • Engagement is consistent with customer confidentiality and competitive positioning.
  • Personnel contributing to external AI initiatives in their Cloudax capacity follow PRO-025 Special Interest Group Engagement (cross-reference).

12. Records

  • Ethical concerns and their resolution are recorded in the AIMS event log.
  • Decisions to decline use cases are recorded with rationale.
  • Use cases requiring enhanced review are recorded with the enhanced AISIA outputs in AI-REG-AISIA-001.

13. Compliance Monitoring

  • Compliance is monitored under AI-PROG-COMP-001 and audited under AI-PROG-AUDIT-001.

14. Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually and on material change to the AI ethical landscape, regulatory expectations, or Cloudax’s product.

15. Acknowledgement

All personnel acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
