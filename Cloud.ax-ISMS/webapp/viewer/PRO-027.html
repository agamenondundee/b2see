1. Purpose

This Procedure defines how Cloudax personnel and other parties report information security events, weaknesses, and incidents. Prompt reporting is essential to early detection, containment, and learning.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.6.8 Information security event reporting

2. Scope

This Procedure applies to all personnel, contractors, partners, and visitors with access to Cloudax information, systems, or facilities. It also applies to events identified through automated monitoring tooling.

3. Definitions

  • Information security event: an identified occurrence indicating a possible breach of policy, failure of controls, or previously unknown situation that may be relevant to security.
  • Information security weakness: a state of a system, control, process, or environment that could lead to an event.
  • Information security incident: an event or series of events that has compromised, or is likely to compromise, information security.

4. Principles

  • Report early, report often. A small report quickly is better than a large report late.
  • Reporting is encouraged and protected. Personnel reporting in good faith are not penalised.
  • Speed beats certainty. If something looks wrong, report — investigation is for later.
  • No event is too small. Near-misses and weaknesses are valuable signal.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • All personnel: report events, weaknesses, and incidents promptly.
  • Information Security Manager: receives reports, triages, classifies, and decides escalation.
  • Line managers: support team members in reporting; do not block or filter reports.
  • On-call: receives reports out of hours.

6. What to Report

Personnel must report immediately if they:

  • Lose a Cloudax device, an access credential, a security key, or a paper record containing Cloudax information.
  • Click a suspicious link or enter credentials into a suspicious page.
  • Approve an MFA prompt they did not initiate.
  • Receive a suspicious email, message, or phone call.
  • Send Cloudax information to the wrong recipient.
  • Receive Cloudax information that does not appear to be intended for them.
  • Suspect their account or device has been compromised.
  • Suspect malware or unauthorised software on a Cloudax device.
  • Suspect or know that customer data has been accessed, disclosed, modified, or lost without authorisation.
  • Identify a weakness in a Cloudax system, process, or supplier.
  • Observe behaviour that may indicate a policy breach, including by themselves.
  • Are approached by anyone (including personnel) attempting to obtain credentials, customer data, or sensitive information through unusual channels.

Where in doubt, personnel should report. Triage will determine the appropriate response.

7. How to Report

Reports are made through any of the following channels:

  • Primary: the Cloudax security reporting channel — Slack/Teams #security-events channel, monitored continuously during working hours and via on-call out-of-hours.
  • Email: security@cloudax.io — monitored continuously.
  • Phishing: the “Report Phishing” button in Outlook is the preferred channel for suspected phishing.
  • Verbal: speak to the Information Security Manager, the CTO, or a line manager. Verbal reports are subsequently captured in the security reporting channel by the receiver.
  • Out of hours: contact the on-call rota; for serious matters, escalate to the Chief Executive Officer.

Personnel should provide:

  • What happened, in their own words.
  • When (and where) it happened, as accurately as possible.
  • Who was involved, including themselves.
  • What systems, accounts, devices, or data were affected.
  • What they have already done, if anything (e.g. closed an email, changed a password).

8. After Reporting

  • Personnel should not investigate the matter beyond making the report unless asked.
  • Personnel should not delete or alter potential evidence (emails, files, log entries).
  • Personnel should follow any instructions from the Information Security Manager or on-call (e.g. disconnect from network, change password, hand over device).
  • Personnel should not discuss the matter with parties outside Cloudax unless explicitly authorised.

9. Triage and Classification

Reports are triaged by the Information Security Manager (or on-call). Initial triage assigns:

10. Escalation Path

  • Initial recipient: Information Security Manager (or on-call).
  • Operational escalation: Chief Technology Officer for technical incidents; Privacy Lead for personal data implications.
  • Executive escalation: Chief Executive Officer for material incidents (Section 11 of PLAN-IRP-001).
  • External escalation: ICO for personal data breaches per PRO-024; NCSC, Action Fraud, and other authorities per REG-AUTH-001 and PLAN-IRP-001.

11. Confidentiality and Protection of the Reporter

  • Reports are handled with appropriate confidentiality. Distribution is limited to those who need to know.
  • Personnel reporting in good faith — even where the report turns out to be a misunderstanding, or where the reporter was the cause — are not subject to disciplinary action solely for reporting.
  • Cloudax’s whistleblowing arrangements provide additional protection where appropriate.
  • Retaliation for raising a report in good faith is itself a serious breach and is addressed under POL-011.

12. Records and Retention

  • Reports are recorded in the Information Security Event Log, maintained by the Information Security Manager.
  • Records are retained per POL-009.
  • Reports may be linked to entries in REG-RISK-001 (where they indicate risk), PLAN-IRP-001 records (where escalated to incident), or REG-CAR-001 (where they trigger corrective action).

13. Compliance Monitoring

  • Volume, type, and time-to-triage of reports are monitored under PROG-COMP-001.
  • A sustained low report volume is itself a signal warranting awareness reinforcement.

14. Maintenance

This Procedure is reviewed at least annually and on material change to the reporting channels. Changes are approved by the Information Security Manager and recorded in Annex B.

15. Acknowledgement

All personnel are required to acknowledge this Procedure on commencement, on material change, and on request.
