1. Purpose

This Policy defines how Cloudax Ltd. uses cryptography to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and authenticity of information.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.8.24 Use of cryptography

2. Scope

This Policy applies to all use of cryptography by Cloudax — in the platform, supporting systems, endpoints, communications, backups, and removable media.

3. Definitions

  • Cryptography: the use of mathematical techniques to protect information.
  • Key management: the generation, storage, distribution, rotation, and destruction of cryptographic keys.
  • HSM: Hardware Security Module — a physical or virtual device that safeguards and manages digital keys.

4. Principles

  • Use established, well-reviewed cryptographic algorithms and protocols.
  • Avoid bespoke cryptography.
  • Manage keys through approved key management services with strong access controls.
  • Rotate keys at defined intervals and on suspected compromise.
  • Track and respond to deprecation of algorithms and protocols.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Information Security Manager: owns this Policy; approves cryptographic standards.
  • Chief Technology Officer: ensures cryptography is correctly implemented in the platform.
  • System owners: configure cryptography in line with this Policy.

6. Approved Algorithms and Key Lengths

Cloudax aligns with current good-practice baselines, including NCSC Foundation Cryptographic Profile guidance and NIST recommendations. Default approved choices include:

  • Symmetric: AES-256 (or AES-128 where the use case justifies).
  • Asymmetric: RSA ≥ 3072 bits or ECDSA on NIST P-256/P-384, or Ed25519.
  • Hashing: SHA-256 or stronger.
  • KDFs / password hashing: Argon2id, scrypt, bcrypt, or PBKDF2 with appropriate parameters.
  • TLS 1.2 minimum, TLS 1.3 preferred.

7. Encryption in Transit

  • All Cloudax services use TLS for communications carrying Cloudax information.
  • Internal service-to-service communication is encrypted in transit (mTLS or equivalent) where supported by the platform.
  • Cipher suites are restricted to current secure profiles; weak ciphers are disabled.

8. Encryption at Rest

  • Personal data and Confidential or Restricted information is encrypted at rest using approved algorithms.
  • Cloud-native disk encryption is enabled.
  • Object storage uses server-side encryption.

9. Key Management

  • Keys are stored in approved cloud key management services or HSMs.
  • Access to keys is restricted to authorised identities and is logged.
  • Keys are rotated at defined intervals and on suspected compromise.
  • Compromised or retired keys are destroyed in line with provider guidance.

10. Customer-Managed Keys

  • Customer-managed keys (CMK) are supported where the customer contract or data classification requires them.
  • CMK arrangements are documented and tested.

11. Certificates and Public-Key Infrastructure

  • Public TLS certificates are issued by approved certificate authorities.
  • Private PKI, where used, is documented and access-controlled.
  • Certificate expiry is monitored; renewals are automated where supported.

12. Cryptographic Protocols and Deprecation

  • Cloudax tracks deprecation of algorithms and protocols (e.g. SHA-1, TLS 1.0/1.1, RSA ≤ 2048).
  • Deprecated cryptography is migrated within reasonable timeframes.
  • Quantum-resistant cryptography developments are monitored.

13. Hashing and Password Storage

  • Passwords are stored only as salted hashes using approved password-hashing algorithms with appropriate parameters.
  • Password equality checks are constant-time.

14. Encryption of Backups and Removable Media

  • Backups (POL-018) and removable media (POL-013) are encrypted using approved algorithms.

15. Compliance Monitoring

  • Cryptographic configuration is monitored as part of configuration management (POL-015).
  • Failures (e.g. weak cipher use, expired certificates) are alerted.

16. Review and Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually and on material change to cryptographic guidance.

17. Acknowledgement

All personnel involved in cryptographic implementation are required to acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
