1. Purpose

This Awareness Training Programme defines how Cloudax Ltd. develops and maintains information security awareness, education, and competence across its workforce.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.6.3 Information security awareness, education and training; Cl. 7.2 Competence; Cl. 7.3 Awareness

2. Scope

This Programme applies to all Cloudax personnel — directors, employees, contractors, consultants, and agency workers — throughout their engagement with Cloudax. Where appropriate, elements are extended to suppliers and partners with privileged access to Cloudax information.

3. Definitions

  • Awareness: knowledge of information security policies and the part each person plays.
  • Training: structured activity to develop knowledge, skills, or behaviours.
  • Competence: the ability to apply knowledge and skills to achieve intended results.

4. Programme Objectives

  • Every person engaged by Cloudax has the awareness needed to act in line with the Information Security Policy and to recognise and report security risk.
  • Every person in a role with information security responsibility has the competence needed to perform that role.
  • Awareness keeps pace with evolving threats and changes to Cloudax.
  • Records of training are complete, accurate, and available for audit.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Information Security Manager: owns this Programme; selects content; tracks completion; reports to management review.
  • Privacy Lead: contributes content on UK GDPR / DPA 2018 obligations and data subject rights.
  • Line managers: ensure team members complete training on time; address gaps.
  • All personnel: complete assigned training; engage with awareness content; act on what they learn.

7. Curriculum

7.1 Induction Training

Every new joiner completes induction training before being granted standard access. Induction includes:

  • Cloudax’s information security commitments and the Information Security Policy.
  • Acceptable use, including the Manchester shared workspace, hybrid working, and AI tools.
  • Customer data handling and privacy basics.
  • Authentication, MFA, and credential safety.
  • Phishing recognition and reporting.
  • How to report events, weaknesses, and incidents.
  • Disciplinary consequences of policy breach.

7.2 Annual Refresh

All personnel complete annual refresher training that:

  • Reaffirms the Information Security Policy and any material policy changes since the last cycle.
  • Updates personnel on threat landscape changes relevant to Cloudax.
  • Reinforces phishing recognition and includes interactive scenarios.
  • Refreshes privacy obligations including data subject rights.

7.3 Role-Specific Training

Personnel in cohort-specific roles (Section 6) receive additional training:

  • Engineering: secure coding, secure architecture, OWASP Top 10, the SDLC controls in POL-024.
  • Privileged access: privileged access expectations, just-in-time elevation, least-privilege working.
  • Customer-facing: handling customer data requests, breach indicators, escalation paths.
  • Privacy and risk leads: ICO guidance updates, regulatory developments, peer-reviewed risk and assurance training.
  • Leadership: ISMS performance reporting, sign-off authority, regulatory landscape briefings.

7.4 Just-in-Time Training

Targeted communications are issued in response to events, e.g.:

  • Threat advisories specific to Cloudax’s technology stack.
  • Lessons learned from incidents (anonymised as appropriate).
  • Communication of new or revised policies.

8. Phishing Simulation Programme

  • Cloudax runs phishing simulations at least quarterly, varying by sophistication and theme.
  • Simulations are educational, not punitive. Failure triggers a short follow-up coaching, not disciplinary action.
  • Repeated failure within a cycle is reviewed by the line manager and Information Security Manager.
  • Simulation outcomes feed the Annual Objectives (ISMS-OBJ-001).

9. Delivery Mechanism

  • Standard content is delivered through an approved learning platform, supplemented by live sessions for role-specific training.
  • Awareness communications are issued through Slack, Teams, or email channels as appropriate.
  • Quick-reference materials (one-pagers, decision aids) are made available in the ISMS repository.

10. Records and Evidence

  • The Information Security Manager maintains the master Training Records evidencing completion by individual, cohort, course, and date.
  • Records are retained per POL-009 Records Retention Policy and Cl. 7.2 of ISO/IEC 27001:2022.
  • Records are made available to the auditor on request.

11. Effectiveness Measurement

  • Completion rate — target 100% within 30 days of due date.
  • Phishing simulation outcomes — click rate, report rate, and trend over time.
  • Event reports — volume of personnel-reported events over time as a leading indicator of awareness.
  • Knowledge checks built into training content.
  • Periodic survey of personnel on policy familiarity.

12. Failure to Complete Training

  • Personnel who do not complete required training within 30 days of due date are flagged to their line manager.
  • Continued non-completion is escalated to the Information Security Manager and may be addressed under POL-011 Disciplinary Policy.
  • Where access is conditional on training (e.g. privileged access), failure to complete may result in temporary loss of access.

13. Annual Plan and Calendar

The Information Security Manager publishes a calendar at the start of each cycle showing:

  • Compulsory annual refresh windows.
  • Phishing simulation cadence.
  • Role-specific training scheduled for the cycle.
  • Awareness campaigns aligned to relevant external events (e.g. Cyber Security Awareness Month).

14. Maintenance

This Programme is reviewed at least annually and updated when material change occurs to the threat landscape, the Cloudax workforce, or the regulatory environment.

15. Acknowledgement

All personnel are required to acknowledge this Programme on commencement, on material change, and on request.
