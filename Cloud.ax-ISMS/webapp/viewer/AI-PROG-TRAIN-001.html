1. Purpose

This AI Awareness and Training Programme defines how Cloudax Ltd. develops and maintains AI awareness, education, and competence across its workforce. It implements Clauses 7.2 (Competence) and 7.3 (Awareness) of ISO/IEC 42001:2023 and supports A.4.6 Human resources.

ISO 27001:2022 references: ISO 42001 reference: Cl. 7.2 Competence; Cl. 7.3 Awareness; A.4.6 Human resources

2. Scope

This Programme applies to all Cloudax personnel — directors, employees, contractors, consultants, agency workers — throughout their engagement with Cloudax. Where appropriate, elements are extended to suppliers and partners with privileged influence over Cloudax AI systems.

3. Definitions

  • AI awareness: knowledge of Cloudax’s AI Policy, AI principles, and the part each person plays in responsible AI.
  • AI competence: the ability to apply AI knowledge and skills appropriate to role.

4. Programme Objectives

  • Every person engaged by Cloudax has the AI awareness needed to act in line with the AI Policy and to recognise and report AI risk.
  • Every person in a role with AI responsibility has the competence needed to perform that role.
  • AI awareness keeps pace with the evolving AI landscape — capabilities, risks, regulation.
  • Records of AI training are complete, accurate, and available for audit.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • AI Governance Lead: owns this Programme; selects content; tracks completion; reports to management review.
  • Chief Technology Officer: contributes content for AI Builder cohort; ensures engineering competence is current.
  • Privacy Lead: contributes content on AI privacy, AISIA, and data subject impact.
  • Line managers: ensure team members complete AI training on time; address gaps.
  • All personnel: complete assigned training; engage with awareness content; act on what they learn.

7. Curriculum

7.1 All-Personnel Induction (AI Awareness)

Every new joiner completes AI awareness as part of induction. Topics:

  • Cloudax’s AI Policy and AI Principles.
  • How AI is used in the Cloudax product.
  • Responsible use of AI tools (generative AI, coding assistants) at work — what is permitted, what is restricted.
  • Recognising AI bias, harm, and ethical concerns.
  • How to report AI concerns through PRO-027 or directly to the AI Governance Lead.
  • Confidentiality and data handling implications of AI tool use.

7.2 Annual Refresh

All personnel complete annual refresher training that:

  • Reaffirms the AI Policy and any material policy changes since the last cycle.
  • Updates personnel on the AI threat landscape and regulatory changes.
  • Reinforces responsible AI tool use.
  • Reinforces the principles of bias awareness, transparency, and human oversight.

7.3 Engineering / Product (AI Builders)

  • Responsible AI development principles.
  • AISIA process and practical application.
  • Bias and fairness in AI: detection, measurement, mitigation.
  • AI security: prompt injection, jailbreaking, model theft, data poisoning.
  • Production AI monitoring: drift, accuracy, fairness instruments, anomaly response.
  • Data for AI: provenance, quality, preparation, lawful basis.
  • AI-specific change management.
  • AI documentation: model cards, system documentation, customer-facing documentation.

7.4 AI Governance and Risk Leads

  • ISO/IEC 42001:2023 in depth.
  • Adjacent standards: ISO/IEC 22989, ISO/IEC 23894.
  • Regulatory landscape: UK AI policy, EU AI Act, ICO AI guidance.
  • AISIA methodology and facilitation.
  • AI risk management at depth.
  • AI incident response leadership.
  • Engagement with regulators on AI matters.

7.5 Customer-Facing Personnel

  • Cloudax AI capabilities and limitations — what to communicate to customers.
  • Customer questions about AI — how to answer; when to escalate.
  • Recognising AI-related customer concerns; escalation paths.
  • AI incident communication — what is shared, by whom, when.

7.6 Leadership

  • AIMS performance reporting.
  • AI risk acceptance authority.
  • AI regulatory landscape briefings.
  • AI ethical decision-making.

7.7 Just-in-Time

Targeted communications are issued in response to events:

  • AI threat advisories specific to Cloudax’s technology stack.
  • Lessons from AI incidents (anonymised as appropriate).
  • Communication of new or revised AI policies.
  • Regulatory developments requiring immediate awareness.

8. Delivery Mechanism

  • Standard content is delivered through an approved learning platform, supplemented by live sessions for cohort-specific training.
  • Awareness communications are issued through Slack, Teams, or email channels as appropriate.
  • Quick-reference materials (one-pagers, decision aids, AISIA templates) are made available in the AIMS / ISMS repository.

9. Records and Evidence

  • The AI Governance Lead maintains the master AI Training Records evidencing completion by individual, cohort, course, and date.
  • Records are retained per POL-009.
  • Records are made available to the auditor on request.

10. Effectiveness Measurement

  • Completion rate — target 100% within 30 days of due date.
  • Knowledge checks built into training content.
  • AI event reports — volume of personnel-reported AI concerns over time as a leading indicator of awareness.
  • Periodic survey of personnel on AI policy familiarity.

11. Failure to Complete Training

  • Personnel who do not complete required AI training within 30 days of due date are flagged to their line manager.
  • Continued non-completion is escalated to the AI Governance Lead and may be addressed under POL-011 Disciplinary Policy.
  • Where AI tool access is conditional on training, failure to complete may result in temporary loss of access.

12. Annual Plan and Calendar

The AI Governance Lead publishes a calendar at the start of each cycle showing:

  • Compulsory AI annual refresh windows.
  • Cohort-specific training scheduled for the cycle.
  • AI-relevant external events worth attending (conferences, regulator engagement).
  • Awareness campaigns aligned to relevant external events.

13. Coordination with the ISMS Training Programme

  • AI training and information security training are delivered as a joint curriculum where practical, with AI-specific modules.
  • The same training platform and records system are used for both.
  • Personnel see one training calendar covering both.

14. Maintenance

This Programme is reviewed at least annually and updated when material change occurs to the AI threat landscape, the Cloudax AI portfolio, or the regulatory environment.

15. Acknowledgement

All personnel are required to acknowledge this Programme on commencement, on material change, and on request.
