1. Purpose

This Disaster Recovery Plan (the “Plan”) sets out how Cloudax Ltd. recovers ICT services after a disaster. It complements the Business Continuity Plan (PLAN-BCP-001) by focusing on the technical recovery of platform and supporting systems.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.5.30 ICT readiness for business continuity; A.8.14 Redundancy of information processing facilities; A.8.13 Information backup

2. Scope

This Plan covers the recovery of:

  • The Cloudax conversational AI platform (production and customer-facing services).
  • Supporting cloud infrastructure, including identity, networking, storage, and security tooling.
  • Source code repositories, CI/CD pipelines, and developer tooling.
  • Customer data, including voice recordings, transcripts, and derived data.
  • SaaS applications used to operate Cloudax (productivity, communications, ticketing, monitoring).

3. Definitions

  • Disaster: a sudden event causing significant disruption or loss of ICT services or data.
  • Failover: the act of switching service from a primary to an alternative facility.
  • Failback: the act of returning service to the primary facility once it is restored.

4. Architecture and Resilience Posture

Cloudax’s recovery posture is built on three pillars:

  • Cloud-native architecture using established cloud service providers with multi-region capability and provider-native resilience features (auto-scaling, managed databases with replication, object storage with regional redundancy).
  • Defence in depth across regions, accounts, and providers, including segregated identity for backup and recovery operations.
  • Backup arrangements per POL-018 Backup Policy, including cross-region replication, immutability for ransomware mitigation, and tested restoration procedures.

8. Recovery Strategies

8.1 Multi-Region Failover

  • The Cloudax platform is deployed across at least two cloud regions, with active or warm-standby capacity in the secondary region.
  • Customer data is replicated cross-region in line with customer data residency obligations.
  • Failover decisions are taken by the Chief Technology Officer in consultation with the Incident Manager.
  • Failover playbooks are maintained as runbooks (Section 10) and exercised annually.

8.2 Backup-Based Recovery

  • Where multi-region failover is insufficient (e.g. data corruption or ransomware), recovery is from backup per POL-018.
  • Backups are immutable where supported and tested for restoration annually.
  • Recovery to a clean environment is preferred when there is any possibility of compromise.

8.3 SaaS Service Recovery

  • Microsoft 365, identity provider, and other SaaS services rely on provider-native resilience supplemented by Cloudax third-party backup.
  • Where a provider is unavailable for an extended period, Cloudax activates documented backup channels for communications and ticketing.

8.4 Source Code and CI/CD Recovery

  • Source code repositories are mirrored to a third-party backup at least daily.
  • CI/CD pipeline configuration is held in version-controlled Infrastructure-as-Code, recoverable from the same backup.
  • Build artefacts are stored in approved registries with retention sufficient for rollback.

8.5 Identity Recovery

  • Break-glass administrative accounts for the corporate identity provider are documented and protected per POL-002 §9.
  • MFA recovery procedures are documented and tested.
  • In a confirmed compromise, identity recovery is performed in coordination with PLAN-IRP-001.

9. Activation

This Plan is activated by the Chief Technology Officer in consultation with the Incident Manager. Activation may be triggered by:

  • A platform incident exceeding agreed availability tolerances.
  • A confirmed disaster scenario per Section 7.
  • Activation of PLAN-BCP-001 where ICT recovery is required.

Activation triggers opening of an incident bridge, time-stamped event logging, and selection of the recovery strategy.

10. Recovery Runbooks (cross-reference)

Detailed step-by-step runbooks are maintained separately by the engineering team and version-controlled alongside Infrastructure-as-Code. Runbooks cover:

  • Region failover for the Cloudax platform.
  • Restore from backup for customer data, configuration, and source code.
  • Identity provider break-glass and recovery.
  • CI/CD pipeline rebuild.
  • Network reconfiguration in degraded modes.

Runbooks are reviewed at least annually and updated whenever the underlying technology changes.

11. Verification of Recovery

  • Recovery is verified through automated health checks, smoke tests, and customer-impact monitoring.
  • Customer data integrity is verified through sampling and reconciliation against last-known-good state.
  • Recovery is signed off by the Chief Technology Officer before stand-down is declared.

12. Customer Communication

Customer communication during disaster recovery follows PLAN-BCP-001 §11, with technical detail limited to what customers need to act on (e.g. expected resumption time, data-loss exposure, any retry guidance).

13. Failback to Normal Operations

  • Failback to the primary region or environment is undertaken once it has been re-established and verified.
  • Failback is planned to minimise customer disruption; where possible, it is performed during a maintenance window.
  • Failback is signed off by the Chief Technology Officer.

14. Post-Recovery Review

  • Every recovery is followed by a blameless post-incident review covering technical recovery, customer impact, and lessons learned.
  • Findings feed continual improvement and management review.

15. Testing

  • This Plan is tested at least annually through a combination of tabletop exercise and at least one technical recovery test.
  • Backup restoration is tested at least annually per POL-018.
  • Test outcomes are recorded and reviewed at management review.

16. Maintenance

This Plan is reviewed at least annually and on material change to the architecture, recovery objectives, or supplier base. Material changes are approved by the Chief Executive Officer and recorded in Annex B.

17. Acknowledgement

All personnel involved in technical recovery are required to acknowledge this Plan on commencement, on material change, and on request.
