1. Purpose

This AI Risk Treatment Plan records the treatment of AI risks identified in the AI Risk Register (AI-REG-RISK-001), in accordance with Clause 6.1.3 of ISO/IEC 42001:2023 and the methodology set out in AIMS-RA-001.

ISO 27001:2022 references: ISO 42001 reference: Clause 6.1.3 AI risk treatment

2. Scope

This Plan covers all AI risks identified in AI-REG-RISK-001 that are above the acceptance threshold (Moderate and above), and any Low risks that the AI Governance Lead determines warrant active treatment.

3. Definitions

  • Treatment: the action taken to modify a risk, including selection and implementation of controls.
  • Residual risk: the risk remaining after treatment.
  • Risk owner: the named individual accountable for the risk and its treatment.

4. Roles and Responsibilities

  • AI Governance Lead: owns this Plan; coordinates treatment activity; reports to management review.
  • Risk owners: implement treatment for their assigned risks; report progress.
  • Chief Technology Officer: accountable for technical treatment of AI-system risks.
  • Chief Executive Officer: approves treatment of Critical risks; accepts Critical residual risk.

5. Treatment Approach by Risk Band

  • Low: typically retained, monitored periodically.
  • Moderate: treatment plan documented; controls confirmed effective.
  • High: treatment plan approved by AI Governance Lead; treated within an agreed timeframe; residual risk accepted by AI Governance Lead.
  • Critical: treatment plan approved by Chief Executive Officer; treated as priority; residual risk accepted by Chief Executive Officer; reported to every management review.

6. Treatment Options

  • Avoid: eliminate the risk source (e.g. discontinue a feature, exit a use case, change supplier).
  • Modify: introduce, strengthen, or change controls to reduce likelihood or impact (e.g. add monitoring, enforce human-in-the-loop, redesign data pipeline).
  • Share: transfer some or all of the risk to a third party (e.g. through contract, insurance).
  • Retain: accept the risk and monitor it. Retention requires explicit acceptance at the appropriate authority level.

7. Treatment Plan Structure

Each treatment plan entry contains:

  • Risk reference (linked to AI-REG-RISK-001).
  • Risk description summary.
  • Selected treatment option.
  • Selected controls (with cross-reference to AIMS-SOA-001 and the implementing document).
  • Risk owner.
  • Target completion date.
  • Status.
  • Residual risk (likelihood, impact, band) post-treatment.
  • Acceptance authority and acceptance date.

9. Tracking, Review, and Closure

  • Treatment progress is reviewed by the AI Governance Lead at least quarterly for High and Critical risks; annually for Moderate.
  • On completion, the risk owner reports completion with evidence.
  • The AI Governance Lead verifies treatment effectiveness before recording residual risk and obtaining acceptance.
  • Where treatment proves ineffective, the risk is reopened and re-treated.

10. Approval

This Plan is approved by the Chief Executive Officer at issue and on material revision. Approval covers the framework and treatment approach. Individual treatment entries within the Plan are approved at the authority level appropriate to the risk band.

11. Maintenance

This Plan is reviewed at least annually and reconciled with the AI Risk Register at every review.
