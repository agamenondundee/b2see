1. Purpose

This Policy defines how Cloudax Ltd. retains, archives, and securely disposes of records and information in line with legal, regulatory, contractual, and business requirements.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.5.33 Protection of records; A.8.10 Information deletion

2. Scope

This Policy applies to all records held by Cloudax in any form — electronic, physical, in cloud services, or in third-party systems on Cloudax’s behalf — covering business records, customer data, personal data, financial records, employment records, and ISMS evidence.

3. Definitions

  • Record: information created, received, and maintained by Cloudax as evidence of activities or transactions, in any form.
  • Retention period: the minimum and maximum length of time a record is kept before disposal.
  • Legal hold: a temporary suspension of normal disposal pending litigation, investigation, or regulatory action.

4. Principles

  • Records are retained only as long as required.
  • Retention periods are documented in the Records Retention Schedule.
  • Records are protected against loss, alteration, and unauthorised disclosure throughout their lifecycle.
  • Disposal at end of retention is secure, auditable, and irreversible.
  • Legal holds override normal disposal until lifted.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Information Security Manager: owns this Policy.
  • Privacy Lead: ensures personal data retention complies with UK GDPR.
  • Information owners: identify retention requirements for records they own.
  • All personnel: dispose of records in accordance with the Schedule and this Policy.

6. Records Retention Schedule

Cloudax maintains a Records Retention Schedule covering all categories of record. The Schedule sets the minimum retention period, the basis (legal, contractual, business), the storage location, and the disposal method. Categories include:

  • Customer contracts and DPAs.
  • Customer voice recordings, transcripts, and derived data (subject to customer contract).
  • Employee records and contractor agreements.
  • Financial records and tax records (statutory minima apply).
  • ISMS evidence (audit reports, training records, access reviews, incidents).
  • Marketing records and consents (where applicable under PECR).
  • Logs and monitoring records (per POL-019).

7. Customer Data Retention

  • Customer data retention follows the customer contract or DPA.
  • On contract termination, customer data is returned, exported, or securely deleted within the timeframe specified in the contract.
  • Data subject rights requests under the UK GDPR (e.g. erasure) take precedence over default retention where applicable.

8. Personal Data Retention (cross-reference)

Personal data is retained only as long as necessary for the purposes for which it is processed, in accordance with POL-010 Privacy Policy and the lawful basis recorded in the Record of Processing Activities (REG-ROPA-001).

9. Information Deletion

  • Information no longer required is deleted using approved methods (cryptographic erasure, secure overwrite, or physical destruction as appropriate).
  • Deletion is recorded for auditability where required by retention obligations.
  • Cloud-based deletion uses provider-supported deletion features and is followed up by verification where contracts require it.
  • Detailed deletion procedures are in PRO-017 Information Deletion Procedure.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.8.10 Information deletion

10. Backup and Archival Retention

  • Backup and archive retention periods are documented in POL-018 Backup Policy.
  • Records subject to mandatory retention are backed up in line with their retention period.

11. Legal Holds

  • Where Cloudax becomes aware of actual or anticipated litigation, investigation, or regulatory action, the Information Security Manager initiates a legal hold.
  • Personnel are notified of records subject to legal hold.
  • Subject records are preserved unaltered until the hold is lifted.

12. Disposal of Records and Media

  • Paper records are disposed of through secure shredding.
  • Digital media is disposed of in accordance with POL-013 Removable Media Policy and PRO-017.
  • End-of-life equipment is disposed of through approved providers with certificates of destruction.

13. Compliance Monitoring

  • Compliance is monitored through internal audit (PROG-AUDIT-001) and management review.
  • Findings are addressed through PRO-031 Corrective Actions.

14. Review and Maintenance

This Policy and the Records Retention Schedule are reviewed at least annually and on material change to legal or contractual requirements.

15. Acknowledgement

All personnel are required to acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
