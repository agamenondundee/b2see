1. Purpose

This document records the information security objectives of the Cloudax ISMS, in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 6.2. The objectives establish the measurable outcomes the ISMS is designed to achieve, in support of the Information Security Policy (POL-001) and the Cloudax business strategy.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Clause 6.2 Information security objectives and planning to achieve them

2. Scope

This document applies to the full scope of the Cloudax ISMS as defined in ISMS-SCOPE-001. Objectives are set at the organisation level and may be cascaded into team or function-level KPIs at the discretion of the responsible owner.

3. Principles

  • Objectives are consistent with the Information Security Policy.
  • Objectives are measurable, where practicable, and have an identified owner.
  • Objectives reflect Cloudax’s small size: a small number of focused outcomes, not a long list of indicators.
  • Objectives are reviewed and refreshed annually, and updated when material change occurs.
  • Progress is reported to management review and used to drive continual improvement.

4. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Chief Executive Officer: approves the objectives and provides resources.
  • Information Security Manager: owns this document; proposes objectives; tracks progress; reports to management review.
  • Objective owners: deliver the assigned objective and report progress.
  • All personnel: contribute to the objectives through their day-to-day work.

5. Setting Objectives

Objectives are set with reference to:

  • The Information Security Policy and the ISMS Scope Statement.
  • Outputs from the Risk Assessment Methodology and the Risk Treatment Plan.
  • Customer contractual commitments and regulatory obligations.
  • Findings from the Internal Audit Programme (PROG-AUDIT-001), incidents, and management review.
  • Relevant external benchmarks and threat trends.

Objectives are documented with: a clear outcome statement, a measurement, an owner, a target date, and a baseline where one exists.

6. Standing Objectives

The following standing objectives apply to the ISMS continuously:

  • Protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of customer information and Cloudax information assets.
  • Maintain alignment with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 and the controls applicable under ISMS-SOA-001.
  • Comply with applicable legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements, including the UK GDPR, the Data Protection Act 2018, and customer contracts.
  • Identify, treat, and monitor information security risks under ISMS-RA-001.
  • Detect, respond to, and learn from information security events under PLAN-IRP-001.
  • Continually improve the ISMS in line with management review and incident learning.

Standing objectives are not time-bound and remain in force across review cycles.

8. Measurement and Reporting

  • Each objective has a defined measure or KPI and a target.
  • Progress is recorded by the objective owner and consolidated by the Information Security Manager.
  • Progress against all objectives is reported to management review at least every six months.
  • Significant slippage is escalated promptly; the Chief Executive Officer is informed and may revise the objective, the resourcing, or the target.

9. Resourcing and Authority

The resources required to achieve each objective — personnel, time, budget, and tooling — are agreed as part of the annual planning cycle and confirmed by the Chief Executive Officer. Where additional resources are needed in-cycle, the objective owner raises a request through the management review or directly to the Chief Executive Officer.

10. Communication

  • Standing and current-cycle objectives are communicated to all personnel through the ISMS document repository and at induction.
  • Progress against objectives is shared with personnel through internal communications at least quarterly.
  • Where appropriate, objectives are shared with customers, suppliers, and certification bodies.

11. Review and Refresh

  • Standing objectives are reviewed at least annually by the Information Security Manager and refreshed where the policy or ISMS scope has changed.
  • Annual objectives are set at the start of each cycle, reviewed at six months, and closed out at year end.
  • Objectives are updated mid-cycle where material change requires it (e.g. a new customer obligation, a new regulation, a significant incident, or a change in the threat landscape).

12. Linkage to Risk and Continual Improvement

Objectives are derived from and feed back into the risk management and continual improvement processes:

  • Treatments in the Risk Treatment Plan (ISMS-RTP-001) typically generate objectives where they are material and time-bound.
  • Findings from PROG-AUDIT-001, incidents, and management review can give rise to new or revised objectives.
  • Closure of objectives feeds the closure of corresponding risks and the corrective action register (REG-CAR-001).
