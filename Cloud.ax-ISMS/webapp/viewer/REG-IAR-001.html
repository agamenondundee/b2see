1. Purpose

This Information Asset Register is the inventory of information assets in scope of the Cloudax ISMS. It supports the Information Security Policy (POL-001) and gives effect to A.5.9 Inventory of information and other associated assets.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.5.9 Inventory of information and other associated assets

2. Scope

This Register covers all information assets within the Cloudax ISMS scope, including information itself, the systems that process it, the facilities and devices that hold it, and the people and suppliers associated with it.

3. Definitions

  • Asset: anything that has value to Cloudax — information, systems, devices, software, facilities, suppliers, or personnel relationships.
  • Owner: the named person accountable for the protection and management of an asset.
  • Classification: the protection level assigned to an information asset under POL-004.

4. Asset Categories

  • Information assets — customer data (recordings, transcripts, configuration), personal data, business records, source code, models, training data.
  • System assets — cloud tenancies, applications, services, repositories, identity providers, databases.
  • Endpoint assets — laptops, mobile devices, peripherals.
  • Facility assets — the Manchester shared workspace, cloud-provider data centres (by reference).
  • Supplier and contract assets — customer contracts, supplier contracts, DPAs.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Information Security Manager: owns this Register; ensures the inventory is complete and current.
  • Asset owners: keep records of their assets accurate; flag changes promptly.
  • All personnel: identify and report newly created or acquired information assets.

6. How to Use This Register

  • Each asset is given a unique identifier (IAR-NNN).
  • Each asset has exactly one owner.
  • Classification (Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted) follows POL-004.
  • Linked controls reference the policies and SoA controls that protect the asset.
  • Review date is set per the Records Retention Schedule (POL-009) and in any case at least annually.
  • This document holds a representative sample (Section 7); the live register is maintained as an Excel workbook with the same column structure and is the authoritative source. The Excel version reconciles to this document at each formal review.

  • Material changes to the asset landscape are recorded promptly; a quarterly summary is reported to management review.
