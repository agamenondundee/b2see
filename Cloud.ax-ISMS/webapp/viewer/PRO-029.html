1. Purpose

This Procedure defines how Cloudax Ltd. manages capacity across its services to maintain availability and performance, and to support business continuity. It gives effect to A.8.6 Capacity management.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.8.6 Capacity management

2. Scope

This Procedure applies to all services and resources within the Cloudax ISMS scope where capacity affects availability or performance — platform compute, storage, networking, identity, third-party AI services, ticketing, and supporting SaaS.

3. Definitions

  • Capacity: the maximum amount of work or traffic a system can handle while maintaining required performance.
  • Headroom: the difference between current usage and capacity ceiling.
  • Auto-scaling: the dynamic adjustment of capacity in response to demand.

4. Principles

  • Cloud-native first — use provider-native auto-scaling, managed services, and elasticity wherever practicable.
  • Plan ahead — capacity is forecast against business growth and significant customer onboardings.
  • Monitor in real time — capacity is visible in dashboards; alerts fire before exhaustion.
  • Test under load — capacity claims are validated, not assumed.
  • Customer fairness — quotas and rate limits prevent any one customer from depriving others of capacity.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Chief Technology Officer: owns capacity strategy and architecture.
  • Engineering: implements monitoring, scaling, and operational response.
  • Information Security Manager: ensures capacity considerations are part of risk and continuity (POL-014, PLAN-BCP-001).
  • Customer-facing leads: provide forecast input from customer growth and engagement.

6. Capacity Domains

  • Compute: platform compute capacity in production cloud regions.
  • Storage: customer data, telemetry, logs, backups.
  • Network: ingress, egress, inter-service.
  • Third-party AI services: rate limits, request quotas, cost ceilings.
  • Identity: provider-imposed limits on operations per second, MFA enrolment scale.
  • Build / CI: pipeline concurrency, artefact storage.
  • Support and on-call: human capacity — ticket volume, on-call rotation depth.

7. Capacity Planning

7.1 Forecasting

  • Forecasts are produced at least quarterly and after material customer engagements.
  • Forecasts consider customer pipeline, expected growth, and seasonal patterns.
  • Forecasts feed contract negotiations with capacity-relevant suppliers (e.g. AI providers, cloud committed-use).

7.2 Headroom Targets

  • Headroom targets are set against typical and peak load (e.g. peak ≤ 70% of provisioned capacity for compute).
  • Targets are documented per service.
  • Where targets are persistently breached, capacity is increased or architecture changes are considered.

7.3 Customer Onboarding Capacity

  • Material new customer engagements include capacity assessment by Engineering.
  • Where new engagement would impair existing service, customer-facing leads stage the onboarding accordingly.

8. Monitoring and Alerting

  • Capacity metrics are exposed in dashboards available to Engineering and the on-call rota.
  • Alerts fire before capacity is exhausted, with target time-to-act before degradation.
  • Third-party rate-limit usage is monitored; backoff and queuing are designed.

9. Auto-Scaling and Elasticity

  • Customer-facing services use auto-scaling where supported.
  • Auto-scaling boundaries (min, max) are set with reference to expected demand and cost ceilings.
  • Stateful services are scaled with regard to data residency and replication constraints.

10. Capacity Reviews

  • A capacity review is held at least quarterly.
  • Reviews cover headroom against targets, forecast accuracy, recent incidents with capacity contribution, and required changes.
  • Material findings are escalated to management review.

11. Customer Quotas and Rate Limiting

  • Customer-facing endpoints implement rate limiting and quotas to prevent inadvertent or malicious resource exhaustion.
  • Quotas are documented in customer-facing documentation.
  • Customer requests for higher quotas follow a defined approval path.

12. Customer Communication on Capacity Issues

  • Capacity-related incidents follow PLAN-IRP-001 / PLAN-BCP-001 communication arrangements.
  • Capacity-related advisories (e.g. planned maintenance, expected peak periods) are communicated in advance through customer-facing channels.

13. Records

  • Forecasts, capacity reviews, and changes to capacity arrangements are recorded.
  • Records are retained per POL-009.

14. Compliance Monitoring

  • Capacity is reviewed at internal audit (PROG-AUDIT-001) where capacity could affect security or continuity.
  • Findings are recorded in REG-CAR-001.

15. Maintenance

This Procedure is reviewed at least annually and on material change to architecture or supplier base.

16. Acknowledgement

Engineering personnel and on-call team members are required to acknowledge this Procedure on commencement, on material change, and on request.
