1. Purpose

This Procedure operationalises the AI System Lifecycle Policy (AI-POL-005) by defining the practical steps for taking an AI system from concept to decommissioning at Cloudax.

ISO 27001:2022 references: ISO 42001 reference: A.6.1.3 Processes for responsible design and development of AI systems; A.6.2.2–A.6.2.8 (full lifecycle); A.6.1.2 Objectives for responsible development

2. Scope

This Procedure applies to every AI system within the AIMS scope (AIMS-SCOPE-001), including new AI features in the Cloudax platform, material changes to existing AI systems, and decommissioning of retired ones.

3. Definitions

Definitions are per AI-POL-005 §3.

4. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Chief Technology Officer: owns this Procedure; accountable for execution.
  • Feature owner / lead engineer: drives the lifecycle for the AI system in their domain.
  • AI Governance Lead: signs off stage gates (Section 12); ensures AISIA alignment.
  • Privacy Lead: signs off where personal data or data subject impact is material.
  • AI Builders: execute the procedural steps.

5. Process Overview

The lifecycle has six stages with three stage gates between them. Gates are decision points; passing a gate requires the documented evidence specified in Section 12.

6. Concept and Requirements Stage

  • Feature owner drafts the AI System Specification (template held in the AIMS repository).
  • AISIA is initiated under AI-PRO-002; for high-impact features the AISIA continues in parallel through later stages and is closed before deployment.
  • Data needs are identified; new datasets are scoped under AI-PRO-003.
  • Third-party AI dependencies are identified; new providers are onboarded under AI-PRO-021 (cross-reference to PRO-021) plus AI-POL-009.
  • AI risks are identified and added to the AI Risk Register (AI-REG-RISK-001) with AI Governance Lead awareness.
  • Concept review (Gate A) signs off proceeding to Design and Development.

7. Design and Development Stage

  • Architectural and design decisions are documented with rationale and alternatives considered.
  • Code is developed under POL-024 Secure Development Lifecycle Policy.
  • Monitoring is built in from the start — not retrofitted.
  • Reproducibility is preserved — training and evaluation can be re-run from documented inputs.
  • Documentation is maintained alongside the code (in the same repository where feasible).
  • Code review by a person other than the originator under POL-024.

8. Verification and Validation Stage

  • Verification confirms the AI system meets its specification — unit tests, integration tests, evaluation against held-out data.
  • Validation confirms the system performs as intended in operationally representative conditions — staged rollout, customer pilot, real-world signal monitoring.
  • Evaluation across population segments where bias is a risk.
  • Adversarial robustness, prompt injection resistance, and edge-case behaviour evaluated.
  • V&V evidence is documented and held with the AI system record.
  • V&V outputs feed AISIA refresh.
  • Pre-deployment review (Gate B) signs off proceeding to Deployment.

9. Deployment Stage

  • Deployment follows POL-025 Change Management plus AI-PRO-005 (AI-specific change management).
  • Pre-deployment checklist confirmed: AISIA current and signed off; V&V complete; monitoring operational; rollback path tested; customer-facing documentation updated.
  • Deployment is staged where feasible — limited rollout, monitoring, broader rollout.
  • Production release (Gate C) is signed off by the CTO.
  • Customer notification per the customer DPA where required.
  • AI System Inventory (AI-REG-INV-001) updated to reflect production status.

10. Operation and Monitoring Stage

  • Continuous monitoring of accuracy, drift, fairness indicators, anomaly detection, error rates.
  • Alerts routed to the on-call rota with documented response procedures.
  • Periodic monitoring summaries reported to AIMS management review.
  • Periodic evaluation refresh — at least annually.
  • AISIA refresh on the cadence set by AI-POL-004.
  • Material AI changes during operation re-enter at Stage 2 (Design and Development) and run through stage gates again.
  • Decommissioning decision (Gate D) is taken when the system is no longer needed, no longer fit for purpose, or superseded.

11. Decommissioning Stage

  • Customers are notified per their contract.
  • Data associated with the system is handled per POL-009 and AI-REG-DATA-001.
  • Models and weights are archived or deleted per PRO-017.
  • AI System Inventory (AI-REG-INV-001) is updated.
  • Lessons-learned review captures longitudinal observations.

13. Documentation Requirements per Stage

Each stage produces specific documentation, held with the AI system record in AI-REG-INV-001:

  • Concept stage: Specification draft; AISIA initiation record.
  • Design and Development stage: Architecture decisions; design notes; code (in the SCM); training and evaluation pipeline (Infrastructure-as-Code where feasible).
  • V&V stage: V&V plan and outputs; bias evaluation; adversarial robustness evaluation.
  • Deployment stage: Pre-deployment checklist; deployment plan; production release record.
  • Operation stage: Monitoring dashboards; periodic summaries; AISIA refreshes; incident records.
  • Decommissioning stage: Decommissioning plan; data disposition record; lessons-learned report.

14. Records

  • Lifecycle records are held with the AI system record in AI-REG-INV-001 and the AIMS document repository.
  • Records are retained per POL-009.

15. Compliance Monitoring

  • Compliance is monitored under AI-PROG-COMP-001 and audited under AI-PROG-AUDIT-001.
  • Auditors will typically sample AI systems and trace each through the stage gates.

16. Maintenance

This Procedure is reviewed at least annually and on material change to AI development practice.

17. Acknowledgement

AI Builders, the CTO, and the AI Governance Lead acknowledge this Procedure on commencement, on material change, and on request.
