1. Purpose

This Policy defines how Cloudax Ltd. captures, retains, and protects logs to support security, operational, audit, and forensic requirements.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.8.15 Logging

2. Scope

This Policy applies to all systems within the ISMS scope that generate logs, including endpoints, identity providers, cloud services, applications, container platforms, network components, and SaaS applications.

3. Definitions

  • Log: a record of events occurring in a system, application, or network.
  • SIEM: Security Information and Event Management — a system for collecting, normalising, and correlating logs.

4. Principles

  • Sufficient logging: enough is captured to detect, investigate, and respond to events.
  • Centralised: logs are forwarded to a central facility for retention and analysis.
  • Tamper-resistant: logs are protected from unauthorised modification or deletion.
  • Time-aligned: clocks are synchronised across systems.
  • Privacy-aware: logs do not contain unnecessary personal data; sensitive content is masked where appropriate.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Information Security Manager: owns this Policy.
  • Chief Technology Officer: ensures logs are produced and forwarded from in-scope systems.
  • System owners: ensure systems they own log appropriately.

6. What is Logged

Logs include, as appropriate to each system:

  • Authentication and authorisation events (success and failure).
  • Privileged actions and administrative events.
  • Access to customer data and Restricted information.
  • Security control events (alerts, blocks, malware detections).
  • Network events relevant to security (egress filtering, VPN connections, anomalies).
  • Application events (key business actions, errors, integrity-relevant operations).
  • Configuration changes and deployments.

7. Centralised Log Storage

  • Logs are forwarded to a centralised log store / SIEM.
  • Log forwarding is itself monitored; failures are alerted.

8. Log Retention

Default log retention targets, aligned to the Records Retention Schedule (POL-009):

  • Security-relevant logs: at least 12 months online; longer in archive where required by contract or law.
  • Audit and access logs covering customer data: at least 12 months.
  • Operational logs: as needed for operations (often 30–90 days).

9. Log Integrity and Tamper Protection

  • Logs in the centralised store are write-protected against modification by source-system personnel.
  • Object-lock or equivalent immutability is used where supported.
  • Access to logs is logged.

10. Time Synchronisation

  • In-scope systems synchronise time using approved NTP / authoritative sources.
  • Clock skew is monitored.

11. Logging of Personal Data and Privacy

  • Logs are designed to avoid unnecessary inclusion of personal data.
  • Where personal data is logged for legitimate purposes, retention and access are aligned with the lawful basis and data minimisation.
  • Sensitive fields (passwords, tokens, payment details, voice content) are excluded or masked (POL-016).

12. Application and Audit Logs

  • Applications produce logs sufficient to reconstruct user actions, key business events, and security-relevant operations.
  • Audit logs include user identity, action, target, time, and source where applicable.

13. Customer Data Access Logging

  • Access to customer data — including voice recordings and transcripts — is logged at sufficient detail to support investigation.
  • Logs include identity of the actor, action performed, customer/tenant context, and timestamp.

14. Access to Logs

  • Access to centralised logs is restricted on a least-privilege basis (POL-002).
  • Privacy considerations apply to log access; access is monitored and reviewed.

15. Compliance Monitoring

  • Coverage and integrity of logging are reviewed during internal audit.
  • Failure of log forwarding is treated as an information security event under PRO-027.

16. Review and Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually and on material change.

17. Acknowledgement

All personnel are required to acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
