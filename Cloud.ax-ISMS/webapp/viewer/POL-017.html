1. Purpose

This Policy defines how Cloudax Ltd. detects and prevents the unauthorised exfiltration, leakage, or disclosure of information by personnel, systems, or third parties.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.8.12 Data leakage prevention

2. Scope

This Policy applies to all channels through which Cloudax information may leave a controlled environment, including email, messaging, file sharing, cloud services, removable media, printing, screen capture, and AI services.

3. Definitions

  • DLP: data leakage prevention — the set of technologies, processes, and policies designed to prevent the unauthorised disclosure of information.
  • Exfiltration: the unauthorised transfer of information out of a controlled environment.

4. Principles

  • Defence in depth: DLP is one layer alongside classification, access control, encryption, and awareness.
  • Risk-based: controls are proportionate to data classification and channel risk.
  • Detect and prevent: where prevention is impractical, detection and rapid response are applied.
  • Minimise friction: legitimate business activity is not unduly hindered.
  • Transparent: personnel are informed that DLP is in operation and what it monitors.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Information Security Manager: owns this Policy and the DLP programme.
  • Chief Technology Officer: implements DLP controls in cloud and platform services.
  • Privacy Lead: advises on personal data handling within DLP.
  • Line managers: address behaviour-based DLP findings.

6. Channels Subject to DLP

Cloudax applies DLP controls to the following channels, in line with their risk:

  • Email and approved messaging platforms.
  • Cloud storage and file sharing.
  • Web egress (browser uploads, web forms, SaaS applications).
  • Endpoints (USB devices, printing, clipboard).
  • Source code repositories (secret detection, sensitive content).
  • AI services (data submitted to public AI services).

7. Detection Strategy and Coverage

  • Sensitive data types are defined (personal data, payment card data, credentials, source code, customer voice/transcript data).
  • Detection rules cover regex patterns, keywords, classifiers, and label-based detection where Microsoft Purview-style labels are applied.
  • Anomaly detection identifies unusual patterns of access, download, or transfer.

8. Response to DLP Events

  • DLP events are triaged by the Information Security Manager.
  • Genuine breaches are escalated to PLAN-IRP-001 Incident Response Plan.
  • Personal data breach assessment follows POL-010 Privacy Policy.
  • Behaviour issues are addressed through coaching or POL-011 Disciplinary Policy as appropriate.

9. False Positives and Tuning

  • DLP rules are tuned to reduce false positives while maintaining coverage.
  • Tuning changes are reviewed by the Information Security Manager.

10. Customer Data and Voice/Transcript Data

  • Customer data is treated as Restricted (POL-004) and is subject to dedicated DLP rules.
  • Bulk export of customer data is detected and alerted.
  • Customer data must not be sent through personal email, personal cloud, or personal messaging.

11. Generative AI and Public AI Services

  • Submission of customer personal data, source code, credentials, and Confidential or Restricted information to public AI services is detected and prevented in line with POL-003 §12.
  • Approved enterprise AI services with appropriate contractual safeguards are listed by the Information Security Manager.

12. Email and Messaging

  • Email and messaging platforms are configured with DLP rules covering personal data, credentials, and Restricted-class content.
  • External recipient warnings are configured for emails containing classified information.

13. Cloud Storage and Sharing

  • Sharing of files containing personal data or Restricted information outside the organisation is restricted and logged.
  • Public sharing links are restricted by default; exceptions are time-bound and logged.

14. Endpoint Controls

  • Endpoint DLP enforces removable media controls (POL-013) and printing restrictions for Restricted information.
  • Clipboard controls may be applied to virtual desktops and isolated browsing.

15. Privacy Considerations and Monitoring

Cloudax’s monitoring of communications and content for DLP purposes is operated under POL-020 Monitoring Policy and POL-019 Logging Policy. Monitoring is proportionate, transparently communicated, and conducted in compliance with the UK GDPR and DPA 2018.

16. Compliance Monitoring

  • DLP coverage and event volumes are reported to management review.
  • Effectiveness is reviewed at least annually.

17. Review and Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually and on material change.

18. Acknowledgement

All personnel are required to acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
