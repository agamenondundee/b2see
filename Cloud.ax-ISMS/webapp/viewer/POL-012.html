1. Purpose

This Remote Working Policy defines the requirements for working away from Cloudax-controlled premises, including home working, the Manchester shared workspace, customer sites, public spaces, and travel.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.6.7, A.7.7, A.7.8, A.7.9

2. Scope

This Policy applies to all personnel and authorised contractors performing work for Cloudax outside Cloudax-controlled premises, on any device, network, or location.

3. Definitions

  • Remote working: performing work outside the Cloudax shared workspace.
  • Hybrid working: a combination of remote and on-site working.

4. Principles

  • Remote working is supported but does not relax security requirements.
  • Information receives protection commensurate with its classification regardless of working location.
  • Personnel apply judgement based on context (e.g. public observability, network trust).
  • Cloudax provides the tools and training required for secure remote work.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Information Security Manager: owns this Policy.
  • Line managers: ensure team members can work remotely securely; address gaps.
  • All personnel: comply with this Policy in all working locations.

6. Authorisation to Work Remotely

  • Remote working is authorised in line with Cloudax’s working arrangements.
  • Personnel must not perform work from countries or environments not approved by Cloudax (e.g. extended international travel) without prior approval.

7. Home Working Environment

  • Work in a location where screens cannot be readily observed and calls cannot be overheard by household members or visitors.
  • Cloudax devices, paper records, and credentials are kept out of reach of household members.
  • Cloudax accounts must not be accessed by anyone other than the assigned user.
  • Home Wi-Fi must be secured with WPA2 or WPA3 and a unique strong password; the default router admin password must be changed.

8. Public Spaces and Travel

  • Avoid working on Confidential or Restricted information where observation by others is likely.
  • Untrusted public Wi-Fi must not be used without the approved corporate VPN; mobile hotspots are preferred.
  • Devices must not be left unattended in public places, vehicles, or hotel rooms; hotel safes are used where available.
  • Loss, theft, or suspected compromise must be reported immediately.

9. The Manchester Shared Workspace

Cloudax operates from a shared workspace in central Manchester. The multi-tenant nature of this environment requires specific controls:

  • Screens are positioned to avoid observation by other workspace tenants and visitors.
  • Privacy filters are used on devices regularly used in shared areas.
  • Calls involving Confidential or Restricted information are taken in private booths.
  • Headphones are used for any call involving sensitive matters.
  • Devices, access cards, notebooks, and removable media must not be left unattended in communal areas.
  • Tailgating is challenged; visitors are signed in and escorted.

10. Customer Sites

  • Personnel comply with the customer’s security requirements, in addition to Cloudax’s.
  • Photography, video, or audio recording at customer sites requires the customer’s explicit authorisation.
  • Customer information collected on site is protected and transferred to Cloudax through approved channels (POL-005).

11. Devices and Network Access

  • Cloudax-issued devices are the primary means of work.
  • Devices have full-disk encryption, endpoint protection, MDM, and automatic screen lock enabled.
  • Operating systems and applications are kept up to date.
  • BYOD is permitted only where expressly authorised and the device is enrolled in MDM (see POL-003).
  • Remote access to Cloudax internal services uses the corporate VPN or zero-trust access broker, with MFA.

12. Clear Desk and Clear Screen

  • Lock screens whenever leaving a device unattended (including for short periods).
  • Clear desks of papers, notebooks, and removable media at end of day or extended absence.
  • Dispose of confidential paper through secure shredding.
  • Wipe whiteboards after meetings.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.7.7 Clear desk and clear screen

13. Equipment Siting and Protection

  • Site equipment to minimise unnecessary access and observation.
  • Avoid placing devices in environments at risk of physical damage (heat, water, theft).
  • Use cable locks where appropriate in shared spaces.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.7.8 Equipment siting and protection

14. Security of Assets Off-Premises

  • Cloudax-issued devices and access credentials remain Cloudax property and must be safeguarded at all times.
  • Devices are not loaned to others, including family members.
  • On termination of employment or engagement, devices and credentials are returned promptly.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.7.9 Security of assets off-premises

15. Loss, Theft, and Damage

  • Loss, theft, or suspected compromise must be reported immediately, day or night, in line with PRO-027 Event Reporting Procedure and PLAN-IRP-001 Incident Response Plan.
  • Cloudax may remotely lock or wipe lost or stolen devices.

16. Review and Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually and on material change.

17. Acknowledgement

All personnel are required to acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
