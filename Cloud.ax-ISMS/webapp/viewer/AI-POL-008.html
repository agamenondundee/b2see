1. Purpose

This Policy governs the responsible use of AI systems — both AI tools used by Cloudax personnel for internal work and AI capabilities Cloudax provides to its customers. It implements ISO/IEC 42001:2023 Annex A.9.

ISO 27001:2022 references: ISO 42001 reference: A.9.2 Processes for responsible use of AI systems; A.9.3 Objectives for responsible use of AI system; A.9.4 Intended use of the AI system

2. Scope

This Policy applies to:

  • Use of AI tools by Cloudax personnel for internal work.
  • Use of AI capabilities Cloudax operates and provides to customers.
  • Customer use of the Cloudax platform, to the extent Cloudax can govern it through contract, design, and operation.

3. Definitions

  • Intended use: the use of an AI system for the purpose, in the conditions, and by the user populations specified in its design.
  • Out-of-intended-use: use that falls outside the intended use, whether by accident, exploration, or deliberate misuse.
  • Approved AI tool: a third-party AI tool that Cloudax has assessed and authorised for use by personnel.

4. Principles

  • AI is used for purposes consistent with Cloudax’s AI Policy and operational interests.
  • Human judgement is preserved — AI augments, it does not replace human accountability.
  • Confidentiality is maintained — Cloudax confidential and customer data are not exposed to AI tools without appropriate safeguards.
  • Outputs are reviewed — AI-generated outputs are checked before use, especially in customer-facing contexts.
  • Misuse is taken seriously — detection and response are operational, not theoretical.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • AI Governance Lead: owns this Policy; maintains the approved AI tools list; investigates concerns.
  • Chief Technology Officer: technical owner of platform AI use and misuse detection capabilities.
  • Line managers: ensure team members understand and follow this Policy; address day-to-day use questions.
  • All personnel: comply with this Policy in their use of AI tools.

6. Internal Use of AI Tools by Cloudax Personnel

  • Cloudax maintains a list of approved AI tools — generative AI assistants, coding assistants, document assistants, etc. — with the permitted use cases for each.
  • Personnel may use approved tools within the permitted use cases.
  • Use of unapproved AI tools for Cloudax work requires assessment and approval by the AI Governance Lead.
  • Use of AI tools for personal purposes outside Cloudax work is governed by personal arrangements, not by this Policy.

7. Generative AI in Cloudax Work

Generative AI (text, code, image, voice) used in Cloudax work follows additional requirements under POL-003 §12 and this Policy:

  • Personnel review generative AI output before using it in customer-facing materials, code merged to production, or formal communications.
  • Generative AI is not used to fabricate quotes, statistics, or attributions.
  • Cloudax confidential information and customer data are not submitted to generative AI tools that lack appropriate data handling commitments (no-training, no-retention, appropriate jurisdiction).
  • Generative AI use that materially shapes a Cloudax product or commercial decision is recorded sufficiently to enable later review.
  • Code assistants is reviewed by a human (the originator and a reviewer at minimum) before merge to protected branches.

8. Use of AI to Process Cloudax or Customer Data

  • Use of AI tools to process Cloudax confidential or customer data must comply with: POL-004 Information Classification, POL-005 Information Transfer, POL-010 Privacy, AI-POL-006 Data for AI Systems, and AI-POL-009 Third-Party AI Relationships.
  • Customer data is not submitted to AI tools other than those that form part of the Cloudax platform deployment for that customer (i.e. covered by the customer DPA).
  • Personnel handling customer data are trained to recognise when an AI tool would breach this constraint.

9. Out-of-Intended-Use of AI Systems Cloudax Operates

  • Each AI system Cloudax operates has documented intended use (cross-reference to AI-POL-005 §8).
  • Out-of-intended-use scenarios are identified and addressed through one or more of: technical guardrails (input filtering, output filtering, scope restriction), guidance to customers, contractual constraints, monitoring.
  • Where out-of-intended-use is identified post-deployment, response includes investigation, mitigation, and AISIA refresh under AI-POL-004.

10. Customer Use of the Cloudax Platform

  • Customer use is governed by the customer contract, including any acceptable use terms.
  • Cloudax’s acceptable use commitments include not knowingly facilitating: discriminatory use, harassment, deception of end-users about the AI nature of the interaction beyond legal limits, or other use materially inconsistent with the AI Policy principles.
  • Customer-facing acceptable use terms reflect these commitments and are written in plain language.
  • Material customer use concerns are escalated to the AI Governance Lead and may trigger contract enforcement action.

11. Misuse Detection and Response

  • Cloudax monitors platform behaviour for signs of misuse — e.g. attempts at prompt injection, content patterns inconsistent with the customer’s declared use case, abnormally high request volumes, or output requests inconsistent with intended use.
  • Detection signals are reviewed by the on-call rota and escalated to the AI Governance Lead and CTO as appropriate.
  • Misuse response may include rate limiting, customer engagement, contractual escalation, or platform-level block.
  • Material misuse incidents are recorded and may trigger AI-PLAN-IRP-001.

12. Objectives for Responsible Use

  • Every Cloudax person knows the approved AI tools and the permitted use cases.
  • Every AI system Cloudax operates has documented intended use that is technically and contractually enforced.
  • Out-of-intended-use detection signals exist and are reviewed.
  • Material misuse is detected, recorded, and responded to.

13. Records

  • Approved AI tools list is maintained by the AI Governance Lead.
  • Misuse incidents are recorded in AI-REG-INC-001.
  • Material out-of-intended-use findings are recorded against the AI system in AI-REG-INV-001 and may trigger AISIA refresh in AI-REG-AISIA-001.

14. Compliance Monitoring

  • Compliance is monitored under AI-PROG-COMP-001 and audited under AI-PROG-AUDIT-001.

15. Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually and on material change to AI tools available to personnel or to the Cloudax AI product.

16. Acknowledgement

All personnel acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
