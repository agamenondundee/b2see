1. Purpose

This Information Classification Policy defines how Cloudax Ltd. classifies and labels information so that it receives a level of protection proportionate to its sensitivity, value, and the consequence of unauthorised disclosure or modification.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked controls: A.5.12 Classification of information; A.5.13 Labelling of information

2. Scope

This Policy applies to all information created, received, held, or processed by Cloudax in any form (electronic, paper, verbal, or recorded), and to all personnel and third parties handling Cloudax information.

3. Definitions

  • Classification: the assignment of an information asset to a defined level based on its sensitivity and value.
  • Labelling: the application of an indicator (visual or metadata) of the classification level to an information asset.
  • Information owner: the person accountable for the protection of a particular information asset.

4. Principles

  • Every information asset has an owner and a classification.
  • Classification reflects the highest sensitivity of the information contained, considering legal, contractual, and business factors.
  • When in doubt, classify higher; reclassify down once confirmed.
  • Handling rules follow classification automatically; unclassified material defaults to Internal.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Information owners: classify the information they create or hold; review classification on change.
  • Information Security Manager: owns this Policy; provides guidance and dispute resolution.
  • All personnel: apply classifications and handling rules; treat unmarked material as Internal until confirmed.

6. Classification Scheme

Cloudax operates a four-level classification scheme:

  • Public — information approved for unrestricted disclosure (published marketing content, public website, press releases).
  • Internal — information intended for use within Cloudax and authorised third parties (internal announcements, general policies, non-sensitive project information).
  • Confidential — unauthorised disclosure would cause material harm (customer contracts, commercial terms, non-public roadmap, most customer data).
  • Restricted — unauthorised disclosure would cause serious harm; strictly limited distribution (credentials, encryption keys, production call recordings, sensitive personal data, security incident details).

7. Labelling of Information

  • Documents and presentations include the classification in the footer or document control block.
  • Emails containing Confidential or Restricted information include the classification in the subject line or first line.
  • Files containing Confidential or Restricted information are stored in approved repositories with appropriate access controls.
  • Where automated labelling tools are deployed (e.g. Microsoft Purview), labels are applied consistently.
  • Public information may be labelled or unlabelled at the discretion of the publisher.

8. Reclassification

Classification is reviewed when:

  • The information undergoes material change.
  • The information is published, made public, or formally shared with third parties.
  • Retention triggers require it (e.g. expiry of commercial sensitivity).
  • An incident or audit indicates the classification is incorrect.

9. Customer Data and Voice/Transcript Data

As a conversational AI provider, Cloudax processes customer voice recordings, transcripts, and derived metadata. These are classified as follows:

  • Customer voice recordings, transcripts, and call metadata: Restricted (where they may contain personal data) or Confidential (anonymised or aggregated).
  • Customer configuration, integrations, and prompt content: Confidential.
  • Customer-derived analytics and aggregated insights: Confidential, reclassified to Internal once aggregated and anonymised in accordance with the Data Anonymisation Procedure.
  • Test data must not be created or used in violation of POL-024 Secure Development Lifecycle Policy.

10. Handling Requirements by Classification

Each classification carries handling requirements covering storage, transmission, access, sharing, retention, and disposal. Detailed handling rules by classification are set out in PRO-008 Information Handling Procedure. Summary requirements include:

Public

  • No restrictions on storage or transmission.

Internal

  • Stored on Cloudax-approved systems; not shared externally without business reason.

Confidential

  • Stored on Cloudax-approved systems with access controls.
  • Transmitted using encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+).
  • Shared externally only under contractual confidentiality.
  • Not stored on personal devices or accounts.

Restricted

  • Stored only on Cloudax-approved systems with strict access controls and logging.
  • Encrypted at rest and in transit using approved cryptography (POL-023).
  • Shared externally only with explicit authorisation and contractual safeguards.
  • Subject to data leakage prevention controls (POL-017).
  • Pseudonymised or masked where possible (POL-016 Data Masking Policy).

11. Information Sharing and Transfer

Information sharing and transfer is governed by POL-005 Information Transfer Policy.

12. Storage, Retention, and Disposal

Retention and disposal are governed by POL-009 Records Retention Policy and the Records Retention Schedule. Information is securely deleted on expiry of retention periods (see POL-009 and PRO-017 Information Deletion Procedure).

13. Compliance Monitoring

Compliance with this Policy is monitored through internal audit (PROG-AUDIT-001), data leakage prevention controls (POL-017), and management review.

14. Review and Maintenance

This Policy is reviewed at least annually and on material change.

15. Acknowledgement

All personnel are required to acknowledge this Policy on commencement, on material change, and on request.
