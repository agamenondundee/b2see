1. Purpose

This Procedure defines how Cloudax Ltd. operates its backup systems day-to-day, performs restoration, and tests backup integrity. It implements POL-018 Backup Policy.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Linked control: A.8.13 Information backup

2. Scope

This Procedure applies to all backups in scope of POL-018, including the Cloudax platform, source code, configuration, and SaaS-resident data.

3. Definitions

  • Backup: a copy of information made for the purpose of restoration following loss or corruption.
  • Restoration: the process of recovering data from backup to a usable state.
  • RPO / RTO: recovery point and recovery time objectives — see PLAN-BCP-001 / PLAN-DRP-001.

4. Principles

  • Backups are run, monitored, and verified continuously.
  • Restoration is tested regularly — untested backups are unreliable.
  • Failures are alerted and acted upon promptly.
  • Restoration access is controlled — separation from production access mitigates ransomware risk.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Chief Technology Officer: owns backup operations.
  • Engineering / on-call: monitor backup jobs, respond to failures, perform restoration.
  • Information Security Manager: receives reports on backup status; reviews restoration test outcomes.

6. Backup Inventory

  • Engineering maintains a backup inventory listing each backup job: source system, frequency, retention, storage location, encryption status, owner, RPO/RTO target, and last successful run.
  • The inventory is reviewed quarterly.

7. Daily Operations

7.1 Monitoring Backup Success

  • Each backup job emits success / failure status to the centralised monitoring stack.
  • Success is required across all backup jobs each day; partial success is treated as failure of the missing component.

7.2 Handling Backup Failures

  • Backup failures generate alerts to the Engineering on-call channel.
  • Investigation begins within 1 business hour during business hours; emergency response out of hours where the failure indicates a wider outage.
  • Root cause is determined; backup is re-run; if re-run is not possible, the gap is recorded and addressed.
  • Sustained backup failure (more than 24 hours for a Tier 1 backup) is escalated to the Information Security Manager and the Chief Technology Officer.

7.3 On-Call Response

  • On-call playbooks document the response to common backup failure modes.
  • Playbooks include identification, mitigation, escalation, and reporting steps.

8. Restoration Operations

8.1 Restoration Request Path

  • Restoration may be requested by: a system owner (planned recovery, e.g. data corruption), the Incident Manager (during incident response), or a customer (under contract / data subject rights).
  • Requests are submitted to the Engineering on-call channel.

8.2 Authorisation for Restoration

  • Restoration affecting production environments requires CTO approval (or designate, during incident response).
  • Restoration involving customer data follows the customer DPA, with Privacy Lead engagement where customer consent is required.

8.3 Performing the Restoration

  • Restoration is performed using approved tooling under the direction of the on-call engineer.
  • Restoration is performed to a clean environment where the cause may be compromise.
  • Source-target traceability is preserved — which backup, which point in time, what was restored.

8.4 Post-Restoration Verification

  • Restored data is verified for integrity through automated checks and sample inspection.
  • Restoration is signed off by the requesting role.
  • Customer-facing restoration is verified and confirmed to the customer.

9. Restoration Testing

  • Restoration tests are performed on a defined cadence per backup type — at least annually for each material backup.
  • Tests verify both data integrity and the ability to meet RTO.
  • Test failures are addressed under REG-CAR-001.
  • Test outcomes are reported to management review.

10. Backup Inventory Reconciliation

  • Quarterly reconciliation confirms that all in-scope assets have appropriate backup arrangements.
  • New systems are added to the inventory at commissioning.
  • Decommissioned systems are removed.

11. Cross-Region and Off-Site Backups

  • Backups for Tier 1 systems span at least two regions where supported by the provider.
  • Cross-region replication is monitored; lag is alerted on.
  • Customer data residency requirements are honoured — backups remain within the regions agreed with the customer.

12. Encryption and Key Management

  • Backups are encrypted at rest and in transit using approved cryptography (POL-023).
  • Backup encryption keys are managed in approved key management services with logged access.
  • Where customer-managed keys apply, the key arrangements are documented and tested.

13. Records

  • Backup success / failure logs are retained per POL-009 (typically 12 months).
  • Restoration tests are recorded with date, scope, result, and actions arising.
  • Customer-facing restorations are recorded against the customer record.

14. Compliance Monitoring

  • Compliance with this Procedure is reviewed at internal audit.
  • Restoration test outcomes are reported under PROG-COMP-001 §8.4.

15. Maintenance

This Procedure is reviewed at least annually and on material change to backup architecture or supplier base.

16. Acknowledgement

Personnel involved in backup operations and on-call rota are required to acknowledge this Procedure on commencement, on material change, and on request.
