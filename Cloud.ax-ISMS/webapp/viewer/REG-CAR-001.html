1. Purpose

This Corrective Actions Register is the documented evidence of nonconformities and corrective actions required by Clauses 10.1 and 10.2 of ISO/IEC 27001:2022. It supports A.5.36 Compliance with policies, rules and standards and is the primary evidence of continual improvement.

ISO 27001:2022 references: Cl. 10.1 Continual improvement; Cl. 10.2 Nonconformity and corrective action; A.5.36 Compliance

2. Scope

This Register covers corrective actions arising from any source affecting the Cloudax ISMS — audits, monitoring, incidents, customer findings, supplier issues, management review, and operational observation.

3. Definitions

  • Nonconformity: non-fulfilment of a requirement.
  • Correction: immediate action to address a nonconformity — fixing the symptom.
  • Corrective action: action to eliminate the cause of a nonconformity to prevent recurrence — fixing the root cause.
  • Opportunity for improvement: a non-mandatory observation that could enhance the ISMS.

4. Sources of Corrective Actions

  • Internal audit (PROG-AUDIT-001).
  • External audit (certification body).
  • Compliance monitoring (PROG-COMP-001).
  • Information security incidents (PLAN-IRP-001).
  • Personal data breaches (PRO-024).
  • Customer assurance findings.
  • Supplier assurance and incident findings.
  • Management review (MGT-MR-LOG).
  • Operational events and weaknesses reported under PRO-027.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Information Security Manager: owns this Register; classifies nonconformities; tracks closure; reports to management review.
  • Action owners: implement correction and corrective action; record evidence.
  • Approvers: verify effectiveness before closure.

6. Lifecycle of a Corrective Action

  • Identify and record the nonconformity, including its source and the requirement breached.
  • Assess for severity (Section 7) and assign an owner.
  • Apply correction — immediate action to address the symptom.
  • Investigate root cause.
  • Decide on corrective action proportionate to risk.
  • Implement the corrective action; record evidence.
  • Verify effectiveness — has the cause been addressed and the nonconformity prevented from recurring?
  • Close, with sign-off by the approver.

8. How to Use This Register

  • Each entry has a unique identifier (CAR-NNN), a description, source, severity, owner, target closure date, status, evidence references, and closure notes.
  • This document holds a representative sample showing the structure; the live register is maintained as an Excel workbook with the same column structure.
  • Status values: Open, In progress, Pending verification, Closed, Reopened.

10. Reporting

  • Open and overdue actions are reported to management review at least every six months.
  • Major nonconformities are escalated to the Chief Executive Officer immediately.
  • Closure rate and time-to-close are tracked over time as a leading indicator of ISMS health.

11. Maintenance

  • This Register is reviewed continuously and updated whenever an action is raised, progresses, or is closed.
  • This document structure is reviewed at least annually.
